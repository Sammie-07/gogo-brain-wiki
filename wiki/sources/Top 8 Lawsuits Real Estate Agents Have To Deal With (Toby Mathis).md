# Top 8 Lawsuits Real Estate Agents Have To Deal With (Toby Mathis)

> Toby Mathis, Esq. on the most common legal threats facing real estate agents — and how to avoid them.

## Key Takeaways

- Real estate agents face increasing legal complexity — staying current on litigation trends is essential for survival.
- **The 8 lawsuit categories most relevant to agents:**
  1. **Breach of contract** — failing to fulfill obligations in a purchase or representation agreement
  2. **Negligence** — failure to disclose known defects or material facts
  3. **Breach of fiduciary duty** — putting personal interests above client's
  4. **Misrepresentation** — false or misleading statements about a property
  5. **Fair Housing violations** — any discriminatory practice in showing, selling, or renting
  6. **Commission disputes** — unclear agreements, dual agency conflicts
  7. **Failure to disclose** — material facts that would affect buyer's decision
  8. **Personal liability** — operating without proper entity protection means personal assets are exposed
- **Protection strategies**: accurate record-keeping, state/federal compliance, adequate E&O insurance, and entity formation (LLC) to separate personal from business assets.
- Toby's core message: prevention is cheaper than litigation — structure your business properly before the problem arises.

## Quotes

> "Real estate agents need to stay up-to-date on the latest litigation trends to avoid falling prey to lawsuits."
