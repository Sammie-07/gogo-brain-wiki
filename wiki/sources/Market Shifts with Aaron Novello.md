# Market Shifts with Aaron Novello

> GoGet'Em Community session with Aaron Novello on navigating real estate market shifts. Covers the "Season of Disappointment," adaptability as survival strategy, volume decline reality, and tactical adjustments for a flatter market.

## Key Takeaways

- **"Season of Disappointment":** Novello's frame for the current market cycle — not a crash, but a prolonged period of adjustment where most agents underperform expectations. Requires mindset recalibration.
- **2008 parallel:** Novello turned 17 transactions/year into 100/year during 2008 crisis by adapting while others froze. Market shifts = opportunity for those who move.
- **Money addiction vs. how you receive it:** Most agents are addicted to receiving money in one specific way (traditional buyer/seller deals). The shift forces diversification.
- **Darwin: most adaptable survive:** Direct application to real estate — not the strongest agents survive downturns, the most adaptable ones do.
- **Volume reality:** Volume dropped from ~7M transactions to ~3.8M. May stay flat for a decade. Planning around hope is a poor strategy.
- **"Hope is not a strategy":** Agents who wait for the market to return vs. agents who adapt to the market as it is.
- **Role-play daily:** Practice scripts and objections every day. Skill atrophies in a soft market if not maintained deliberately.
- **"Money is in the selling of the service":** Revenue comes from converting leads, not from the market improving.
- **Lead cash offer programs:** A tactical adjustment for the current market — cash offer programs attract more motivated sellers.
- **Contact rate math:** Need 30–40 contacts per hour (with modern tools) vs. 7–8 with standard manual dialing to remain competitive.

## Quotes

> "You're not addicted to money. You're addicted to receiving it a certain way. That's the problem."

> "The most adaptable species survive. Darwin was talking about Realtors and he didn't even know it."

> "Hope is not a strategy. Plan for the market as it is."
