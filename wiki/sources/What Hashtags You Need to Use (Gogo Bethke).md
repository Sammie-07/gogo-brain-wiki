# What Hashtags You Need to Use (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo walks through her exact five location-specific hashtag set for buyer/seller content. Ties directly to the 3 ZIP codes homework — your chosen areas become your hashtag set. Brand hashtag is non-negotiable on every post.

## Key Takeaways

- **Hashtags are search words.** Someone searching "#BrightonMichigan" will see every post tagged with it. Your location-specific posts must use the right hashtags to surface in those searches.
- **Instagram is currently at 5 max hashtags** (down from 30). Choose intentionally — you can only cover one or two of your three ZIP codes in a single post set.
- **Gogo's five hashtag set (Brighton, MI example):**
  1. `#BrightonMichigan` — city + state (necessary when city name is common; "Brighton" could return Brighton, England)
  2. `#BrightonMichiganHomesForSale` — what people actually search
  3. `#gogorealestate` — personal brand hashtag (always included, every post)
  4. `#LivingstonCounty` — county-level reach
  5. `#LivingstonCountyRealEstate` — county + intent keyword
- **Always include your brand hashtag.** Every post, every time. If someone hears about you anywhere and searches your brand name, they find you.
- **Avoid generic hashtags** like `#homeforsale` or `#realtor` — those are saturated by agents from Kentucky to Alaska. Specificity = smaller audience = higher placement.
- **Location-only rule.** Use location hashtags only when the post is actually tied to that location. The algorithm scans images — a palm tree in a post tagged "#BrightonMichigan" (no palm trees there) will suppress the post. Don't lie to the algorithm.
- **Save your 5 hashtags in the Brand Tracker / brand kit document** — so they're always one click away. Update the brand kit if you change markets or add a new ZIP.
- **Price = buyer quality principle reinforced here.** Gogo skipped Pinckney (population 9,000, homes ~$100K cheaper) and specialized in Brighton (8-minute drive, $3K+ more per deal). The ZIP you hashtag = the buyers you attract.

## Quotes

> "The more specific you are, the smaller is the audience, and the higher you're going to show up."

> "Your brand hashtag must be on every single post. Every post I've ever made is always tagged as Gogo Real Estate so they can find my post, they can find my account, they can follow me."

> "The algorithm, the AI, is smart enough. It will scan the image, it is going to see a palm tree, and it's going to see there are no palm trees in Brighton, Michigan, and it's not going to show your post because you're lying."
