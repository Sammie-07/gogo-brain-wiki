# How to Create a Database for Email Marketing (Gogopreneur)

> Gogopreneur episode on building and owning an email database. Covers platform ownership risk, 5-step database framework, tools, Gogo's personal metrics, and list hygiene.

## Key Takeaways

- **Own your database:** Social media followers are not yours — platforms can be deplatformed, algorithm-suppressed, or shut down. An email list is an asset you own outright.
- **5-step framework:**
  1. Define your audience
  2. Collect emails (KV Core, lead magnets, open houses, events)
  3. Tools to manage (Go High Level CRM recommended)
  4. Build trust through campaigns
  5. Grow and clean the list continuously
- **Go High Level:** Gogo's recommended CRM/email platform. Full pipeline + automation + email in one.
- **SendGrid:** Used for delivery analytics and tracking.
- **Gogo's metrics:** 43.68% average email open rate; 4.1M emails sent year-to-date.
- **Segmentation:** Tag every contact by lead source, interest, stage. Different segments get different messaging.
- **Collection touchpoints:** KV Core IDX, lead magnets, open houses, events, any in-person interaction.
- **Never give a direct link:** Always collect name + email before delivering any content. The info exchange is the gate.
- **Clean list every 6 months:** Remove unengaged contacts. A clean list improves deliverability and open rates.
- **"Give give give sell give give give sell":** Content cadence for email sequences — value-heavy with infrequent direct asks.

## Quotes

> "Your social media followers belong to the platform. Your email list belongs to you."

> "Give give give sell give give give sell. That's the rhythm."

> "Never give a direct link. Always collect the information first. Make the exchange."
