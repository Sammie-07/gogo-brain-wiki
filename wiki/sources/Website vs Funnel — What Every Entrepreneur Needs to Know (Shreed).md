# Website vs Funnel — What Every Entrepreneur Needs to Know (Shreed)

> GoGet'Em Community session with Shreed — the expert behind all of Gogo's websites. Covers the purpose and differences between websites and funnels, what makes each effective, and how to build a results-driven digital presence.

## Key Takeaways

- **Shreed:** The person who builds and maintains all of Gogo's websites. GoGet'Em Community training presenter on digital presence strategy.
- **Website vs. funnel distinction:**
  - **Website** — digital storefront/home base. Builds credibility, houses all information, serves multiple audiences and purposes.
  - **Funnel** — single-purpose conversion path. Guides a specific visitor toward one specific action (lead capture, purchase, booking).
- **Core features that convert:** Clear messaging, compelling calls to action, simple navigation, and a single focus per page.
- **When to use each:** Websites for brand establishment and multi-purpose presence. Funnels for specific campaigns, lead magnets, product launches.
- **Results-driven design:** A good website/funnel isn't just visually appealing — it's engineered to guide visitors toward taking action.
- **Strategic clarity:** Choose the right tool for the specific goal. Don't try to make a website do what a funnel should do and vice versa.

## Quotes

> "A website builds trust. A funnel drives action. You need both, and you need to know which one to use when."
