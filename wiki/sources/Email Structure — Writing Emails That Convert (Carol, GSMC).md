# Email Structure — Writing Emails That Convert (Carol, GSMC)

> Carol (Gogo's email copywriter/strategist) walks through email structure principles using two live email examples: a long storytelling email (REMLO) and a short direct email (Social Media Challenge). Part of GSMC 2026 copywriting month.

## Key Takeaways

- **Core principle:** Storytelling builds trust. "Facts tell, stories sell" — don't just state information, tell a story the reader can relate to. If they feel understood, they trust you.
- **Inbox = intimate space.** On social media, people scroll fast and fight the algorithm. In email, they're paying closer attention. Meet them at that level: be personal, be simple, be direct.
- **Serve, don't sell.** Your energy comes through in copy. Readers can feel when someone is desperate to sell. Shift to a service mindset — *"I found a solution to your problem and I want to show it to you"* — and the writing changes.

### Two Email Formats

| Format | Structure | CTAs | Best For |
|--------|-----------|------|----------|
| **Long storytelling** | Personal story → problem → solution → what's in it for them → CTA | 1 (at the end) | Trust-building, launches, high-investment products |
| **Short direct** | Authority statement → outcome → direct invitation | 3 (distributed) | Simple offers, events, challenge sign-ups, high-conversion lists |

### REMLO Email Breakdown (Long Format)
- **Opens with personal story:** Gogo realized she'd given a lender $9M worth of buyers and received nothing back.
- **Relatable pain:** Many agents have felt used by lenders.
- **Authority + numbers:** Grounds the story in real data ($9M given away).
- **Smooth product introduction:** REMLO is presented as the solution she found, not a pitch.
- **What's in it for them:** One point of contact, more deal control, additional income stream on existing transactions.
- **CTA:** Single red button at the end — "Click below to learn more and apply."

### Social Media Challenge Email Breakdown (Short Format)
- Opens with authority: *"I never cared about likes on social media. I only ever cared about what they turn into."*
- No storytelling — pure positioning.
- Three CTAs distributed throughout.
- Speaks directly to the reader's desired outcome (revenue, not likes).
- Shorter paragraphs; punchy sentences.

### Structural Principles
1. **Storytelling + authority** — tell a real story AND have numbers; both together create trust + intrigue
2. **Clearly state what's in it for them** — make it simple; don't bury the benefit
3. **Confident CTA** — don't apologize or hedge. Trust your offer. *"If you're apologizing for bothering them, you've already lost."*
4. **Simple subject lines** — statistics show simple subjects = more opens and clicks
5. **Multiple CTAs increase click rate** — Team Gogo's recent research shows distributing 3 CTAs outperforms 1 CTA in shorter emails

### Mindset
> "You want to show them, not just tell them, that you actually have a solution to their problem. When you're worried about serving people, your own mindset is going to change and it's going to reflect in your copy as well."

Different readers need different approaches — some connect with storytelling, others prefer direct and simple. Mix both formats. Test. Adapt.
