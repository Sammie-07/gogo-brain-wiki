# Getting A Hold Of Your Business

> Presentation on taking control of time, income, and business by knowing your hourly rate and delegating everything below it.

## Key Takeaways

- Core premise: getting a hold of your business is about working smarter, not harder
- Hourly rate formula: Annual GCI ÷ (hours/week × 52) = your hourly rate. Example: $250,000 ÷ 2,080 = $120.19/hr
- If you wouldn't pay someone $120/hr to set up a Canva post, stop doing it yourself
- Red Light / Green Light exercise: red = emails, DMing, social posting, setting appointments, graphics; green = coaching calls, deals, money-making activities
- "If it doesn't move the needle, it doesn't belong on your calendar"
- Listing side math: 10 hours average per listing → at $10k commission = $1,000/hr
- Buy side math: 25 hours average per buyer → at $10k commission = $400/hr
- Commission control: post what you want to attract. Post $1M listings, attract $1M buyers. Post $70k land, attract $70k buyers
- Build a buyer's agent team to remove yourself from buy-side transactions
- Gogo's current hourly rate: $3,086/hr — can no longer justify working with sellers either ($30,860 per 10-hour deal)
- DISC Assessment used for hiring (Tony Robbins DISC)
- VA team (14 VAs in 7 capacities): Executive Assistant, Social Media Assistant, Lead Designer, Tech/Numbers, Onboarding, FB Groups Manager, Podcast, Website Creator, Video Editor
- Training method: I do it → you watch → you do it, I watch → you do it alone
- All VAs cross-trained; each has a backup person for their tasks
- Create SOPs by recording yourself while teaching — never have to teach it again
- VA rule: "Google it. Ask a co-worker. Then ask me."
- Delegate it, automate it, or eliminate it

## Quotes

> "If you're still doing $10/hour tasks, you'll never reach $1,000/hour goals."

> "Delegate it, automate it, or eliminate it. That's your new mantra."
