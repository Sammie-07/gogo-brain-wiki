# Agent Attraction — The Top 10 Tips (Nobody Else Will Give You Straight)

> [[Gogo Bethke]]'s full #teamgogo Wednesday training walking through her actual Top 10 for agent attraction — the fundamentals that haven't changed because they don't need to — used to build [[entities/#teamgogo]] to 1,600+ agents across 46 states and 7 countries. Opens with a [[Jason Dupree|Jason]]/[[concepts/Real Estate Teams|REMLO]] mortgage-market update.

## Key Takeaways

**Gogo's Top 10 (in order):**

1. **Mindset is the whole game.** "If you believe you can or you believe you can't, you're right." Nothing else — international footprint, co-sponsorship, ease of the model — matters if you don't believe you deserve a downline. Do the multiplication math live: put *your* gut number on line 1, multiply by 2 down 7 generations (50 → 100 → 200 → 400 → 800 → 1,600 → 3,200 on level 7). Talk in "when," never "if" — *if* signals a 50/50 belief.
2. **Know your product cold** so you can handle any objection. Get on "a million" eXp calls. Be first to say "I don't know — but I know who does; let me call you back" (she calls upline [[Kurt Johnson|Kurt]], then [[Mark Zeda|Mark Z]], then Dan Beer). Use **[[eXp Realty|Mira]]** (eXp's AI in the agent dashboard) like a best friend that knows everything about eXp.
3. **Know your audience / ideal agent** — "I'm looking for the agents who are looking for me" ([[Brent Gove]]). Use the **[[concepts/Niche Finder|Niche Finder]] answered from the *agent-attraction* angle** (not production): it returns your IG bio, blog posts, SEO keywords, and a 3-month day-by-day plan for *attracting agents*. Gogo's ideal agent = **$4–7M production** (already knows paperwork, has "a taste for money," caps → guaranteed rev share, and at $7M can be taken to ICON). Start with "low-hanging fruit": people just like you or two steps behind.
4. **Know your competition** — interview competitors over coffee ("help me understand the RE/MAX cap & cut") to learn their math, then combat it with numbers, not feelings. Key combats: **The Real Brokerage has only 5 levels (no 6 & 7 — where most of your downline and biggest pay sit), you must stay an active producing agent to ever collect rev share, and its stock hovers near $1 (NASDAQ-delisting risk).** Co-sponsorship at Real *halves* your rev-share % per level; eXp's model doesn't. KW hides the 6% royalty on top of the $18K cap. "Free car wash" combat: your $200 first-closing stock ÷ $15 = 13 free car washes/year.
5. **How to attract:** post content and say **"comment OR DM 'partner'"** — serious agents will never publicly comment (their broker would see), so they DM. A [[ManyChat]] auto-message sends them the videos → booking calendar. **Block your calendar 2 weeks out even if you're free** (scarcity — "people want what they can't have"). Requiring prospects to watch the videos and wait pre-qualifies them → she claims ~100% close rate by the time they reach the call.
6. **The three-way call** (you + your agent + Gogo) at **gogobethke.com/partner** — for when you don't want to pitch/handle objections yourself. Etiquette: be on 3–5 min *early*; introduce and "fluff the feathers" of *your agent* at length; say only a *tiny* bit about Gogo (over-listing her accolades diminishes the agent); text Gogo the agent's stats ~1 hour before so she knows exactly what to pitch (leads/FastCap for new agents; cap-back/retirement/ICON for producers).
7. **"Why Not eXp"** group presentation — last Wednesday of every month (same page); ~2–2.5 hrs because you can't change a life in 5 minutes. Invite lukewarm prospects here first, then close on a three-way call.
8. **Bragging isn't bragging when it's true.** Show screenshots — the $16K stock deposit, the rev-share that covered the mortgage. Per the definition she reads live: bragging needs *others to be lesser* ("…and most people could never do what I did"); confidence is just genuine sharing ("I built a team of 1,650 agents"). People who call it bragging are usually not where you are.
9. **Reverse-engineer numbers from the MLS / a lender** and lead with money: "With my morning coffee I ran your production — you paid RE/MAX ~$37K last year; here you'd have $16K in your retirement account. I can make you $57K more doing the same thing." Max Powers story: messaged him cold — "I guarantee you're bleeding money" (3 states, 3 brokerages, 3 caps/fees/CRMs).
10. **Tools for attraction:** a dedicated *website* for the attraction leg (a hodge-podge of links signals you don't take it seriously); consistent video content; wins/screenshots; onboarding systems (Team Gogo's $25 onboarding with [[Rafael Mino|Raphael]]); and celebrate/gift your agents — "what gets celebrated gets duplicated" (top producer, top attractor, ICON of the month; join gifts).

**eXp / market facts surfaced:**
- eXp is **~89,000 agents**; only **~16% collect revenue share** (84% earn zero). Cap ~**$2.7M** production; **$16K** annual cap (vs. Real's ~$15K). **Level 6 & 7 pay the most** (~$2,000/agent on L7); **$4,000 capping bonus** to the sponsor in an agent's first year; **$1,400/yr** on level 1; **$200 stock** for your own first closing each year, **$400** for a sponsored agent's.
- **500 Legacy** program: at 500 agents across levels 1–7 you can unlock all levels (a retirement path) — but it comes with heavy obligations (attend/teach at eXp events, breakouts, cons). Gogo doesn't need it (she keeps her FLQAs).
- Alpha-sheet illustration: **Gene Frederick** (sponsored by **Elizabeth Riley**) has ~5,000 more agents than his sponsor — that gap sits on Gene's level 7 (Elizabeth's would-be level 8), worth ~**$10M/yr** on level 7 alone. Bren = #1 (86 FLA / 38 FLQA); Jen & Rob Flick = 105 agents / 34 qualify (~50% FLA→FLQA is typical). eXp is **debt-free with ~$126M cash** and acquired a company for cash (Gogo couldn't recall the name on the call).
- **REMLO / mortgage update ([[Jason Dupree]]):** national avg ~**6.6%**; oil down helped, then a Fed comment on inflation + AI fears erased a week of gains in 24 hrs; purchase market cooled. REMLO = paid **0.5%** of mortgage value; qualify with 6 buy-side transactions.
