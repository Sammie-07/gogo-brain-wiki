# My Money Lessons — 5-Day Live Event (Full Transcript)

> Full live transcript of Gogo's 5-day money training inside the GoGet'Em Community. Days 2 through 5. Day 1 was Barbara Schreihans (tax credits session — group received $580,000+ in tax credits). This source is significantly more detailed than the previously ingested "My Money Lessons" and "My Money Lessons — 5 Days Live (Presentation)" sources.

## Key Takeaways

**Origin Story**
- Gogo and Dwayne made ~$420,000-$430,000 in one year and still had $89,000 in credit card debt
- Earning money and knowing what to do with money are completely different skills
- Her entire research journey was triggered by that one year of high income and zero wealth accumulation

**Three Income Types (Expanded)**
- Active: you must show up; stops when you stop
- Recurring: built once, arrives every month (revenue share, GoGet'Em Community membership)
- Passive: earns without active labor (dividends, rental income, RealBricks returns)
- Goal: shift mix from active toward passive/recurring. Gogo is now ~90% passive or recurring.

**Savings vs. Inflation — The Losing Math**
- 2023 US inflation: 3.4%. Average bank savings account interest: 0.49%. Net loss: 2.9% per year on every saved dollar.
- $100,000 in standard savings = $97,100 in purchasing power one year later
- Have emergency fund (6 months expenses). Everything else must be invested.

**Hourly Rate**
- Annual income / 2,080 = your hourly rate
- Every task below your hourly rate is delegatable

**Investment Apps Gogo Uses**
- Robinhood: "fun money" account (~$10K), starbucks money. Loves the layout; does not trust for life savings.
- Coinbase: Bitcoin and crypto
- E*Trade and Charles Schwab: stocks only (no crypto)
- Spreads money across all because FDIC insurance only covers $250K per SSN per bank

**Stock Selection Strategy**
- Invest in companies she personally uses and believes will be here in 10+ years
- Her holdings: Visa, Delta, Ford, Verizon
- SPY (S&P 500 ETF): 27% return in one year, 18% year-to-date at time of recording

**52-Week High and Low Rule**
- Open Robinhood app, look up any stock, scroll to Stats section
- Find 52-week high and 52-week low
- Rule: buy when down, sell when up
- Example: stock at $40; 52-week high $43, low $30. At $40 there is still upside to $43 (~7.5%). Worth buying if you believe in the company.
- If at 52-week high with no reason to believe it goes higher: wait

**Buy to Hold, Not Day-Trade**
- Day trading creates taxable income. Buy-to-hold creates wealth.
- Gogo never sells to generate income — she holds to build wealth and avoid triggering a taxable event

**Dividend Stocks**
- Some stocks pay dividend yield (percentage of your holding, paid annually while you own the stock)
- Example: Verizon 6.46% dividend yield = $6.46/year on every $100 in Verizon stock
- SPY does not pay dividends but grows faster
- Gogo splits: dividend-paying stocks + growth stocks (like SPY)

**eXp Stock (EXPI)**
- eXp agents can opt into the stock options program
- Receive shares at a 5% discount to market price
- A literal coupon on stocks. Strongly recommend opting in.

**Bitcoin Preview (Before Mark Lack Session)**
- Three things Gogo did not know about Bitcoin:
  1. 24/7 trading (stock market is weekday hours only; Bitcoin never closes)
  2. Fractionalized ownership — Bitcoin was $57,566 at recording but you can buy $10 worth, $100 worth, any dollar amount
  3. Available to everyone — no credit check, no minimum, anyone 18+ with a phone
- Robinhood allows fractional stock investing in dollars (switch the buy mode from "shares" to "dollars" in the app — Gogo did not know this before the session)

**RealBricks — Fractionalized Real Estate**
- Platform: RealBricks (riobrix.com)
- Minimum investment: $10. Minimum age: 18.
- Buy shares in individual listed properties
- Sell shares on secondary market when you need liquidity (like selling a stock)
- Also: invest in RealBricks the company before it goes public at $8/share
- Why it matters: previously, real estate required $62,500+ down payment on a $250K property. Now your kids can invest at 18 with $10.

**Syndication Lesson — Gogo's Mistake**
- She invested in a syndication without doing her own due diligence — trusted a recommendation
- That syndication has had problems; others invested in a different syndication and have been receiving quarterly distributions and seeing appreciation
- Yasmine Rogers (Team Gogo) invested in the right one and is printing money
- Lesson: research general partners thoroughly; same diligence you would apply to buying a property
- Not against syndications in principle — research the GP before handing over $100,000

**HELOCs**
- HELOC = Home Equity Line of Credit; borrow against equity in a property you own
- Strategy: beachfront AirBnb costs $3K/month to run; the HELOC on the property was a cash-flow management tool; AirBnb income covers the costs
- Broader use: fund down payments on next properties, renovations, bridge cash-flow gaps; only pay interest when you draw from it

**Rental Property Types**
- LTR (long-term rental): 12-month lease; predictable income
- STR (short-term rental): Airbnb; higher income potential; more management
- MTR (mid-term rental): furnished, month-to-month; traveling nurses, traveling CEOs; Furnished Finder platform
- Flipping: Gogo explicitly does not flip. Creates taxable income (not wealth), requires active management (another job), sell = pay taxes. Her philosophy: reduce taxable income while others pay her mortgage.

**Cost Segregation — Detailed Numbers**
- Cost segregation study reclassifies building components into 5-, 7-, and 15-year depreciation schedules (vs. standard 27.5 or 39 years)
- On one of Gogo's properties: cost segregation saved $58,973.93 in taxes in one year
- Second property: saved $49,000+ in taxes
- Study cost: $3,000/property; net savings far exceeded cost
- Property also appreciated by $22,000 in one year while being paid for by renters
- Three simultaneous wins: tax savings + appreciation + tenant-paid mortgage
- Recommendation: do not self-report. Hire professional engineers. They can find land value and component classifications that you would miss (example: land value not in public records, had to call the county).
- Tools: kbkg.com has a free preliminary calculator
- Cost segregation training inside GoGet'Em Community from the company that did Gogo's studies

**Credit Card Debt Payoff**
- Make a spreadsheet of all credit cards and their APR
- Pay off highest APR first
- Use credit for everything; pay off on 1st and 15th of every month (twice/month = never accumulate APR)
- Before paying off: cancel all unused subscriptions and recurring bills (she was paying for Keeping Current Matters for months without logging in)

**Credit Cards Gogo Uses**
- Delta SkyMiles Amex: all personal expenses; miles on every dollar; first-class ticket to Romania paid with miles ($120 in taxes only)
- Chase Ink business card: one per company; Chase often provides 12–18 months 0% APR for new business
- Never use debit card at a business dinner — it signals you do not understand money

**Credit Score 630 to 817**
- Pay credit card bills on the 1st and 15th — approximately 3 days before the statement closing date
- Paying before statement closes means the card reports near-zero balance to credit bureaus
- High utilization = low score. Low utilization = high score.
- The trick is timing payment before statement date, not just before due date.

**Affiliate Income Tracking**
- Spreadsheet: affiliate company, unique link, code, what buyer receives, expected monthly payout
- VA logs into every affiliate dashboard monthly; records earned amounts
- Kristy cross-references against bank account — multiple affiliates have "accidentally" not paid

**Affiliate Vetting Standard**
- Never recommend something she does not personally use, regardless of commission offered
- Exception: credible source (like Tony Robbins mastermind recommendation) even if she does not personally qualify
- "I don't ever recommend any product or service that I don't personally use or trust the source."

**Board of Directors**
- Quarterly Zoom with: CPA (David Beck), tax attorney, business advisor, financial advisor, client success coordinator — all from Anderson Advisors
- All advisors hear everything simultaneously; no decisions made in silos

**Business Entity Structure**
- Every business + every investment property: own LLC
- All LLCs: held by holding company
- Holding company: inside a living trust
- "If you look up my name, I own nothing." Privacy + lawsuit protection + probate bypass.
