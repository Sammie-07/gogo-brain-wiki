# How To Make Your Personal Assets Invisible (Toby Mathis)

> Toby Mathis, Esq. on how to structure ownership so assets don't appear in your name — protecting them from lawsuits and creditors.

## Key Takeaways

- **Asset visibility principle**: if your name is on public records for an asset, it can be found by potential litigants and creditors.
- **What shows up in public asset searches**: real estate (your name on deed), cars, boats, RVs — anything titled with a government registry.
- **What doesn't show up**: assets held through properly structured entities (LLCs, trusts, corporations) — these appear owned by the entity, not you personally.
- **The two-step strategy**:
  1. Understand which assets appear in public records
  2. Move those assets into entities so your name disappears from the record
- **LLCs as privacy shields**: a properly structured LLC holds the asset; your name never appears in public records.
- **Wyoming LLCs**: often cited as best privacy vehicle — Wyoming doesn't require member disclosure.
- **Land trusts**: another tool for removing real property from your personal name while maintaining control.
- **Why it matters**: before a litigant files a lawsuit, their attorney does an asset search. If you appear to own nothing, the case isn't worth bringing.
