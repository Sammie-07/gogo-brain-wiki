# Build Your Funnels (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo walks through the anatomy of a funnel/landing page, the difference between funnels and websites, and three use cases (agent attraction, buyers, niche). Uses Melissa Albert's agent attraction funnel as a live example.

## Key Takeaways

**Funnel vs. Website:**
- **Funnel/Landing page:** Simple, boxy, straight to the point, cheaper and faster to build. Serves one single purpose. Each funnel = one specific visitor → one specific action.
- **Website:** More glamorous, dynamic elements, longer to build, more expensive. Multi-purpose. (Example: gogobethke.com)

**Funnel anatomy — the universal structure:**
- **Top (Header):** Name, photo, brokerage, logos, brief bio, CTA button (calendar link). If someone's already ready to act, the button is there immediately.
- **Middle (Meat):** Deeper content — videos about your model, your story, client testimonials. For skeptical visitors who aren't ready to click yet. Feed them everything they need to know before committing.
- **Bottom:** CTA button again (calendar link, contact form). Closes the loop.

**Three funnel types:**
1. **Agent Attraction funnel:** Introduces your brokerage model (eXp), videos explaining rev share/equity/value, calendar booking button to talk about partnering. Example: Melissa Albert's page (melissaalbert.org/partner).
2. **Buyer funnel:** Name/phone/photo at top → search homes link → client testimonials → video → schedule appointment.
3. **Niche/IDX funnel:** Dedicated to a single property type (lakefront, high-rise, land). Auto-updating IDX feed. Generates clients for that specific niche on autopilot.

**Agent attraction funnel walkthrough (Melissa Albert):**
- Header: company logo + personal logo + headshot + full name
- Brief bio explains the eXp model and her team's culture ("we grow together")
- First CTA: "I am interested" button → straight to her calendar
- Disclaimer (if applicable)
- eXp model explainer videos + Gogo's personal video (as sponsor)
- Second CTA: "Let's Talk eXp" → calendar booking
