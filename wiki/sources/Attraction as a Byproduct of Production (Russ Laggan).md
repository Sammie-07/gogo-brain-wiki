# Attraction as a Byproduct of Production — Russ Laggan on the "Would You Like Fries With That?" System

> A [[GoGet'Em Community]] weekly training where eXp VP of Training [[Russ Laggan]] teaches agents to build a revenue-share / attraction business as a natural byproduct of everyday production rather than as a separate, distracting second job.

## Key Takeaways
- **Vertical integration ("fries" framing):** House sales are "the hamburger," attraction/rev-share are "the fries" — "Would you like fries with that?" Attraction should be a *byproduct of production*, not a standalone business. Treating it as a separate "20% of your time" effort triggers a "house divided" effect (one office at 100% + a second at 20% nets ~80%, not 200%) and subconscious self-sabotage.
- **Two spheres of influence:** a primary SOI (people who know/like/trust you) and a secondary SOI of agents and vendors met through transactions.
- **7% SOI conversion benchmark:** in this market a 200-person sphere should yield ~14 sales/year.
- **820 contacts per listing:** every listing connects you to ~20 showing agents plus four "super connector" vendors — mortgage, title, insurance, inspector — each of whom knows ~200 agents.
- **The "How's the market?" elevator pitch** (the #1 question agents get): answer with *"I just did a market update last week — want me to send it to you? Where would you like me to send it?"* — captures contact info and delivers value instead of pitching.
- **Market update as the central magnet tool:** build it in **Gemini** (or ChatGPT) prompting for median sales price, total listings, total sold, and months of supply across last month vs. 1 year vs. 5 years ago. Detroit-metro example: median $286K (vs. $272K a year ago, $230K in 2021); ~14,000 listings vs. ~8,200 closed = sub-2-month supply (~1.8–2.3 months), a seller's market.
- **Always deliver as video, then distribute everywhere:** "see your piehole saying it" makes *you* the expert; post raw on social, push to **YouTube** ("#1 source people pull from"), then email/text the link.
- **Vendor-distribution play:** teach lazy mortgage/title partners to send your (non-branded) market update to every agent they know — they distribute it for you.
- **Buyer-agent feedback play:** promise listing agents structured showing feedback ("Friday morning before noon"), deliver it, then add the agent to your market-update list; pair with a "no nickel-and-dime under $1,000" buyer policy (handyman "Wally" fixes small items for ~$400) so agents love working with you.
- **Attraction = magnetism, not chasing:** "Park the boat, throw a flag up — the fish that swim to you are your people" vs. running fish over with a speedboat ("that's murder"). The "eagle" metaphor: be the place the eagle relocates to when its tree gets struck by lightning, not the one chopping the tree down. "I don't want to call people, I want people to call me."
- **Recruiting conversation recipe:** (A) Where are you now? (B) Where do you want to be? (C) How do we get there? — make it 100% about them ("What's working / not working / what do you love to do?") so positive feeling transfers to you.
- **Five stages of want:** (1) just need money, (2) money but fewer hours, (3) path to financial freedom, (4) build a legacy, (5) help others build a legacy — map the prospect to a stage to know what they want next.
- **New-agent caveat:** only attract brand-new agents if you (or your team) have a system to support them — "no different than getting a dog you can't keep alive"; hand them to the team's onboarding system, then "take them on the road."
- **Mortgage/REMLO context (Jason Dupree):** Team Gogo REMLOs earned over $21,000 in lender commission last month; company-wide REMLO commission over $107,000; program topped **450 agents** nationwide, lending in all 50 states; rates ~6.6%; top producers Melissa Alberts (#3 company-wide, ~$1M) and Morgan Tower (#5).
