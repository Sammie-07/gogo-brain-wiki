# The Art of Short-Term Rentals (Avery Carl)

> Gogopreneur episode with Avery Carl — CEO of The Short-Term Shop, eXp #1 team 3 years running, 250 doors across 20 markets. Framework for building an STR portfolio in mature vacation markets.

## Key Takeaways

- **Origin:** Left a $37K/year job. Built from zero to $40M in assets in 7 years through STR investing.
- **eXp connection:** Leads the #1 team at eXp 3 years running; joined eXp specifically for MLS access.
- **250 doors, 20 markets:** Highly diversified STR portfolio geographically — reduces single-market risk.
- **The Short-Term Shop:** Her brokerage/brand focused exclusively on STR investment properties.
- **Books:**
  - *Short-Term Rental, Long-Term Wealth* (BiggerPockets) — primary framework book
  - *Smarter Short-Term Rentals* (February 2025) — follow-up/updated strategies
- **Only invest in mature vacation markets:** Avoid emerging markets with unproven STR demand. Stick to destinations with established vacation rental history.
- **Biggest mistake:** Buying for cash flow only in a non-appreciating market. Cash flow alone is a trap — appreciation is where real wealth builds.
- **Reinvest all real estate income into more real estate:** The compounding effect of keeping RE proceeds inside RE is the core wealth-building engine.
- **3-times rule → create SOP:** When you do something three times in your business, document it as a standard operating procedure. Systematize before you scale.
- **Book recommendation:** *Unreasonable Hospitality* — hospitality vs. service distinction. Service is delivering what's expected; hospitality is making people feel something.
- **$5K → $40M in 7 years:** Starting capital was minimal; leverage and reinvestment did the work.

## Quotes

> "The biggest mistake I see is buying for cash flow only in a market that doesn't appreciate. You end up with a job, not wealth."

> "Reinvest every dollar your real estate makes back into real estate. That's the whole strategy."

> "Service is giving someone what they ask for. Hospitality is making them feel something they didn't expect."
