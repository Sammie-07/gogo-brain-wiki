# Mastering Your Numbers: Leads, Time & Revenue

> Week 3 of the paid 4-week Business Mastery webinar series. Covers Gogo's complete tracker suite (lead, revenue share, money, affiliate, email, GoGet'Em), business entity structure, tax strategy, the board of directors model, and the origin story of her tracking obsession.

## Key Takeaways

- **Lead tracker fields:** Source, status (A/B/C by timeline), estimated commission. Source breakdown shows year-end ROI per channel. All leads into kvCORE regardless of how they arrived.
- **Revenue share tracker:** Tracks month-over-month agent count and rev share growth. eXp closes historical data after month end — must capture in real time. Per-agent value = total rev share / total agents.
- **Money tracker:** One Google Sheets workbook, one tab per company (GRET, GBC, Gogopreneur, Dugo). Income green, expenses red. Only Gogo and Kristy see it.
- **QuickBooks as source of truth:** All bank accounts and cards connected. Kristy categorizes transactions as they arrive, exports monthly to master Google Sheet.
- **Credit card strategy:** Use credit for every expense; pay down on the 1st and 15th (no APR accumulates). Earn miles on Delta SkyMiles Amex (personal) and points on Chase business cards (one per company). Paying with a debit card at a business dinner signals you don't understand money.
- **S-Corp tax structure:** Draw a W2 salary. Separates payroll taxes from corporate taxes. At 37% bracket: every $1,000 not spent as a write-off costs $370 to Uncle Sam.
- **Car tax story:** CPA told Gogo to buy a new car or pay $56K more in taxes. She bought a $169K car and saved $50K+ in taxes.
- **Tax caution:** Writing off too much makes income look low on paper, which prevents qualifying for mortgages or investment property loans. Coordinate with CPA before buying real estate.
- **Board of directors model:** Quarterly Zoom call with CPA (David Beck), tax attorney, business advisor, financial advisor, and client success coordinator — all from Anderson Advisors. Everyone hears everything; no decisions in a silo.
- **Business structure:** LLC → Holding Company → Living Trust. Each business and investment property in its own LLC. "If you look up my name, I own nothing." Living trust bypasses probate.
- **Affiliate tracker:** Spreadsheet of every affiliate agreement, unique link, code, buyer benefit, and expected monthly payout. VAs log in monthly; Kristy cross-checks bank account. Several affiliates "accidentally" miss payments; the tracker catches it.
- **Email report tracker:** Weekly metrics (contacts, open rate, click-through, unsubscribes). Weekly granularity to isolate which promotion drove which result. 2023: 44.48% open rate on 5.2M+ emails.
- **GoGet'Em webinars tracker:** Total members, new members, new trials, income, cancellations, cancellation rate, website visitors, checkout clicks, actual purchases (closing rate math).
- **RECU USA:** Nearly 500,000-member Facebook group owned by Gogo. Managed by a dedicated VA and volunteer administrators who teach weekly in exchange for one ad slot per week.
- **Financial literacy origin story:** Gogo made $420,000 in one year and had $89,000 in credit card debt. That shock drove her to build the tracking systems she now teaches.
