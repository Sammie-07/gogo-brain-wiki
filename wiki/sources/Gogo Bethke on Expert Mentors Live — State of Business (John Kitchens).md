# Gogo Bethke on Expert Mentors Live — State of Business (John Kitchens)

> Gogo's 6th annual New Year appearance on Expert Mentors Live with John Kitchens. Topic shifted from "state of social media" to "state of business." Gogo shares how she achieved financial, time, and location freedom — and what the machine that makes it possible actually looks like.

## Key Takeaways

- **State of business, not social media:** After six years of "state of social media" conversations with John Kitchens, Gogo changed the topic. Social media remains important but is now a means — freedom is the end.
- **Three freedoms achieved:** Financial freedom (income no longer tied to any single transaction), time freedom (works Monday–Wednesday only, done by 4pm), and location freedom (runs the business from anywhere).
- **Monday–Wednesday work schedule:** By design, Gogo works three days a week. Thursday–Friday–Saturday–Sunday are fully off. "Extended weekends for the rest of my life." This was built deliberately, not stumbled into.
- **14 assistants:** "I have an assistant for everything — from design to emails to websites to numbers trackers." Each VA covers a specific function. This is the machine behind the schedule.
- **Hire before you can afford it:** "I hired Kristy before I could actually afford one." The investment in the assistant creates the capacity to earn more — not the reverse. "Make the hire right before you can afford to make the hire."
- **Hire out your weaknesses first:** Start with what you're worst at (organized chaos — filing, admin, paperwork) so the high-skill work isn't buried in low-skill friction.
- **COVID and the global workforce:** COVID unlocked talent globally — skilled people who previously worked in offices are now virtual and available at a fraction of the in-person cost. This shift made the VA model scalable for solo agents.
- **VA starting point:** "Skip Starbucks today and you'll afford your first VA." $5/hour for a virtual assistant. The first hire creates capacity; capacity creates income.
- **Delegation compounds:** Kristy started by taking admin tasks. Eventually she had enough bandwidth to build businesses — set up GoHighLevel, create courses, manage the entire tech stack. One hire, compounding for 14 years.
