# How to Save Trending Audios and Use Them (Gogo Bethke)

> Full walkthrough of Gogo's audio workflow for Instagram Reels: finding trending sounds, saving them from other people's Reels, adding saved audio to your own videos, and balancing volume between your voice and background music. Part of GoGo Social Media Challenge 2026.

## Key Takeaways

- **Gogo's audio philosophy:** She doesn't know band names or song titles — she saves sounds she hears while watching Reels and pulls from that saved library when creating
- **Save audio from any Reel:** Tap the audio name (under the creator's username, bottom left) → tap the save/bookmark icon → audio stored in your saved sounds
- **Use audio immediately:** Tap "Use Audio" on the sound page → select your video → audio auto-attaches
- **Add saved audio to a new Reel:** + → Reels → choose video → next → music icon (3rd bottom-left icon, after ABC and emoji) → "Saved" tab → select
- **Explore trending sounds:** Same music menu → "Trending" tab → browse and listen → save any you like for later without using them now
- **Browse trending via other people's Reels:** While watching, tap the spinning music disc icon (bottom right, red) → save icon → done. Continue watching.
- **Volume balancing (voice + music):** After adding audio to a Reel → swipe up → tap video clip (turns yellow) → Volume → adjust "Original Clip" (your voice) vs. "Added Audio" (music) independently
  - Background music: turn music down; keep your voice audible
  - Music only: turn original clip volume to zero (your mouth moves, no voice heard)
- **Tool preference:** Gogo edits entirely inside Instagram — no CapCut, no external apps. "I'm in it all day every day. I just learned it." CapCut is a valid alternative (create → download → upload to Instagram)
- **Summary of audio tabs in Reels music menu:** For You · Trending · Original Audio · Saved · Royalty Free

## Quotes

> "I do not know a single band and a single song they ever sing. I just can't remember that kind of stuff. There's no space in my head for that kind of…"

> "I watch other people's content. When I like the video, I just save it. Sometime down the road when I need new sound, I'll just use one of the saved sounds."

> "From now on, when you like it, you go to the bottom right, you click that music, save. That's it. Go back watching videos. Save. Watch videos. Save. Watch videos."
