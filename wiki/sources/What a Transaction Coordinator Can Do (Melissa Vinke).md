# What a Transaction Coordinator Can Do (Melissa Vinke)

> GoGet'Em Community session with Melissa Vinke on the role and value of a Transaction Coordinator (TC). Covers Gogo's 10-transaction rule, TC responsibilities, and how to integrate a TC into your business.

## Key Takeaways

- **TC role:** The TC is the front line of the transaction on the agent's behalf — handles all paperwork, communication, and deadline management so the agent stays in relationship mode.
- **Ideal client:** Agents doing 3–5 deals per month who are ready to delegate the paper.
- **Gogo's 10-transaction rule:** First 10 transactions (5 each side — 5 as buyer's agent, 5 as listing agent) agents must handle themselves. After 10 → TC is mandatory. Learn the process before delegating it.
- **No intake form:** To work with Melissa, simply text/email/call with the property address. Minimal friction to start.
- **TC handles:** Inspections, deposits, wiring, intro emails to all parties, deadline tracking, coordination with title and lender.
- **One dedicated TC per agent:** Relationship-based model. Agent has one consistent TC who knows their style, clients, and preferences.
- **All 50 states:** TC services available nationwide.
- **Introduction language Gogo uses:** "This is Melissa who manages my transactions so I don't push paper." Positions the TC as a professional asset, not overhead.

## Quotes

> "A TC is the front line of your transaction. I represent you so you can stay in front of clients."

> "After your first ten transactions — five each side — you should never process your own paper again."

> "Text me the address. That's it. No intake form, no onboarding call. Just the address."
