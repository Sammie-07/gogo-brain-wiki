# Who Is Your Ideal Client and How To Find Them (Gogo Bethke)

> GoGet'Em Weekly Training. Gogo Bethke teaching. Rescheduled twice (Hurricane Helene, then Asheville relief trip with Beaudoin). Topic: niche specialization and systematic ideal client identification — 4 core questions framework.

## Key Takeaways

- **Specialize first, then find them.** You cannot identify your ideal client without first choosing a niche. The more specific, the more referable and memorable you become — and the better AI can help you.
- **Lakefront case study.** Used throughout as the primary example: pick lakefront homes as a specialty, then build every downstream action around that niche.
- **6-step framework:**
  1. **Choose a specialization** — pick one niche and own it completely
  2. **Create niche content** — for lakefront: home prices, water temps, bacteria alerts, lake events, anything community-relevant
  3. **Identify who your people are** — for lakefront: fishermen, boaters, water sports enthusiasts, fishing tournament participants
  4. **Find where they are** — Facebook groups, fishing tournaments, boating clubs, local communities
  5. **Get in front of them** — join groups, contact admins, offer to sponsor, ask to speak, bring Gatorades (or Coors Light) to events
  6. **Use ChatGPT to amplify** — feed your niche and location; get a complete content plan and list of community entry points
- **The 4 core questions.** Where are they? Who are they? How can I find them? How can I talk to them?
- **Community involvement is authentic.** Real estate is still a relationship business — getting in the same physical and digital rooms as your people is the most durable lead gen strategy.
- **ChatGPT specificity principle.** "The more specific you are, the better ChatGPT can help you." Give it your town, your niche, your goal — get back a full action plan.
- **Real estate is still relationships.** Gogo: "You just need to get in front of people."
- **Personal context.** Rescheduled twice — once for hurricane, once to volunteer in Asheville post-hurricane. Gogo's early coaching memory: $1,600/month coaching felt like a "mortgage payment," would "dry heave" trying to make the payment.
