# Tax Structure and Legacy Planning for Real Estate Professionals (Toby Mathis)

> Tax attorney Toby Mathis brings 25 years of experience to the GoGet'Em community. He owns 300+ properties, runs Tax Tuesday (free bi-weekly Q&A on YouTube), and guides agents through the three-tier evolution from sole proprietor → S-corp → layered entities. The second half covers trusts, dynasty trusts, and leaving a conditional legacy.

## Key Takeaways

**Business Structure:**
- Sole proprietor (Schedule C) = worst structure; highest taxes, highest audit risk, zero liability protection; "the vast majority of real estate agents structure themselves as sole proprietors — that's the worst thing you could do"
- LLC → S-corp election saves ~10-15% of net income on self-employment tax
- At $60K income: S-corp saves $4K+ vs sole prop. Gap grows dramatically with income
- No 7-figure agent uses sole proprietor — zero
- S-corp accountable plan: employer reimburses personal car's business mileage — not reportable income; use MileIQ app (swipe left personal, right for business)
- 121 exclusion can be stacked with 1031 exchange to eliminate ALL capital gains on any property regardless of gain size
- Layering strategy: S-corp → 401K → rental property LLCs → each income stream isolated → more tax appetite
- Team member Kelly available for free initial analysis (no cost consultation)

**Trusts vs Wills:**
- Will = probate required — court proceeding, public record, can be contested by anyone claiming interest (Prince's estate still in probate 6+ years later)
- Trust = no probate, private, self-executing, disinherits anyone who contests, works during lifetime and beyond
- HEMS: Health, Education, Maintenance, Support — standard trust distribution clause
- Dynasty trust: includes healthcare POA, financial POA, end-of-life decision-making, burial instructions — "a few thousand dollars"
- Legacy conditions: "You can only receive funds if you visited Romania for 4 weeks that year" — pass on values, not just money
- "70% of families lose their wealth by the second generation; first generation makes it, second watches it, third blows it"
