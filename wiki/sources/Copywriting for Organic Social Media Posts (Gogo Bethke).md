# Copywriting for Organic Social Media Posts (Gogo Bethke)

> Fourth video in the GSMC 2026 April copywriting series. Gogo walks through four caption formats for organic posts (no ads), with live ChatGPT examples, her personal writing tendencies, and the "me vs. you language" ratio.

## Key Takeaways

- **Copywriting for organic posts = the description/caption** under any post type (reel, photo, carousel — the format doesn't matter, the words do)
- **Four caption formats:**

### Format 1: Hook → Lesson → CTA
The most versatile structure. Works for any post type.
- **Hook** — grab attention, speak to a pain or curiosity instantly
- **Lesson** — the value; can be short bullet points or a full story. Gogo prefers long, wordy, full stories — goes against conventional advice but is authentic to her
- **CTA** — one clear next step
- *Example:* Hook: *"Struggling to find your ideal client on social media? You are not alone."* → Lesson (define micro-niches, narrow your focus) → CTA: *"Join the GoGet'Em Community. Click the link."*
- **ChatGPT tip:** Tell it the format, who you're talking to, and what you want them to do. If ChatGPT knows your voice, it generates without needing more instructions.

### Format 2: Problem → Call Out → Authority
- **Problem** — name it plainly: *"Too many agents are overwhelmed by social media. Does this sound familiar?"*
- **Call Out** — validate their effort while diagnosing the real issue: *"You are doing the work, posting constantly, but not getting leads. It's not your content — it's your audience targeting."*
- **Authority** — prove you've been there and have the solution: *"I built my real estate empire using this exact strategy. In the GoGet'Em Community we'll show you how."*

### Format 3: Contrarian Opinion
Opens by going against popular belief — creates pattern interrupt.
- Hook challenges conventional wisdom: *"Here is something no one tells you about social media marketing in real estate. It's not about more posts. It's about better posts."*
- Lesson reinforces the counter-argument
- CTA brings it home: *"Don't just post to post. Post with purpose."*

### Format 4: Storytelling Post
Gogo's most natural format — and the most powerful for trust-building.
- **No required hook/CTA structure.** Just tell the actual story, from start to finish.
- Lesson at the end (optional): *"Here's what I want you to get from this."*
- Does NOT need to sell anything. Its purpose is to let people get to know you.
- Example: Gogo's first million-dollar listing story — told honestly, raw: *"Have you ever had a million-dollar listing? No. Have you ever sold in our gated community? No. Why should we give you the listing? Because you would change my life."* Sold it in 3.5 hours while still in coming-soon status.
- Sources for stories: family moments, childhood, traditions, culture/immigrant background, current life events, health, anything real
- *"Stories when you share them don't necessarily need a call to action. It's just you truly sharing a story to allow people to get to know you."*

### Me Language vs. "You" Language — The 50/50 Rule

**"You" language** = the hardest thing Gogo does. Naturally, she writes in "I/me" because she built trust by proving results first. But:

> "In the end of the day, you could care less about me. You don't care about my results. You care about your results."

- **"I/me" posts** — achievements, proof, hustle; help people trust you
- **"You" posts** — speak to the reader's outcome, not Gogo's; *"You are probably making one mistake on social media that's stopping you from attracting the right clients."*
- **Gogo's fix:** Write it in "I/me" naturally → paste into ChatGPT → *"Make this in the 'you' language but still sound like me."* It flips it.
- **Recommended ratio: 50% me, 50% you.** People need to see your achievements to trust you, AND they need to feel spoken to directly.

## Quotes

> "Many people will tell you don't be too wordy. But that's just who I am. I like a good story and I am wordy."

> "Just say the truth. Even if you're brand new, you've never sold a million-dollar listing, just say the truth. Talk from the heart because people want to help you."

> "Don't just post to post. Post with purpose."

> "I cannot do 'you' language naturally. It does not come to me naturally. In my head it's all about me."

> "50% of the time it's me and 50% of the time it's the 'you' language. That should be very good for you to sell whatever you are selling."
