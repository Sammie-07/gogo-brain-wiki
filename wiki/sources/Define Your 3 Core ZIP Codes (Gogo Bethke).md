# Define Your 3 Core ZIP Codes (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo assigns homework: pick exactly 3 ZIP codes (or equivalent areas/counties) to focus all 2026 real estate content and marketing on. The area you post about determines the buyer you attract — price point follows content.

## Key Takeaways

- **Three ZIP codes, intentionally chosen.** Not your whole market — three specific areas where you want to consistently generate business.
- **Price point = content subject.** Create content on a $70K ZIP → attract $70K buyers. Create content on a $750K ZIP → attract $750K buyers. You choose your income level through what you post about.
- **Willingness to drive = income multiplier.** Oakland County homes were $150K more expensive than Livingston County — only 30-45 minutes away. A slightly longer drive can mean $5K+ more per deal. Be willing to expand your territory for a materially better commission.
- **ZIP codes flow into hashtags and geo-tags.** Once chosen, these ZIP codes/areas become: your post location tags (Instagram geo-tag), your content hashtags (city/neighborhood names), and the backdrop for your neighborhood market report videos.
- **For rural/low-density markets:** You may need to cover an entire county rather than three ZIPs if individual areas lack enough inventory. Know your market density and adjust the scope accordingly.
- **Homework:** Write down the three ZIP codes (or areas/counties) you want to be present in for 2026. Be intentional. Do it now.

## Quotes

> "If you create content on a zip code where an average home is $70,000, you're going to find $70,000 buyers. If you create content on a zip code where the average home is $750,000, you're going to find $750,000 buyers."

> "Are you willing to drive a little bit that way just so you can make 10 times more per deal?"
