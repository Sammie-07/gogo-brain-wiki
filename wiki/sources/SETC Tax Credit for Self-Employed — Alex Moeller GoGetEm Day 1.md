## Key Takeaways

- **SETC = Self-Employed Tax Credit.** Pandemic-era program for anyone self-employed during 2020 and/or 2021. Non-taxable, non-repayable government credit — not a loan, not a grant requiring business use.
- **Qualification criteria (3 factors):** (1) Self-employed during the pandemic — 1099 income, Schedule C, sole member LLC, partnership on a federal return; (2) Impacted by COVID — self-attestation, no doctor's note required; (3) Paid taxes on net income in 2020 or 2021.
- **Maximum credit:** $32,200 per individual; $64,400 married filing jointly (each spouse claims separately).
- **Taxable year that matters:** 2020 and 2021 only — current income, 2022, 2023, 2024 are irrelevant.
- **The "self-attestation" rule:** The government cannot demand proof of impact. You attest that you were affected; no documentation of illness or school closure required.
- **$70 billion fund** set aside by Congress; less than 5% of eligible Americans have claimed it; remainder reverts to the government. Deadline approximately May of the following year (exact deadline not guaranteed).
- **W-2 income does not disqualify.** The credit is based only on 1099/Schedule C net income. Having a W-2 job alongside 1099 work is fine.
- **Net taxable income is what matters.** High write-offs reduce the credit — the credit is a percentage of taxes actually paid. Someone who made $100K but wrote off $98K receives little.
- **Process:** Pre-qualify in ~7 minutes at the Prosperity Tax Group portal (bit.ly/sctcgogo). AI software calculates the amount instantly. Full filing process takes 1–2 hours (requires uploading actual tax returns — not transcripts, not watermarked copies).
- **Timeline:** IRS processes in 6–12 months. Prosperity Tax Group's "Advance Program" accelerates payment to 30–45 days by collateralizing the credit with a bank — unique in the industry at time of presentation.
- **Fee structure:** 0% upfront. Prosperity Tax Group earns 20% contingency only upon successful receipt of funds. No charge if no money collected.
- **Competing CPA risk:** Many CPAs either don't know about the SETC or may discourage clients out of embarrassment or confusion with PPP loans (which had different rules). Alex recommends filing regardless of CPA's stance.
- **Pre-existing PPP loans do not disqualify** for SETC — they are completely separate programs.
- **S-corps paying W-2 salaries** likely disqualify — the credit is for 1099 self-employment income, not W-2 wages.
- **Married couples filing jointly** complete one combined filing; both spouses with 1099 income can each qualify up to $32,200.
