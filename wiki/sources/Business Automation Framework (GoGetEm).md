# Business Automation Framework (GoGet'Em)

> A framework training for GoGet'Em community members on how to systematically eliminate, automate, and delegate the work that doesn't require the owner — freeing capacity for high-leverage activity.

## Key Takeaways

- **Eliminate → Automate → Delegate (in that order):** The sequence matters. Never automate a task that should be eliminated. Never delegate a task that can be automated. The order of operations prevents building complexity on top of waste.
- **Deep business breakdown — four pillars:**
  1. **Lead Generation** — how suspects enter the funnel
  2. **Sales** — how suspects convert to clients
  3. **Client Delivery** — how clients receive the service they paid for
  4. **Throughput** — how many clients the system can handle without the owner becoming the bottleneck
  Mapping all four pillars reveals which one is the real constraint.
- **"The Road Less Stupid" (Keith Cunningham):** The framework trains on thinking time — scheduled, daily, solitary time to think about the business rather than in it. Keith Cunningham's concept: most business mistakes are made in the absence of thinking, not the absence of information.
- **15 minutes of daily thinking time:** Practical implementation — block 15 minutes each morning to ask: "What is the single most important question I need to answer today?" Write the answer. This prevents reactive daily drift and builds a compound advantage over time.
- **Automation before delegation:** If a task can be handled by a tool (GHL automation, Zapier, ManyChat), build the automation before hiring a person to do it. Automation scales; human labor caps.

## Quotes

> "Eliminate before you automate. Automate before you delegate. Most people get this backwards."

> "Most mistakes aren't made because you didn't have the information. They're made because you didn't take the time to think."
