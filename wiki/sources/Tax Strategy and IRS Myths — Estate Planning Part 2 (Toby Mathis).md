# Tax Strategy and IRS Myths — Estate Planning Part 2 (Toby Mathis)

> Toby Mathis's second GoGet'Em appearance covers IRS myth-busting (the "87,000 agents" story), sole proprietor vs. S-corp audit risk, and why 70% of realtors are structured in the most dangerous way possible for an IRS audit.

## Key Takeaways

- **IRS 87,000 agents myth:** The Inflation Reduction Act funding for 87,000 IRS agents represents replacements over 10 years — not new auditors hired next month to audit taxpayers. The IRS audit rate actually dropped 70%+ since 2012 because they stopped rehiring after the Great Recession.
- **Who actually gets audited:**
  - EITC (Earned Income Tax Credit) recipients — poor families without CPAs; IRS wins easily
  - Sole proprietors (Schedule C filers) — 1,000-1,500% more likely to be audited than S-corps
  - Millionaires: surprisingly low audit risk right now (counterintuitive)
- **The sole proprietor trap:** 70% of realtors file income on Schedule C as sole proprietors. They lose 94-95% of audits. This is the most dangerous business structure for a real estate agent.
- **How to know if you're a sole proprietor:** If you don't file a separate business tax return AND don't draw a salary from your business, you're almost certainly a Schedule C filer — and that's a problem.
- **S-corp advantages:**
  - Lower audit risk (1,000-1,500% lower than sole proprietors)
  - Employment tax savings — significant even at a $25K/year salary level
  - Accountable plan — allows deductions unavailable to sole proprietors
  - Structure: you are both employer and employee; pay income tax only on your salary, not all money hitting your bank account
- **The breakeven:** Even at $25K/year, converting to an S-corp saves enough in employment taxes to justify the cost and complexity. The math pencils out early.
- **Estate planning 101:** Toby opened the floor for questions on basic estate planning — who needs it, core documents (will, trust, power of attorney), when to start. Practical and accessible.
