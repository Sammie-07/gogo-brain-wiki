# GoGet'Em Guest Training — Krista Mashore — Origin Story and Mindset

> Krista Mashore appears as a GoGet'Em guest to share her origin story — severe childhood abuse, foster care, juvenile hall — and how she built one of the most successful real estate coaching businesses in the industry.

## Key Takeaways

- **The story:** Krista suffered severe physical abuse as a child. Left home at 13. Lived in cars, abandoned buildings, friends' closets. Sent to juvenile hall for 3.5 months after breaking into a school locker room.
- **Early struggles:** Couldn't read until 4th grade (special education placement). Has a learning disability — reads things 5 times before they click. After learning to read, became a straight-A student.
- **Foster care:** Couldn't return home. Called her parole officer and was placed in foster care for remaining years. Her father wept the day she left — he didn't know what was happening.
- **Success credentials:** 2,300+ homes sold. Top 1% of agents nationwide almost every year. $45M+ in online contracted sales in 4.5 years. $25M and $10M production awards.
- **Mentors:** Invested hundreds of thousands of dollars in personal development. A mentor told her that leaders need to let people get to know who they are. It took nearly two years to decide to share her story publicly.
- **Core message:** "We all started from the same place." When you see someone successful and tell yourself you can't do what they do — that is an excuse. Nothing about Gogo's accent or appearance or outgoing nature prevents you from achieving what she achieved. The story is the same for all of us.
- **Relationship with Gogo:** They mastermind together, speak regularly, and view each other as allies. "There is plenty of real estate business to go around."
- **GoGet'Em format:** Gogo asked Krista to start with her childhood — believing that agents seeing the full foundation changes how they hear the success framework that follows.

## Quotes

> "When you see people that are successful, the brain does things where we say, 'Well, I can't do that because of this.' It is an excuse and I'm totally going to be tough love and honest."

> "I am somebody who wet the bed until they were 10, could not read until they were in fourth grade, and has not lived home since they were 13. Anything and everything that I have, I've worked my butt off for."
