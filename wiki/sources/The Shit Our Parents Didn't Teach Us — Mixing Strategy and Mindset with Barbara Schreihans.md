# The Shit Our Parents Didn't Teach Us: Mixing Strategy and Mindset with Barbara Schreihans

> GoGet'Em Community "marathon of the soul" deep-dive event. Barbara Schreihans — founder of Your Tax Coach, creator of "How to Write Off Your Life" — opens with her raw personal origin story then delivers her money mindset and financial strategy framework.

## Key Takeaways

**Origin Story (for mindset context)**
- Born 10 weeks premature to a crack cocaine addict mother; literally born addicted to crack and went through hospital withdrawal
- Both parents drug addicts her entire life; mother worked 3 minimum-wage jobs; father was physically abusive and had a 5-year affair
- Father deliberately fell from telephone poles to collect lawsuit settlements; parents filed bankruptcy every 7 years
- Barbara started working at age 12; graduated high school at 15 (just before 16th birthday)
- Got pregnant in college; paid for years of daycare with credit cards; graduated with enormous student loan + credit card debt
- First job: $57K/year in Bay Area with $2,500/month rent and a Prius at 14% interest
- Now: Your Tax Coach on Inc 5000 list at #513, top-5 podcast, 30 employees, 100+ investment doors, moving family to Paris for 2 years
- Her purpose: if saving money helps even one woman escape an abusive relationship, the empire is worth building. 1 in 6 women are in abusive relationships.

**Money Mindset — The Journaling Exercise**
- Your relationship with money is a mixture of your parents' relationships with money — even if you consciously reject their patterns
- Journal exercise: write your mother's relationship with money, then your father's. Compare. Find yourself in both.
- Common patterns: money as secret topic, one partner surrendered paycheck and received an allowance, layaway and payday loans as normal tools, spending triggered guilt or shame
- Historical context: women could not get credit cards in their own name until ~50 years ago

**Confronting Your Money**
- unclaimed.org — government website with unchecked balances tied to your SSN. Search your name and maiden name. People have found million-dollar life insurance policies.

**Money Likes Consistency — Auto-Transfer Rules**
- Auto-transfer every payday to: savings, tax account, stock accounts, Roth IRA
- You will not miss money that leaves before you can decide to spend it
- Start at $5–$20/week if necessary — the habit matters more than the amount
- Barbara auto-transferred $5/week into Acorns starting 2018 while in debt; that account was $100K when she checked it. She now has another $100K stock account.
- Acorns app: rounds up purchases automatically; cash back at common retailers; starts at $5/week

**Bi-Weekly Mortgage Hack**
- Switch from one monthly mortgage payment to paying every two weeks
- On a $1M loan: saves $170,000+ in total interest; pays off in 25 years instead of 30
- Some lenders allow it online; others require a phone call

**Roth IRA — Tax-Free Millionaire Math**
- $134.61/week from age 35 to age 65 = over $1 million tax-free at retirement
- Roth grows tax-free; withdrawn tax-free at retirement
- Income limit is approximately $300K/year for direct contribution
- High earners use backdoor Roth: contribute to non-deductible traditional IRA, immediately convert to Roth
- If your financial advisor never mentioned backdoor Roth, fire them and find one who has
- Set up on Fidelity.com free; must invest the money inside the account (do not leave it in cash)
- Over age 50: contribute an additional $1,000/year

**High-Yield Savings Account**
- Standard bank savings: 0.005% interest. High-yield savings: 4–5% currently
- $100K in high-yield = $5,000/year in passive interest for no effort
- Move any cash sitting in regular savings immediately

**Celebrating Money**
- As you get more successful, do not stop celebrating wins — Barbara noticed her income increased when she returned to intentionally celebrating
- Little wins: afford a house cleaner ($150/week) — celebrate it
- Medium wins: spa day or fancy champagne when hitting a monthly business goal
- Big wins: designer bag every time she closes on an investment property
- Celebrate every dollar coming in AND every dollar going out — gratitude for ability to pay bills
- Pick up every penny off the ground. Say thank you. The universe notices when you scoff at small money.
- Book recommendation: Happy Money — teaches to celebrate every dollar that leaves your account

**Solo 401K**
- For business owners with no full-time employees with 1+ year tenure
- Contribute up to $69,000/year as a business deduction

**HSA — Triple Tax Benefit**
- Only with high-deductible health plan
- Contributions tax-free (reduce taxable income), growth tax-free, medical withdrawals tax-free
- Strategy: put money in now and let it compound until age 65+; use for medical at retirement
- Do not spend it now — compounding is the entire point

**Flexible Spending Account (FSA)**
- For children under 5: pay up to $6,600 of daycare expenses tax-free

**Defined Benefit Plan + Profit Sharing**
- Layer on top of maxed 401K ($23K/year cap)
- Barbara has clients contributing $250K+ to their own defined benefit plan per year
- Contributions are discretionary — can skip in bad years
- Can be done at year-end (up to when tax return is filed, even with extension to September/October)
- Can take a loan against the plan to buy real estate; interest paid back to yourself (not a bank)

**Life Insurance as Wealth Vehicle**
- Cash-value life insurance provides a payout at retirement age, not just at death
- Build cash flow for retirement beyond standard retirement accounts

**Gifting Strategy and Estate Planning**
- Gift up to $18,000/year to any person anywhere in the world, tax-free to recipient
- Couples can each gift $18,000 = $36,000/child/year tax-free
- Above $18,000 to one person: still tax-free to recipient, but you file a gift tax return and it reduces your lifetime estate exemption
- Estate tax exemption: currently $13M per individual ($26M for married couples), expected to drop to ~$4M soon
- Gifting now moves wealth out of your estate at no current tax cost
- Money goes into UTMA accounts (guardian-managed investment accounts for minors)

**Kids on Payroll**
- Must be doing actual work in your business
- Tax-free to them: up to $14,600 (2024) / $15,000 (2025) per child
- Business deduction for you
- Use the income to fund Roth IRA ($7,000/year limit), UTMA accounts, or life insurance
- Add kids to board of advisors — they learn business by being in the room
- Board meetings anywhere (Bahamas, Europe) = business travel deduction

**QBI Deduction**
- 20% deduction for business owners; was set to expire; Trump indicated he would extend it
- Gogo mentioned her CPA was adjusting her W-2 salary to maximize the QBI deduction
