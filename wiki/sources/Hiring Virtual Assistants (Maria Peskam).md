# Hiring Virtual Assistants (Maria Peskam)

> Maria Peskam's framework for finding, hiring, onboarding, and retaining virtual assistants. Covers her proprietary D×E=V formula, the four C's cloning system, the 10/80/10 rule, and GoGet'Em's special VA placement program.

## Key Takeaways

- **D×E=V formula (Desire × Execution = Value):** A VA's value is not their skill set alone — it's the product of their desire to grow and their ability to execute. A highly skilled VA with low desire creates less value than a moderately skilled VA with high desire and strong execution.
- **Four C's cloning system:** To hire someone who can truly take over your tasks, document the four C's for each role:
  1. **Content** — what the task actually is
  2. **Context** — why it matters and how it fits the bigger picture
  3. **Criteria** — what "done right" looks like
  4. **Consequences** — what happens if it's done wrong or not at all
- **10/80/10 rule for delegation:** Give the VA 10% context upfront, let them handle 80% of the execution, then review and refine the last 10%. Resist the urge to take over at the 80% stage.
- **GoGet'Em special pricing:** $9,500/year (vs. open market rates) for English+Spanish bilingual VAs operating on US business hours. Available exclusively to GoGet'Em community members.
- **Hire for attitude, train for skill:** VAs who are coachable and motivated outperform technically skilled but resistant hires. Attitude cannot be trained; skill can.
- **Cross-training from day one:** Every VA should know at least one other role in the business. If the primary VA exits, operations should not collapse.

## Quotes

> "Desire times execution equals value. A 10 in skill with a 2 in desire still only gives you a 20. A 7 in skill with a 9 in desire gives you a 63."

> "You don't need a perfect VA. You need a coachable one."
