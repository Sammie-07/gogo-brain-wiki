# AI for Real Estate Professionals — Intelligence Briefs, Client Avatars & AI Agents (Thomas Heimann)

> A [[GoGet'Em Community]] weekly training in which guest [[Thomas Heimann]] walks agents through adopting AI in their business — building personal and business intelligence briefs, constructing a named client avatar, deploying it in a custom GPT, and automating work with AI agents like [[Claude Cowork]].

## Key Takeaways
- **Three eras of AI** (Thomas's framing): (1) chatbots — you still do the work; (2) custom agents — you guide the work via a set of rules (e.g. [[Claude Cowork]]); (3) agentic AI — agents do the work and hand off to each other in "swarms."
- **Three foundation documents** every agent should build: a **Personal Intelligence Brief** (who you are, values, story, communication style — so AI sounds like you); a **Business Intelligence Brief** (brand, market, USP, systems — one per business); a **Client Avatar** (a named persona with backstory, fears, and aspirations).
- **Build the personal brief by recording your life story** — Thomas dictated his on walks (using a recorder), transcribed it, and used the transcript to generate the brief; he is now on version 5 of his personal brief and updates each brief roughly every 3 months.
- **Client avatar is a 4-step process**: (1) market intelligence report; (2) ideal customer identification (segment, price band, motivation); (3) avatar construction — must be a real person with a name, backstory, fears, and aspirations; (4) deployment in a custom GPT or project.
- **Use "Projects" to segregate memory** — projects share memory internally but don't leak across projects, preventing the AI from getting "confused"; "garbage in, garbage out" — poor prompting and poor input drive poor results.
- **Custom GPTs** keep their memory within the GPT, can be shared by link, or published/sold in the GPT store; you can pick the underlying model and add knowledge files (brief + market report + avatar).
- **Live demo with agent Holly (Oahu)**: generated a market report, a short personal intelligence brief, and a buyer avatar ("Malia"), then a custom GPT ("Marketing to Malia") that outputs platform-separated social content (Facebook / Instagram / LinkedIn / TikTok), image-generation prompts, and HeyGen video scripts.
- **Model selection**: **Claude Sonnet** = fast everyday work and content; **Claude Opus** = high-level reasoning and big decisions (Thomas used Opus across ~8 rounds to negotiate a partnership/operating agreement); **Claude Haiku** = simple fast tasks like email checks. Sonnet yields roughly 5x the usage of Opus, so don't default to Opus; Claude lets you switch models mid-chat (ChatGPT does not). When unsure, just ask the AI which model to use.
- **[[Claude Cowork]]** runs scheduled or one-off tasks inside a virtual machine, connecting to Gmail/Slack and a shared folder — Thomas uses it to auto-sort downloaded email attachments and to run a 10-step "Building in Public" content pipeline that transcribes a video, drafts the blog/social posts, and creates an Asana task for a teammate.
- **Tools named**: Whisper Flow (dictation, "a million times better than Apple Dictation"); OpenClaw (agent platform — most GitHub stars ever; Thomas runs a 19-agent autonomous org and an Opus-vs-ChatGPT debate setup on it); HeyGen (Avatar 4 video/avatars); ElevenLabs (professional voice clones — upload up to ~2 hrs audio, $20/mo plan, usable in HeyGen via API key); Google Gemini (free with Google Workspace, good for research); Claude for CMAs (better/easier to adjust than RPR).
- **Pricing**: base $20/mo ChatGPT/Claude plans hit usage walls; Thomas runs $100/mo Max plans on both after racking up $100 in overage fees; ChatGPT recommended as the more budget-friendly option if money is tight.
- **Free Team Gogo offers**: an AI Toolkit (includes a real estate coach GPT and a Florida real estate social-media GPT, plus a Claude Cowork getting-started guide) via QR code; **Sarah**, an AI real estate executive assistant at meetsarah.io (lives in WhatsApp, free 3 months then $19.95/mo vs $49.95 regular); **Apex AI Coach** at apexaicoach.com (bespoke 2-hr life-story session + 8 personality profiles, 30-day money-back guarantee, 30% Team Gogo discount expiring Sunday at midnight).
- **Mortgage Minutes ([[Jason Dupree]], [[Texana]])**: rates ~6.5% (down >40 bps YoY); refinances up 35% YoY; purchases up 8% YoY but down recently; a Fed rate increase is back on the table as inflation rises. The **REMLO** program lets agents become licensed loan officers in all 50 states (requires 6 buy-side transactions in the prior 12 months), earning ~$1,600–$2,200 per file plus commissions on HELOCs/bridge loans previously referred out.
- **Urgency**: Thomas (citing Tony Robbins) warns the next 2 years will bring more advance than all prior human history; waiting a year lets AI-using competitors leapfrog you.
