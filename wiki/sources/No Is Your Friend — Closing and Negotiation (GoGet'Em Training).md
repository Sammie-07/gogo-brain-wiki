# No Is Your Friend — Closing and Negotiation (GoGet'Em Training)

> An unidentified GoGet'Em presenter teaches a complete closing and negotiation framework built around the principle that "no" is the most powerful tool in a real estate conversation — rooted in Chris Voss's "that's right" methodology.

## Key Takeaways

- **Core insight:** Clients want to say no. No is their safe place. The goal is not to force yes — it's to make "no" your friend by framing questions so that "no" is the answer you want.
- **Chris Voss integration:** "That's right" is the gold standard for closing — the moment a client says "that's right," both parties feel confident and aligned. This is better than "yes," which can trigger buyer's remorse.
- **"Stop selling, start helping":** The moment clients sense you're selling them something, they resist. "Nobody wants to be sold. Everyone wants to buy." Your job is to help them get what they told you they want.
- **No-framing examples:**
  - "If I found you a house that didn't have these things, would you want to buy it?" → "No" = perfect alignment.
  - "We certainly wouldn't want to spend money on a house that didn't have what we wanted, would we?" → "No" = validation that you're on their side.
  - This makes clients feel heard, understood, and in control — reducing buyer's remorse risk.
- **Outside-in showing strategy:** Before entering a house, send clients to look at the street while you open the lock box. "We sell outside in — community → neighborhood → street → house." 
- **Why outside-in:** Clients tell each other things they won't tell you — concerns about the neighbor's yard, the railroad tracks, the proximity to a commercial plaza. The outside-in approach surfaces these objections BEFORE they kill the deal silently while you're showing the interior.
- **Lock box example:** Don't make clients stand in rain/wind while you struggle with a lock box. Tell them: "Stay here, take a look at the street — I'll get the door open and come get you." Professional, client-centered, practical.
- **50-question library:** The presenter references a list of 50 questions for buyer attraction and closing situations.
- **Empathy foundation:** Clients can feel whether you're working for them or for your commission. Empathy transmitted = trust established = deals close.
