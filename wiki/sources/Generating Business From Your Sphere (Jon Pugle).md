# Generating Business From Your Sphere

> Guest trainer [[Jon Pugle]] teaches a GoGet'Em Community class on stopping the chase for strangers and instead mining the people who already know, like, and trust you — through handwritten notes, gratitude videos, situational outreach, a 200-person power list, connector economics, and client-appreciation events.

## Key Takeaways
- **Sphere before strangers.** The fastest path to business (in both production and agent attraction) is people who already know, like, and trust you — not open houses, paid leads, or cold strangers. Roughly 80–90% of real estate transactions come by referral or someone the client knows; only ~10% work with a stranger.
- **Human connection is the AI-proof moat.** "The antidote for AI is human connection." AI will replace automations and back-office work, but not the people portion of the job — so lean hard into being human to insulate yourself.
- **Handwritten notes are the killer tactic.** A Guinness-record car salesman sold 13,000 cars in one year with no drip campaigns — just handwritten gratitude notes to everyone he'd ever met, every month. A real estate agent copied it, sent 40 notes his first month, and made **$40,000** from a single reply. Notes stand out because nobody gets them; people keep them on their desk.
- **Five notes a day.** Pugle's stepdad (a top Southern California broker for 40 years who ran Coldwell Banker) drilled it in: write and mail five handwritten notes every day and you'll never need to ask for business again. Keep them 3–5 sentences, personal, no business card, no ask.
- **30-second gratitude videos.** Film a specific, genuine video for each person ("Hey Mike, thought of you, so grateful for you…") — never one generic clip blasted to everyone. It's not salesy; it sets you apart.
- **Situational outreach triggers** remove the "I don't know what to say" excuse: "driving by your neighborhood," "I'll be in your area Tuesday anyway," "I'm already showing a house nearby — want to piggyback?" Telling them you'll already be there takes the pressure off. Also mine their last three social posts and comment on something specific — the guessing game is over.
- **Tag your CRM by interest.** Pugle's best lender flagged him under "golf" and periodically sent golf articles, jokes, products, invites — felt personal, wasn't. Ask ChatGPT to find a relevant article/product for each interest tag, then send "saw this and thought of you." People love to be thought of.
- **Put people's kids and pets at the forefront.** He won a listing by making the sellers' three dogs part of the pitch, then followed up with custom dog-bakery treats — still praised in his Yelp review years later. "People love their pets and their children more than they love themselves."
- **Community leadership and sponsorship.** Don't buy a $250 little-league banner — pledge $250 (or $100) *from every closing* and broadcast it far and wide, so the whole community is motivated to send you business. Bring the free snow-cone/ice-cream truck on opening day (costs almost nothing), branded shirt on, no ask. If you won't sponsor, volunteer/lead (PTA, coach, church); if you won't lead, at least join a recurring hobby group at the same day/time/place so the same people see you consistently.
- **The 200-person power list → 50 closings.** Call (never text) all 200, warm them for ~3 months adding value, then ask each for *one* introduction over the next 12 months via a **three-way text** (like introducing two people at a party — they'll honor it). Hold them accountable ~4x/year. Math: 150 say yes → ~75 actually introduce → ~50 close. Even a botched version (35 closings) makes you an icon.
- **"Just want one" scales to every vendor.** Hold title, escrow, mortgage, inspectors accountable to one introduction per year in exchange for your referrals — "if you can't give me one, we don't work together anymore."
- **The connector role is the whole job.** Ask each contact "where's the pain?" and what they need (HVAC, babysitter, housekeeper), then map needs to vendors across your database, deliver warm intros both directions, and demand one real estate referral from each vendor in return. Connect people to what they need and "you will be rich."
- **Board-of-directors dinner.** Once a year, take your 12 best (or most influential) referrers to a fancy dinner, present your goals, and ask them to co-author your client events and vendor list — "people participate in things they author." Treat influential-but-quiet people as if they already refer heavily and they start to.
- **Four client-appreciation events a year = ~30 touches.** Save-the-date → invite → reminder → event → "sorry we missed you" (the most powerful touch — people want to be missed). Most invitees never show, so you get all the touches without paying for them; split the cost with lender/title. Use a **VIP intake form** (spouse, kids, pets, anniversary, favorite food/restaurant, charity) to send **unexpected, personalized gifts** — e.g. calling a client's vacation hotel to send chips-and-guac to their room (hotels often comp it). "Treat people's children better than you treat them" — send sick kids' gift baskets.
