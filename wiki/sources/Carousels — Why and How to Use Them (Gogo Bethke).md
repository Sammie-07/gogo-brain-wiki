# Carousels — Why and How to Use Them (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo explains what carousel posts are, why the algorithm rewards them above single-image posts, the slide structure that maximizes engagement, content ideas for real estate, and how to get started. Includes live analytics from her pinned "16 things I track" carousel.

## Key Takeaways

### What a Carousel Is
A multi-slide post (up to 10 slides) on Instagram or Facebook. Each slide can be the same image with different text, or different images — but always contains information the viewer has to click through to fully consume.

### Why Carousels Win With the Algorithm
The old metric: follower count.
The current metric: **time spent on profile + saves + shares + comments.**

Carousels force engagement by design — viewers who want all 10 steps must click through every slide. Eight minutes on one post sends a powerful signal:

> "Instagram sees that Joe Schmo just spent the last 8 minutes on your profile... That tells Instagram that whatever you posted must be very informational, must be really good — and they're going to feed it to other people."

**Engagement signals that trigger algorithmic distribution:**
- Time spent (clicking through slides)
- Saves ("I want to come back to this")
- Shares ("my friends need to see this")
- Comments (volume = quality signal)

**Real example — Gogo's pinned "16 Things I Track" carousel:**
| Metric | Number |
|--------|--------|
| Views | 9,000 |
| Likes | 133 |
| Comments | 119 |
| Saves | 48 |
| Shares | 11 |
| Existing followers | 82.3% |
| **Non-followers (algorithm push)** | **17.7%** |

17.7% of 9,000 views = ~1,600 strangers reached for free — because the algorithm saw saves, shares, and deep engagement.

### Carousel Structure
1. **Slide 1 — Hook:** Must be catchy enough to make them click. Everything depends on this.
2. **Slides 2–9 — Content:** Valuable, detailed, educational. Make each slide worth the click.
3. **Slide 10 (or earlier) — CTA:** What do you want them to do next? Event, free resource, DM, booking.

*CTA placement tip:* If you're unsure they'll stay to the end, put the CTA earlier in the sequence — don't lose it at the bottom.

### Content Ideas for Real Estate Carousels
- **Market report:** Monthly snapshot of your farm area — avg days on market, avg price, local news (new businesses, school districts). Title: *"Top 10 most random facts about [County] real estate market."*
- **Buyer education:** "10 steps to buying your first home"
- **Seller education:** "10 steps to selling your home for top dollar"
- **Educational/framework:** Multi-step processes, checklists, systems (like Gogo's 16 trackers)
- **Local spotlights:** New businesses, community events, neighborhood developments

**Rule:** Always include yourself in the photos. *"We are selling you and your services."* You at the listing sign, at closing, at the open house, in the kitchen.

### Creation Workflow
- **Tool:** Canva — ideal for professional-looking carousels
- **Gogo's process:** She generates the idea; Ellen (Team Gogo VA) creates in Canva
- **Starting cadence:** One carousel per month → scale to one per week once comfortable

## Quotes

> "Back in the day it mattered how many followers you have. Today it matters how much time people spend on your profile, how many times they comment, how many times they share your post, how many times they save your post."

> "Make sure you're in the picture — because we are selling you and your services."
