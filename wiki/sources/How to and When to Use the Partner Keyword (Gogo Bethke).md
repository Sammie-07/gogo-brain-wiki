# How to and When to Use the Partner Keyword (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo explains the strategic WHY behind the "partner" keyword — specifically how storytelling is the trigger that makes agents want to comment, and how the automation takes it from there.

## Key Takeaways

- The "partner" keyword ManyChat automation exists to move agents from social media to your "Partner With Me" page and ultimately into your calendar — exactly as buyers and sellers are moved off-app.
- **"Facts tell, stories sell."** Don't pitch eXp to agents. Tell a story that makes them feel what it would be like to be part of your team — then invite them to comment the keyword at the end.
- **Gogo's live example:** Her son (type 1 diabetic) had an incident while traveling. Team Go agent Valerie helped find a diabetic device in the middle of the night, four hours away. Gogo posted the story, ended with: *"If you don't have a real estate family like ours, why don't you join us? Comment or DM me the word 'partner' and I can't wait to see you on my calendar."*
- The keyword is flexible — can be "partner," "agent," "join," or anything that fits your brand. What matters is that it takes them to your "Partner With Me" page.
- When to use it: any time you're talking to an agent audience and want them to join you. Story format → keyword CTA → automation handles the rest.
- The automation (ManyChat) then: sends welcome message → "I am in" button → calendar link → 23-hour follow-up if no action taken.

## Quotes

> "Facts tell, stories sell."

> "If you don't have a real estate family like ours, like the Team Go family, why don't you join us? Just comment or DM me the word 'partner' and I can't wait to see you on my calendar."

> "You can change it to 'agent.' You can change it to 'join.' You can change it to whatever that keyword is going to be for you."
