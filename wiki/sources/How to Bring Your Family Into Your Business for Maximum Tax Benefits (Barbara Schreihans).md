# How to Bring Your Family Into Your Business for Maximum Tax Benefits (Barbara Schreihans)

> GoGet'Em Live Training. Barbara Schreihans ("The Tax Coach") — CPA and tax strategist, brand: "Shit Our Parents Should've Told Us." Previous GoGetEm guest. Topic: legal strategies for hiring family members to reduce taxes, fund retirement, and shift income.

## Key Takeaways

- **Core principle.** IRS allows business owners to pay legitimate wages to family members for real work. Done correctly: creates a business deduction, shifts income to lower brackets, funds retirement accounts.
- **Hiring children.** Children under 18 working for a sole proprietor parent are exempt from Social Security and Medicare taxes. Standard deduction (~$13,850) means the first ~$13K of wages is tax-free to the child. Child can fund a Roth IRA with earned income — powerful long-term wealth building.
- **Legitimate work for children.** Social media content creation, data entry, filing, photography, video, office cleaning. Must be real work, documented, paid at market rate.
- **Tax math example.** 37% bracket owner pays 16-year-old $12K for real social media work: owner deducts $12K (saves ~$4,440 in federal tax), child owes zero (under standard deduction), child can contribute to Roth IRA. Family unit net benefit: $4,440+ in tax savings.
- **Hiring a spouse.** Spouse salary opens access to additional retirement accounts (401(k), HSA). Must have a legitimate role at market-rate compensation.
- **Family LLCs / management companies.** More sophisticated structure — create a management company owned by lower-tax-bracket family members; business pays management fees to family entity.
- **Documentation is non-negotiable.** Time records, job descriptions, market-rate pay. Paper trail essential for audit protection.
- **Why this matters for agents.** Self-employed real estate agents miss strategies W-2 employees don't have. Family employment is one of the most underutilized legal strategies available.
- **Gogo connection.** Aligns with Gogo's "make it, keep it, grow it" philosophy — not just earning wealth, but structuring to keep it.
