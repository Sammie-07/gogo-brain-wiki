# Homepage Structure — Copywriting for Websites (Gogo Bethke)

> Third video in the GSMC 2026 copywriting series. Gogo walks through her own live websites (The Circle coaching, gobethke.com, partner-with-me/eXp, GoGet'Em Community) to show what a proper homepage structure looks like and why each element matters.

## Key Takeaways

- **8 homepage elements** — in order of importance. Not every website needs all 8; what's required depends on the complexity of what you're selling.

### The 8 Elements

**1. Above the Fold**
What a visitor sees on landing — *without scrolling*. Must instantly communicate what the page is about. Two checks:
- Does it explain exactly what this is in one glance?
- Is the **mobile version** also optimized? (Builders always do desktop first and forget mobile — check both)

*Example — The Circle:* "High-level limited seats mastermind for six-figure entrepreneurs to scale to seven figures."

**2. Clear Outcome Statement**
Plain language. No big words. No corporate-speak. Says exactly what the visitor will get. Same as the above-the-fold copy on simpler pages — they're often the same thing.

**3. Who Is It For / Who Is It NOT For**
Gogo *starts* with who it's NOT for to filter out unqualified leads before they waste her time on clarity calls. For The Circle: *"If your goal is to learn how to close your next deal, this is not the place for you."* This qualification filter is intentional — she has outgrown early-stage coaching and wants to spend her time on $650K+ earners ready to scale to millions.

**4. Call to Action**
Crystal clear. Assume the visitor has no idea who you are. Spell it out. One button. One action.
- eXp page: *"Let's Talk eXp"*
- The Circle: *"Apply Now"*
Never leave visitors guessing what to do next.

**5. SEO Optimization**
Keywords = the words Google spiders scan when indexing your page. Think: "If I had never heard of me and wanted to find me, what would I search?" For realtors: *"best realtor in Brighton Michigan"* — that phrase must appear in your website copy verbatim (or close to it).

*Live example of what not to do:* Gogo realized mid-video that her Circle coaching page never once used the word "coach" or "coaching." Called her team to fix it on the spot. "That's very stupid. This is going to get redone."

**6. Header**
The top visual element of the page. Options:
- Static image (your photo + name) — most common, clean
- Interactive/animated header — Gogo's gobethke.com header moves; she finds static boring
The header is the visual impression before any words land.

**7. Video**
Required when the decision is complex or high-stakes. Optional for simple products where the visitor already understands what they're buying.

*Why Gogo uses 4–5 videos on her eXp partner page:*
- Switching brokerages is a life decision — people shouldn't do it just because she said so
- Videos replace her having to repeat hour-long presentations (each 1–2 hrs) to every single agent
- Agents watch at their own pace; by the time they book, they've already self-qualified
- *"Saves six hours of my life. They watch it whenever they are ready to make the switch."*
- After watching, they see the "Let's Talk eXp" button and book themselves

**8. FAQ Section**
Only include if you keep getting the same questions. Proactively answers objections so buyers can decide without contacting you. Good for digital products, membership programs, and listing/buyer pages where commission rate and coverage area questions repeat.

**Testimonials** (bonus — placed at ~75% down the page):
Social proof that helps undecided visitors say yes. Video testimonials are best (rare); written testimonials from Google/Facebook are fine. Already have reviews? Pull them in as images. *"If Casey and Deborah and Lou and Bridget made this decision, I should too."*

## Quotes

> "'Above the fold' means if I click a link and land on that website without scrolling — what do I see? That's the 'above the fold' message."

> "Most of them forget to adjust the cell phone version. Make sure your cell phone version gets optimized too."

> "I not only include who is it for, but sometimes — and a lot of the times — I also include who is it not for just to limit the application."

> "Imagine that everybody who is on your website has no idea who you are. They have no idea what you do. Spell it out to them."

> "I don't have to be a broken record. Record yourself. Put the videos in. Tell the agents they have to watch these before they jump in my calendar."

> "If you were looking for the 'best realtor in Brighton, Michigan,' does your website say 'best realtor Brighton, Michigan'? If not, you should probably include it."
