# Tax Credit Tools for Real Estate — 45L and Cost Segregation (Dawn Lavanway, Eric Oliver)

> GoGet'Em community training delivered by Dawn Lavanway (Account Executive) and Eric Oliver (VP, Channel Partnerships) of Cost Segregation Authority — a firm Gogo herself uses for her rental properties. Gogo was on a cruise; Larissa hosted. Covers the two primary tax credit tools: 45L energy credits and cost segregation with bonus depreciation.

## Key Takeaways

**45L Credit:**
- 45L = energy-efficient home credit under Inflation Reduction Act; now permanent through 2032
- $500-$5,000 per door for properties placed in service 2023-2032 (inflation-adjusted)
- Applies to any dwelling unit (eat/sleep/cook/bathroom) — ADUs, fourplexes, SFRs, apartment buildings
- Credit goes to whoever held the construction loan (had basis at construction) — not the final buyer
- Can amend returns to go back 3 years
- Remodels may qualify if taken down to studs with new windows + insulation
- RESNET-certified third-party certification required (~$300-400/door)
- Non-refundable — carries forward until fully utilized

**Cost Segregation:**
- Accelerated depreciation: reclassify components of real estate from 27.5/39-yr to 5/7/15-yr
- Three IRS tests: removable without damage / retired with connected equipment / unique to business use
- 5-7 yr assets: carpet, countertops, cabinets, LVP flooring, window coverings, appliance electrical/plumbing
- 15-yr assets: parking lots, curbs, gutters, trees/shrubs, irrigation, asphalt
- Typically reclassifies ~30% of building's depreciable basis to short-term assets
- Bonus depreciation (2024): 60%, phases down 20%/yr to zero by 2027 (Congress attempting to restore 100%)
- "New to you" rule: bonus depreciation applies to USED properties — you don't need to buy new
- Catch-up depreciation: do cost seg on properties already owned, front-load all missed deductions onto current-year return
- Rate arbitrage: deduct now at income tax rate, pay recapture at lower capital gains rate — not a wash, it's a spread
- Cost Segregation Authority: 20 years, 25,000 studies, 40-50 pg reports, partners with your CPA (doesn't file taxes)
- Gogo used them on her personal rental properties and was excited about the results
