# Let's Do Some Math Again

> Short-form video. [[Gogo Bethke]] makes the PPP (Productivity Per Person) argument for why brokerage choice is a production decision, citing Team Go's numbers vs. the national average.

## Key Takeaways

- **PPP = Productivity Per Person.** Total transactions ÷ headcount. The honest measure of a brokerage's production environment.
- **Most agents don't know their brokerage's PPP** — or don't care. Gogo argues this is a critical mistake.
- **National brokerage average:** ~2 transactions/agent/year
- **[[wiki/entities/#teamgogo]] PPP:** 6.15 — roughly 3× the national average
- **NAR stat (2025):** 71.1% of licensed US realtors sold **zero** homes in 2025. This number weighs heavily on industry averages.
- **Team Go trailing 12 months:** 7,445 transactions. 2026 target: ~10,000.
- **"You are the average of the 5 people you surround yourself with."** If true in life, why accept a low-PPP environment at work?
