# Control Your Emotions, Control Your Income (Gary Coxe)

> GoGet'Em Community session with behavioral expert Gary Coxe on the link between emotional control and income. Companion to the earlier "How Your Feelings Can Sabotage" session.

## Key Takeaways

- **Gary Coxe background:** Was on NBC; helped a company generate $1M in sales; worked with Dr. Phil for 3 years.
- **Core premise:** Your feelings are fabricated — they do not represent objective reality. You create them based on thoughts and interpretations.
- **"Future and past is present" framework:** People live mentally in either the past (regret, trauma) or future (anxiety, worry) — never in the present. Income suffers because action only happens in the present.
- **Positive thinking = spray paint on rust.** Affirmations without addressing root beliefs are temporary. You must change the underlying belief structure.
- **Faith vs. doubt:**
  - Faith = natural ability to take action without evidence.
  - Doubt = inaction, fear, anxiety. Doubt kills income before action begins.
- **Belief vs. value (extended):** If your behavior contradicts your stated belief, your *value* (what you actually reinforce with action) is the real driver — not the belief you claim.
- **Emotional control = income control:** Agents who react emotionally to objections, rejection, or market shifts lose deals. Neutral emotional state = consistent prospecting = consistent income.

## Quotes

> "Positive thinking is like spray paint on rust. It covers it, but the rust is still there."

> "Faith is your natural ability to take action. Doubt is just fear in disguise."

> "Your feelings are fabricated. You made them up."
