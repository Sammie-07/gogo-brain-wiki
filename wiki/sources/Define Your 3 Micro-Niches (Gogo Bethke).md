# Define Your 3 Micro-Niches (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo introduces the "micro-niche" concept — communities you already belong to outside of real estate where trust is pre-established. Her four: diabetes, Bulldogs, immigrant communities, and fishing. Homework: identify 3 of your own.

## Key Takeaways

- **Micro-niche = a community you already belong to where you've earned trust through shared experience — not through real estate.** The real estate business comes naturally because the relationship exists first.
- **Low-hanging fruit principle:** What comes easiest to you? Who is easiest to convert to a client because you have something in common? That's your micro-niche.
- **Gogo's four micro-niches:**
  1. **Diabetes community** — Son Koby is Type 1 diabetic. Active in Livingston County diabetes Facebook groups and in-person events. Trust built through shared parenting experience.
  2. **Bulldog lovers** — Large Facebook community around the breed. Love of the dog = instant connection with other Bulldog owners.
  3. **Immigrant community** — Romanian + Hungarian communities. Can explain real estate in English, Hungarian, or Romanian. Shared immigrant experience + language = deep trust.
  4. **Fishing community** — Dwayne fished every Wednesday and Saturday in tournaments for decades. 20-30 year relationships built completely outside real estate.
- **Other micro-niche examples:** Veterans, disability/handicap communities, sign language users, tennis club, biker's club, yoga studio regulars, ethnic/cultural communities.
- **You already have these relationships.** Through your lifetime you've developed skills and communities around those skills. Identifying them is the work — then you post into those groups when relevant content applies.
- **Homework:** Write down 3 micro-niches — communities where you belong and trust has already been earned.

## Quotes

> "What is that thing that comes easiest to you? What are those things that are your low-hanging fruit — the easiest for you to convert someone into a client because you have something in common with them?"

> "Look at things that have nothing to do with real estate but where you have access to a group of people — and they trust you because you've developed those relationships."
