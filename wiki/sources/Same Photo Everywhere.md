# Same Photo Everywhere

> Part of [[Gogo Bethke]]'s *Gogo's Social Media Challenge 2026* series. The brand consistency principle: one photo, used identically across every platform and touchpoint — the foundation of personal brand recognition.

## Key Takeaways

- **You are your logo.** Never use a generic logo as a profile image — you sell services, you are the brand. Personal brands (Tony Robbins, Gary Vee) are built around a face, not a symbol.
- **Same photo = brand recognition.** Best Buy, Starbucks, Target, McDonald's — identical logo from Romania to California. Your face must work the same way.
- **Every touchpoint:** Instagram, Facebook (personal + business), YouTube, LinkedIn, email signature, business cards, yard signs — the exact same photo.
- **Background tip:** White background works for email signatures; colorful background makes you pop in social media feeds.
- **When to update:** If your appearance changes significantly (hair, age), update immediately — but everywhere at once.
- **Three ways to get the photo:**
  1. Hire a photographer (~few hundred dollars, best result)
  2. Teenager + phone → edit with **Darkroom** or **Perfect365** apps
  3. AI avatar method — HeyGen (Laura to teach in an upcoming GSMC module)
- **Brand Tracker tie-in:** Plug your finalized profile photo link into the headshot field of your [[Personal Branding]] Brand Tracker doc.
- **Commitment embodied:** Gogo filmed this with a bad cold, having spilled tea on herself — she showed up because she made a commitment. A lived example of her own [[Goal Setting]] CPR principle.
