# Realtor's Secret to Extra Income — REMLO Program (Joe Vegas)

> Gogopreneur episode with Joe Vegas explaining the REMLO (Real Estate Mortgage Loan Originator) program — how licensed Realtors can also become mortgage loan officers and earn commissions on both sides of a transaction.

## Key Takeaways

- **REMLO:** Real Estate Mortgage Loan Originator. Allows a licensed Realtor to also act as the mortgage loan officer on buyer transactions — earning on both the real estate commission and the mortgage origination.
- **2022 HUD rule change:** HUD redefined the conflict of interest standard in late 2022. Real estate agents acting as loan originators were reclassified as NOT a conflict of interest — and actually deemed a benefit to consumers. This opened the door to REMLO at scale.
- **Federal bank charter advantage:** Unlike state-by-state mortgage licensing, REMLO is licensed through a federally chartered bank. One license covers all 50 states automatically — no need for individual state exams, CE, and fees.
- **Qualification:** Minimum 6 units or $1.8M on the buy side in the prior 12 months. Spirit of the law, not letter — exceptions for maternity leave or extenuating circumstances.
- **Time investment:** ~4 hours of training (comparable to annual CE). Not a full second career — designed to fit into an active agent's workflow.
- **Sign-up process:** Go to remoprogram.com → fill out interest form → Remo support team contacts within 24 hours.
- **Market context:** 71% of Realtors sold zero homes in 2024. 2024 was the worst real estate year since 1996. 0.04% of agents sold 40+ homes. REMLO as income diversification is directly relevant in a flat market.
- **"Full service agent":** Gogo's framing — an agent who can handle both the real estate and the financing in-house, end to end.
