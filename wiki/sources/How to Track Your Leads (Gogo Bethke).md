# How to Track Your Leads (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo walks through her lead tracker spreadsheet (2024–2027 template) — why she created it alongside her CRM, how each tab works, and how the tracker reveals weaknesses in your pipeline.

## Key Takeaways

- **Why a spreadsheet alongside a CRM:** CRMs show hundreds/thousands of leads at once and are overwhelming. The lead tracker answers one question: "Who do I need to talk to TODAY to close them?" Gogo uses both — CRM for automations, spreadsheet for daily decision-making.
- **Template name:** Lead Tracker 2024–2027 (download, make a copy, customize the lookup table)
- **Lead priority rating (A/B/C):**
  - **A** — Ready NOW. Buying, selling, or doing something in the next 30 days
  - **B** — 30–90 days. Ready-ish. A few things need to happen first (e.g., pre-approval, new job settling in)
  - **C** — Long shot. Unrealistic wishlist or timeline years away. Keep in the system; call periodically
- **Columns tracked:** Rating (A/B/C), Agent (dropdown from lookup table), Client name, Email, Address, Phone, Lead date, Client type (buyer/seller/renter/investor), Lead source, Property type, Approximate property value, Potential commission (auto-calculates at 3%), Added to CRM (yes/no), Notes, Agent split
- **Lead source lookup table** (fully customizable): CRM, Facebook, FSBO, Instagram, LinkedIn, Open houses, Past clients, Referrals, Sign calls, Sphere, TikTok, Website, YouTube, Zillow, Cold calling, Door knocking, Lender referrals, and more
- **Top of spreadsheet auto-totals:** Total volume in pipeline + total potential commission — "When I open my lead tracker I automatically know I have $1.2 million iron in the fire and I'm working on making $36,000"
- **Four tabs:**
  1. **Leads** — all incoming leads; source, rating, type, value
  2. **Active Listings** — leads that became listings; includes listing price, active date, open house dates
  3. **Pending** — accepted offers; tracks actual under-contract price vs. original estimate
  4. **Closed** — completed deals; graduation of the lead through the full pipeline
- **Summary tab (pie charts):** Auto-generates based on dropdown selections. Shows: % of business by lead source, % of business by agent, % of business value by source. Answers: "Where should I spend my time?"
- **Diagnostic use:** If leads are flowing but closings are low → objection handling or follow-up problem. If pending deals fall apart → execution or responsiveness problem. The tracker shows WHERE the leak is.
- **Every lead goes into the CRM**, regardless of source — tracker and CRM work in parallel, not instead of each other
