# What Top Agents Do Differently on Instagram (Month 5 LIVE)

> A [[GoGet'Em Community]] LIVE panel where [[Gogo Bethke]] interviews two [[eXp Realty]] agents — [[Allison]] on turning Instagram into GCI through a relocation niche, and [[Cat West]] on turning Instagram into revenue share through faith-based agent attraction — proving what works by the dollars, not the likes.

## Key Takeaways
- Gogo's frame: judge social media by "the proof in the US dollars in the bank account," not views or likes. Two money paths covered — GCI from transactions ([[Allison]]) and revenue share from agent attraction ([[Cat West]]).
- [[Allison]] (Staten Island → New Jersey relocation niche): last year her best solo year — 52 transactions, $26M volume, and ~$250K of that attributed directly to Instagram. Her bio literally reads "selling in Staten Island, moving to New Jersey" — speaking to one person, not everybody.
- Niche discipline: she only works clients who must move (their house is already in contract), avoids first-time buyers, and refuses to chase luxury because "relocation will never go out of style — everybody needs to move from one place to another."
- Geo-tagging strategy: Allison tags Staten Island on every post (even when filming in NJ) so content surfaces where her relocating homeowners actually are. "Vlog tours" are neighborhood tours — not other agents' listings — invented after agents pushed back on her filming their homes.
- Per-post dollar value: Gogo's math — $250K ÷ ~1,000 posts/year ≈ $250 per post. Every post carries a dollar value even if it doesn't directly close a client.
- Production system: films every Friday 9–2 with a $20/hr assistant holding the camera + a printed 14pt script (in her own words, not read robotically); pays an in-house full-time editor (found via Lena, her assistant of 3 years) instead of $1,500–$2,000/mo agencies that "can't do real estate because it's emotional and it has to be you."
- [[Cat West]] (Oviedo, FL; 8 years in real estate; EXP): overall organization of 120 agents, 28 sponsored personally, ~28 attracted via Instagram; ~$100K revenue share in the last 12 months; 555 agent leads in database; 10 coaching clients since late last year.
- Cat's brand = Christian top-producer coaching realtors "to scale without burnout." Bio structure: line 1 = who I am (top producer, Christian coach/speaker), line 2 = who I help (realtors scale without burnout), line 3 = lead magnet (3-day faith devotional for realtors). Profile treated as a "storefront."
- Agent avatar: someone selling $5M+/year, who loves Jesus, and is a mom of young kids. Cat wants the wrong-fit followers to leave so raving fans binge her content. Keyword/call-to-action is "partner."
- Attraction content that converts: farming/mailer reels (CMA box B-roll → lead capture with discount codes; uses Mailbox Power) and openly talking about her faith + what EXP has done for her. "People are buying you, not EXP" (echoed by Allison: "Nobody's buying EXP… they're buying you").
- Agent attraction is a long game: best agents take 6 months to 1.5 years of relationship-building before joining. Reels attract, stories convert ("it happens in the DMs"), lifestyle content proves she's a real person.
- Instagram mechanics: post at least 1×/day if using it as a business (Allison in a "growth phase" at 3×/day), stay in stories daily, and use DMs "like they're God." Instagram penalizes scheduled/copy-pasted posts — post manually in-app; use native "shared access" (business/creator + verified) to let a teammate post on your behalf without sharing your password.
- Tooling: both moved caption/script projects from ChatGPT to [[AI for Real Estate|Claude]] — Allison dumped all her videos/captions into a Claude project that writes long + short captions in her voice (no dashes, no emojis) and generates vlog scripts; Cat uses Claude for hook variations. Allison runs everything on [[GoHighLevel]] pipelines (lead → warm → appointment → contract) with automated email drips + [[ManyChat]]; Cat uses Stand Store, Boards, ManyChat (only for guides/mailers), and Calendly for a dedicated attraction booking link. Both use BoldTrail selectively for links.
- One-account rule: never run separate personal/professional accounts — promote everything (team brand, community) under your personal brand account and make the other accounts collaborators.
- Both stressed authenticity over copying, and evolving your brand as your life phases change ("your soul will tell you when you've outgrown an era"). Gogo's circle coaching reopening announced at the end.
