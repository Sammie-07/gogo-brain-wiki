# GoGet'Em Brand Audit Call — Amanda Richards and Angela Kane

> Live brand audit call reviewing two members: Amanda Richards (A&W Sells Homes, US) and Angela Kane (Miss EXP, Toronto, Canada). Covers QR code testing rule, brand tracker as central hub, two-button Linktree strategy, Canadian platform adaptations, and digital business cards.

## Key Takeaways

### Amanda Richards (A&W Sells Homes)

- **Brand name:** "A&W Sells Homes" — A is Amanda, W is her son.
- **QR code failure:** Amanda's QR code led to lofty.com homepage (her CRM) instead of her personal website — due to missing "https://" or "s" in the URL when the QR was generated.
- **Gogo's QR rule:** Before deploying ANY marketing material, test every link and QR code yourself. If you didn't test it, you don't know it's done. "Don't send me a link unless you actually tried it."
- **Test failure impact:** ~80% of people who scanned the QR got the wrong destination. Fix via ChatGPT or GoGet'Em tech support call.
- **Design feedback:** Redo all designs to one master template, then resize for each platform — one cohesive design that works everywhere.
- **Phone number on marketing:** Yes — use a smart number tied to CRM, not personal cell.
- **LinkedIn formatting:** Add dashes or separators between name sections for readability.
- **Brand tracker as central hub:** Contains every link, platform, and asset your business uses — one click to anything. Separate brand tracker per business if you operate multiple.

### Angela Kane (Miss EXP, Toronto/Clarington, Canada)

- **Instagram bio:** Good design, good brand name (Miss EXP). Overall positive — love the name, love the design.
- **Canada-specific:** No Zillow, Realtor.com, or Homes.com. Canadian equivalent is **House Sigma** — note this in the brand tracker.
- **Hashtags:** Use "Toronto" over local community name — more traffic, larger hashtag.
- **Linktree problem:** Generic "Book a Call" button with no context — anyone from the internet (including random people) could book a 30-minute call without knowing what type of call it was.
- **Fix — two separate call buttons:**
  1. "Partner with me at eXp" → agent attraction/eXp conversation calendar
  2. "Real estate inquiry" → buyer/seller consultation calendar
- **Why:** Filters who gets on your calendar. Real estate clients route differently than agent attraction prospects. Prevents time-wasters. "When I click that button, I need to know what I'm signing up for."
- **Location in bio:** Include Toronto (more traffic) even though she serves multiple communities. If you still want real estate leads, location stays in bio.
- **Blink Q digital card:** Angela hasn't used a physical card in 5 years. If you use a digital card, you don't need to add physical card to your brand tracker.
- **Speaking milestone:** Angela was scheduled to speak at eXp Regionals in Michigan and Orlando — noted as a brand-building moment.

## Universal Lessons

1. **Test everything before deploying** — QR codes, links, calendar buttons, every CTA
2. **Two call buttons on Linktree** — agent attraction AND real estate; don't mix them
3. **Brand tracker = central hub** — every link clickable, organized, accessible for VA
4. **Location matters** — if you want local leads, your location must be in your bio and hashtags
5. **Adapt Gogo's system to your market** — Canadian agents need Canadian platforms (House Sigma, etc.)
