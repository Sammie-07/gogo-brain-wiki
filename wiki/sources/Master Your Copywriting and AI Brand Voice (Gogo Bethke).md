# Master Your Copywriting & AI Brand Voice

> A GoGet'Em Community live training where [[Gogo Bethke]] hands the mic to her team — [[Kristy Waker]] (custom-GPT brand-voice setup), Fernie (email hook structure), and Lou (AI dos & don'ts) — to teach agents how to make ChatGPT write copy that sounds like them, not a robot.

## Key Takeaways
- Copywriting is just "the way we communicate" — its purpose is to **speak the language of your ideal client** so the right people find you; do the ideal-client and ideal-agent homework (with the provided ChatGPT prompts) first, because you can't write to someone you haven't defined.
- [[Kristy Waker]] built a shareable **custom GPT ("My AI Brand Voice")** on Gogo's account: paste 3–5 pieces of your own writing (emails, social posts, Zoom transcripts) via "Fast-Track Voice Setup," or use "Guided Interview" (~20 questions) if you have no content, and it outputs a full brand-voice profile.
- Paste that profile into ChatGPT → Settings → **Personalization → Custom Instructions**, and fill in the "About You" nickname/occupation fields, so every future chat writes in your voice.
- Gogo works AI by **voice, not typing** ("I hit the microphone, I talk to it like it's my best friend") — tell it the full story, then say "act like the best social media marketer on the face of the earth."
- **Specificity drives quality**: a closing post improves dramatically once you add numbers, multiple-offer detail, cash buyer, next-door lot, and rental strategy. Investors want to see the bottom line, not fluff — "convince with numbers, not with words."
- Every piece of copy needs a **single, explicit call to action** (DM, call, text + number) — one CTA, not five options, or people do nothing.
- Fernie's email framework: agents fail on **messaging, not consistency**. Structure every email **Hook → Problem → Story → Solution → CTA**. "The hook is everything — if they don't open it, nothing else matters."
- **Email is a shortcut, not a replacement** — you still must know what to say and who you're talking to; ChatGPT then drafts in seconds.
- **Pre-batch emails on a weekly theme calendar** so you never stare at a blank screen: Gogo's team sends one email/day (Mon social challenge, Wed [[REMLO]]/partner, Thu The Circle, Sat business tips, Sun mindset). Beginners start at **one email/week**, rotating their money-maker segments (buyers / sellers / investors / renters).
- Emails go out through **[[GoHighLevel]]** (~$97/mo, 30-day free trial) — Gogo dumps her whole sphere there for mass emailing rather than [[KVCore]], which she reserves for actual buyer/seller leads; dedicated email-scheduling training is planned for month 9 (September).
- Lou's prompt formula: give it **who I am / what I do / where / who I target / my tone / their pain points** — "write a warm, story-driven listing for a 3/2 bungalow in Logan Square for first-time buyers who care about walkability and natural light" beats "write a listing for a 3-bed house."
- **AI tells to strip out**: em dashes (the "AI signature"), buzzwords (leverage, optimize, unlock your potential, boost revenue), missing space before the first word, uniform paragraph lengths, "in today's fast-paced world," and "in conclusion." Humans mix sentence lengths, use contractions, add opinions/stories/local references — read it aloud and break where you run out of air.
- **Edit for readability & humanity**: shorten sentences, break paragraphs, add contractions, opinions, local references, and a CTA; screenshot Lou's checklists and upload them to ChatGPT's memory so it self-corrects.
- **Maintain your GPT over time** — Gogo's instructions from 2 years ago still listed old kid ages and a stale bio ("we did 3.6 billion"); revisit custom instructions every 1–2 years, and correct it live ("no, I'd never say that") so it keeps learning your voice.
- The **3-step AI workflow**: (1) give context, audience, tone, goal → (2) generate a rough draft → (3) human-edit for readability, voice, specificity, and CTA.
