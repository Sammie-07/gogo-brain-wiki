# Build a Personal Brand with Boundaries (Natalie Davison)

> GoGet'Em Community training. Natalie Davison is a brand communications expert, professional speaker, and realtor in Moncton, NB, Canada. In Gogo's eXp downline. Published in Entrepreneur.com, Financial Post, Social Media for Business for Dummies.

## Key Takeaways

- **Great brands are not for everybody.** They attract AND repel in equal measure. Generic = invisible.
- **Trust = openness + competence.** Most people sacrifice openness to look competent — that's the trap. Gogo's open-book style is why her audience trusts her.
- **Your brand is the sum of every interaction** — how you answer the phone, how you show up on camera, how your kids behave in public.
- **The Character Diamond** (from Hollywood screenwriting professor David Freeman): 3–5 traits that define your character. Fewer than 3 = boring. More than 5 = confusing.
  - **North Star** — what you stand for / your claiming competence (Gogo: teaching entrepreneurs how to build wealth)
  - **Counter Star** — your kryptonite / the backstory that makes your north star believable (Gogo: came to US as a nanny with nothing)
  - **Non-Negotiable** — the passion you cannot compromise on; the anchor (Gogo: everything she does builds leverage)
  - **Quirk** — the flaw or unusual trait that makes you relatable and stops the scroll (Gogo: shows up no matter what — no makeup, pajamas, any time)
- A cliché diamond (all the same traits as everyone in your industry) makes you invisible. Even one unique point is enough to draw people in.
- Boundaries = you decide what to share. Natalie shares her teenage kids; not everyone will or should.
- Content categories flow naturally from the diamond — no rules on posting frequency, just consistency with who you are.
