# The 5 Core Elements of High-Converting Copy (Gogo Bethke)

> Second video in the GSMC 2026 copywriting series. Gogo delivers a universal 5-part framework that works for social media posts, emails, website copy, and digital product pages — same structure every time.

## Key Takeaways

- **The 5-element framework applies to everything:** social media captions, emails, website copy, digital product pages, seller/buyer/agent attraction content
- **The framework:**

| # | Element | What it does | Example |
|---|---------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | **Hook** | Grabs attention; speaks directly to the target person | *"Could you use some more million-dollar buyers?"* |
| 2 | **Problem** | Names the problem they have — one sentence, bluntly | *"But when I go to your website, I see $52,000 listings."* |
| 3 | **Amplify Pain** | What happens if they DON'T fix it? Make the cost of inaction real | *"You're working hard to attract luxury buyers but losing them the moment they land on your site."* |
| 4 | **Solution** | What's the fix? Keep it clear and specific | *"Match your website's back-end filter to only show million-dollar homes."* |
| 5 | **Call to Action** | Tell them exactly what to do next | *"Join my live Tuesday at 2pm — I'll show you how."* |

- **Universal application:** Flip the example for sellers, buyers, or agent attraction — same 5 steps, different audience
- **Rules:**
  - Never present a problem without a solution
  - Never present a solution without first naming the problem
  - Never post anything without a call to action
- **Memorization tip:** Write the 5 steps and staple them somewhere. After a few reps, it becomes automatic — "you're going to pretty much just know what you need to do for the rest of your life"

## Quotes

> "As long as your copywriting answers all of these questions, you're good to go."

> "Don't present me a problem without a solution. Don't present me a solution without the problem. And don't post something without a call to action at the end. It's that simple."

> "Hook, problem, amplify pain, solution, call to action. That's it. Five things. Go create copywriting."
