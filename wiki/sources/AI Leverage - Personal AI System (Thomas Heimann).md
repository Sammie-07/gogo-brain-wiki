# AI Leverage — Build Your Personal AI System

> [[Thomas Heimann]] trains the [[GoGet'Em Community]] on building a foundation of AI "intelligence briefs" and client avatars, then layering [[Projects]] and multi-agent [[Agentic AI]] on top to turn yourself into the AI authority in your market.

## Key Takeaways
- **Three eras of AI**: (1) the AI chatbot ([[ChatGPT]], [[Claude]]) where most people still live; (2) custom agents / workflows ([[Claude Code|Claude Cowork]], custom GPTs) that do guided work on a schedule; (3) [[Agentic AI]] — multiple specialized agents that make decisions and hand off work to each other autonomously. Heimann's book on era three is titled *Twice Ahead*.
- AI is compressing decades of change into months: the internet took ~20 years to reshape business, mobile ~10, AI is doing it "in months." Heimann went from 30 min/day of AI learning a year ago to 3–5 hrs/day now; "the learning compounds."
- **Garbage in, garbage out**: generic prompts produce obviously-AI output. The fix is *context*, delivered through foundational documents.
- **Personal Intelligence Brief** — who you are: background, values, communication style, personal story, strengths/weaknesses, personality profiles. Heimann built his by dictating hours of his life story on walks (Plaud recorder), transcribing it, and having AI distill it. His grew from 3 pages to ~30; he re-versions it every ~3 months (currently v5).
- **Business Intelligence Brief** — one per business (he keeps separate briefs for investment properties, title companies, and the AI business) so context doesn't become a "mishmash."
- **Client Avatar** — a hyper-detailed persona of the ideal customer (name, history, financials, fears, desires, aspirations). Build a *separate* avatar for buyers vs. sellers, and even per-listing. The most important fields are the fears/desires — "the psychological buttons you push in your messaging."
- **Avatar-building workflow (3 steps)**: (1) market intelligence report for the geographic area (3-yr trends), (2) ideal-customer identification, (3) synthesize both into the avatar. Live demo built one for agent Nancy — a 67-yr-old empty-nester seller "Mary Ellen Whitaker"; Nancy said it "nailed it."
- **Projects** (in both ChatGPT and Claude) segregate memory — a project is a blank slate that only sees its uploaded source files and its own chats, so businesses/health/personal data never co-mingle. Upload your briefs + avatar into a project to give it context.
- **Markdown over Word for machines**: always download briefs in *both* .docx (for humans) and .md (for AI) — markdown uses far fewer tokens and is easier for AI to parse.
- **Model selection / cost**: [[DeepSeek]] V4 (Pro/Flash) is claimed at ~Claude Opus level for ~1% of the cost. Reserve Opus 4.7 for high-level consulting; use cheap models for the 99% of routine agent work. Heimann runs ~6 agents (incl. Hermes and the Meet Sarah system) on DeepSeek — **$142 for all of May** (started with a $20 deposit). He also invested $30K in local hardware (~2 TB memory) to run local LLMs with no API fees.
- **Tool stack mentioned**: [[Claude Code|Claude Cowork]] (workflow automation), Hermes Agent + OpenClaw (free/self-hosted agentic AI you can run locally and talk to via Telegram/WhatsApp), [[HeyGen]] (video avatars), [[ElevenLabs]] (voice cloning / voice AI), [[Grok]] (Grok Voice Fast 1.0), Whisper Flow (dictation to agents), [[High Level]] (AI voice/receptionist + inside-sales agents).
- **Two products pitched**: **Apex AI Coach** (apexaicoach.com) — a bespoke AI coach + "board of advisors" built from a 1.5–2 hr interview, 8 personality profiles, and the intelligence briefs of 3–4 business heroes you choose (Heimann chose Elon Musk, Peter Thiel, Richard Branson, Tony Hsieh); modeled on Napoleon Hill's *Think and Grow Rich* imaginary board. **Meet Sarah** (meetsarah.io) — a real-estate AI tool, free for 90 days then $19.95/mo. GoGet'Em members get 30% off Apex forever with code **GGM**.
- **Closing charge**: don't sit on the sidelines — experiment, fail, iterate, and "become the AI authority in your market"; the analogy is being the only agent with a website in 2000.
