# Create Your "Local Expert" Tagline (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo shares her own tagline ("Go with Gogo") and encourages agents to create one — something memorable, fun, and repeatable that ends every reel, story, call, and stage appearance. Optional but recommended.

## Key Takeaways

- **Gogo's tagline:** *"Go with Gogo"* — on her business card, website, yard signs, everywhere. Simple, memorable, rhymes with her name.
- **Other example:** A Michigan agent's tagline is "[Name] Household Name" — "[Agent's Name], your household name."
- **A tagline should:** Rhyme or be catchy, be instantly memorable, put a smile on someone's face, and function as a consistent sign-off.
- **Uses:** End every reel, end every story, close every stage appearance, sign off every call. Repetition = brand recall.
- **A tagline is NOT required.** If you have a difficult-to-pronounce name and nothing makes sense, skip it — you can be very successful without one. But if you can come up with something fun, it adds personality and makes your brand stickier.
- **It took Gogo months to land on hers.** Don't rush it. But once you find it, use it everywhere and never stop.
- **Self-deprecating tone:** She calls it "very hard" and "very original" — her point is that simplicity IS the strategy. Don't overthink it.

## Quotes

> "'Go with Gogo' — that was my tagline. It was on my business card, my website, my yard signs. Literally everywhere you can think of."

> "Is there a tagline that you could have for your business that you could end Reels with and end Stories with and drop the mic from stages with?"

> "You can be very successful without one. But if we could come up with a fun tagline — it just makes it a little more fun and hip and easygoing."
