# Assumable Mortgages & AssumeList (Nora Simpson & Mike Lorino)

> A #teamgogo weekly call hosted by [[Gogo Bethke]]: the main segment is **[[AssumeList]]** (an assumable-mortgage marketplace) presented by Nora Simpson (Head of Education) and Mike Lorino (CEO). Opens with a [[Jason Dupree]] REMLO/mortgage minute and includes [[Alexis Carrera]]'s "skin in the game" marketing-services-agreement segment and Gogo's media-release form.

## Key Takeaways

**Assumable mortgages / [[AssumeList]]:**
- **What it is:** a buyer takes over (assumes) the seller's existing low-rate loan — rate, balance, and servicer stay the same. Only **FHA, VA, and USDA** loans are assumable. Many assumable loans sit at 2.4–3% (issued 2020–21).
- **Who can assume:** FHA — any creditworthy buyer (needn't qualify for an FHA program) but must live in the home the first 12 months. VA — even non-VA buyers/investors can assume if the veteran leaves the tied entitlement behind (AssumeList has a VA entitlement calculator). USDA — more restrictive: the assuming buyer must meet USDA rules, notably income **≤115% of local median household income**.
- **The equity gap → second mortgage:** because prices rose and principal has been paid down, the buyer covers the gap between price and remaining balance. **Buyer generally still needs ~10% down**; the rest can be financed via a **second mortgage** (AssumeList has a preferred lender with the lowest second-mortgage rates + a calculator).
- **Savings today:** ~$500–$1,000/month now (was $2,000–$3,000 a few years ago); some pursue assumptions to save even ~$100/month.
- **The platform:** aggregates **44,000+ assumable listings across 55 MLSs / 26 states** (27th launching), incl. off-market via skip-trace/mailers; only shows homes with assumable loans **below 5%**.
- **eXp enterprise license:** [[eXp Realty|Leo Pareja]] bought an enterprise license so **every eXp agent gets free access** (otherwise ~$80/mo); agents issue **buyer sub-accounts** and see all client activity. Access: **AssumeList.com/exp** → "I already have a code" → eXp code **BZEUEVKM** → register with business + eXp email. Codes rotate ~every 2 days to push agents to a live training.
- **Auto-listing for agents:** a property in a covered area (e.g., South Florida / Beaches / Miami MLS) is **auto-added once listed on the MLS** — only if its loan is assumable and below 5%.
- **Fees (paid by buyer/seller, NOT the agent):** a **$2,300 flat TC fee** = **$200 upfront setup** + $2,100 at clear-to-close; **no charge beyond the $200 if the assumption isn't approved**. Their TC team claims "never a turndown, never longer than 60 days."
- **Why banks resist:** servicers must legally allow FHA/VA assumptions but keep those teams hidden/understaffed (they'd rather write a new ~6.25% loan than transfer a 2.7% one). AssumeList navigates this; DIY consumers usually fail. All liability transfers seller→buyer in a federally regulated process.
- **Marketing angle:** agents promoting assumables reportedly **double their business in ~6 months**. Gogo's tactic: a green-screen video in front of a listing photo (address whited out) — "What if I could get you this house for 2.43%?" — to drive inbound leads. Integrates with **Follow Up Boss**; **BoldTrail** API in progress. Daily Q&A Mon–Fri 11am ET (homes.assumelist.com/learn).

**[[Jason Dupree]] REMLO / mortgage minute:**
- ~75% of current purchases are first-time buyers (~54%) + investors (~23%). REMLO agents earn on the transaction, the loan, AND a later refinance.
- Highlighted a **100%-forgivable down-payment-assistance grant** (forgivable after 6 payments / 7 months; FHA-based; set high ~7.25% to harvest credits, then FHA-streamline down to ~6.25%; income limit ~150% AMI) plus investor programs (DSCR, bank-statement loans, HELOC-to-invest, LLC structuring). Context: **CNBC — mortgage rates at a 2-year high**.

**[[Alexis Carrera]] — "skin in the game" marketing services agreement:**
- After eating ~$1,200 in marketing when a seller fired her, she built a **Marketing Services Agreement** so clients bear marketing-cost risk. **Option A:** agent advances costs, seller **reimburses documented third-party marketing expenses** on cancellation/withdrawal/termination (optionally **escrowed with title** up front). **Option B:** client **pays vendors directly** (agent may offer a commission credit at closing).
- Lessons: always specify **a dollar amount AND a payment deadline**; clear it with your broker/state. Framed by Nora as the "neuroscience of sales" — a commitment device that signals professionalism. Commission ≠ marketing: "You get the Porsche, you pay for the Porsche" — marketing is an out-of-pocket expense separate from the listing fee.
