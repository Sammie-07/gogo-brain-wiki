# Commercial Edge Training for Residential Agents (Bridget)

> Bridget — 30+ year real estate veteran with a lending background, creator of Commercial Edge Training — teaches GoGet'Em members how residential agents can break into commercial real estate and serve clients more fully.

## Key Takeaways

- **Bridget's background:** 30+ years in real estate. Started in lending — top producer in both residential and commercial loans. Transitioned naturally to commercial because of her numbers/finance fluency.
- **Commercial Edge Training:** A course and mentorship program specifically for residential agents learning commercial real estate.
- **Why learn commercial:**
  1. Clients ask. Affluent residential clients often own commercial properties and ask their trusted agent first — most agents say no and lose the deal and the relationship.
  2. Be a full-service advisor: help clients with portfolios, analyze deals, find better-performing assets, execute 1031 exchanges into higher cap-rate properties.
  3. Multiple income streams: commercial deals alongside residential = additional revenue without replacing existing business.
- **The commercial landscape is changing:** Historically dominated by older white men. Many are retiring. More women and diverse agents entering now. "It's really a great time."
- **Same license:** Residential and commercial agents hold the same license in most states. Knowledge and mentorship are the only barriers.
- **Don't do it alone:** Commercial requires a mentor until comfortable. Wrong advice in a commercial deal causes serious financial and legal harm to clients.
- **1031 exchanges:** Key tool for commercial clients. Sell an underperforming asset, reinvest in a higher-cap-rate property, defer taxes. Powerful wealth-building for clients AND a reason for clients to stay with you.
- **Cap rates:** Commercial's primary valuation metric. Understanding cap rates lets you advise clients on asset performance vs. alternatives.
