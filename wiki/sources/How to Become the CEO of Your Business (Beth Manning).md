# How to Become the CEO of Your Business (Beth Manning)

> GoGet'Em Community session with Beth Manning on thinking and operating like a CEO. Covers mental mind mapping, specificity in numbers, financial tracking, and the CEO muscle.

## Key Takeaways

- **Start with the end in mind / Mental mind mapping:** Visualize the exact outcome before taking action. Not vague ("I want to do more deals") but precise ("I want to close 24 deals at an average price of $350k").
- **Specificity in numbers:** Beth's drill — same agent, different outcomes depending on avg sale price:
  - 24 deals @ $200k = very different income than
  - 24 deals @ $300k or $400k or $500k
  - Most agents never consciously choose their avg sale price. CEOs do.
- **Her first year:** $2.7M in sales. Got to $10M primarily by doubling her average sale price — not by doubling transaction count.
- **QuickBooks over Excel:** Gogo's reaction was "oh hell no" to using Excel for financials. QuickBooks (or equivalent) mandatory for any agent running a real business. Track every dollar.
- **CEO muscle:** Like any muscle, it atrophies without use. The CEO muscle is built by doing the boring, important things consistently — financials, planning, systems review — even when they're not exciting.
- **SMART goals reminder:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Goals without a number and a deadline are wishes.

## Quotes

> "Most agents never decide what their average sale price will be. A CEO decides."

> "Gogo said 'oh hell no' to Excel. QuickBooks. Non-negotiable."

> "The CEO muscle is built doing the boring important things everyone else avoids."
