# Equipment Used for Social Media (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo's full equipment walkthrough for social media content creation — lights, phone stand, microphones. Total cost ~$200. Core message: don't overthink it, your phone is the main thing. Part of GoGo Social Media Challenge 2026.

## Key Takeaways

- **Total cost:** ~$200 or less, purchased over time. "It's really not a whole lot of things."
- **Primary tool:** Your phone. That's it. "Have a phone. That's it. It's really that simple."
- **Item 1 — Computer ring light:** Plugged into computer USB. Used daily. Ideal for at-desk recording, Loom, Zoom, podcast-style content.
- **Item 2 — Two large panel lights:** Used only for big lives/podcasts where she needs light from every angle with no shadow. Match both panels to the same number setting so they project identical color temperature (warm/cool). They cause headaches with extended use — not for daily filming.
- **Item 3 — Collapsible phone stand:** Gogo's favorite. Folds down small, extends to ~6 feet. Handles both horizontal and vertical video. Has a Bluetooth remote to start/stop recording (Gogo never uses it — too much to charge and sync). She just starts the video and trims the beginning later. Travel-friendly (though it looks "funky").
- **Item 4 — Clip-on desk light:** For sitting in a chair in darker areas. Plugs in, adjustable brightness and warm/cool temperature. Can tilt down so you look up slightly at camera. Phone slots in front so you're shooting into the light.
- **Microphone:** Rode (she says "Rodeo"). Should use one outdoors or in noisy environments. Gogo often skips it at home — "I'm not that pre-planning." All links in Gogo's Amazon storefront.
- **Amazon store:** All equipment linked there — no need to search. Link in GSMC lesson description.
- **Top recommendation if budget is tight:** The collapsible phone stand (travel-friendly, versatile) + computer ring light.

## Quotes

> "Don't overthink it. Shoot video. Bye."

> "Have a good phone and that's about it."

> "I rather just start a video and then delete that little portion at the beginning while I started it than to figure out how to charge it."
