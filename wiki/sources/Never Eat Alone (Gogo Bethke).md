# Never Eat Alone (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo introduces the "Never Eat Alone" concept — learned from her first real estate coach Sarah ($1,500/month) — with a 5-day weekly schedule for in-person coffees/meals structured by audience type: experts (Mon/Wed), anyone (Tue/Thu), agents (Fri).

## Key Takeaways

- **Source of the concept:** Gogo's first real estate coach Sarah, a "four-foot-nine firecracker." Cost: $1,500/month — more than her mortgage at the time.
- **The philosophy:** "Every commission is handed to you by another human. Every money that comes to you, money exchanges hands between humans." You are in the human capital business. Never eat alone.
- **The 5-day weekly coffee/meal schedule:**
  | Day | Who | Purpose |
  |-----|-----|---------|
  | Monday | An expert | Learn, absorb knowledge, get smarter |
  | Tuesday | Anyone | Neighbor, cousin, yoga friend, grocery store acquaintance → turn into client |
  | Wednesday | An expert | Same as Monday — learning day |
  | Thursday | Anyone | Same as Tuesday — relationship building |
  | Friday | An agent | Agent attraction — face to face, heart to heart |
- **Budget-scaled format:**
  - No money: coffee (cheapest)
  - Making money: lunch
  - Printing money + need tax write-offs: dinner and drinks
  - Whoever invites, pays.
- **In marketing, who knows you matters more than who you know.** Quantity of people you put yourself in front of = quality of your pipeline.
- **"When God pops someone in your head, call them."** Gogo treats intuitive nudges as signals. Story: she thought of a past client, called them, and they said "I was planning to call you today — we're having a baby and need a bigger house." This happens consistently.
- **Never scroll alone at a restaurant.** Fill your calendar with people. Every meal is an opportunity.

## Quotes

> "Every commission is handed to you by another human. Every money that comes to you, money exchanges hands between humans. So you never eat alone."

> "In marketing — it is important to know people, but it's more important who knows you."

> "When God pops someone in my head, I look at that as a sign. Call that person."

> "If you're living in the same town and I'm going to catch you at the bar all by yourself just eating and scrolling on your phone, I don't even know what I'm going to do to you."
