# My Thoughts on Emails (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo makes the case for email as a non-negotiable marketing channel: why you don't own your social media followers, why your inbox is a gold mine you don't know you have, and the math behind why one email a day is worth sending even to a small list.

## Key Takeaways

- **You don't own your social media followers.** Instagram, Facebook, TikTok — any platform can shut your account down tomorrow. You can have 100,000 followers and know none of them. If the platform disappears, your business disappears with it.
- **Email = owned audience.** An email list is yours. Platform goes down → you email your list → problem solved. They know who you are, you know who they are.
- **You already have a gold mine.** Anyone who ever emailed you or you emailed them is already in your email database. Most people don't realize they already have 3,000–5,000 addresses sitting in their inbox. Real estate agents who've been in the business long typically have far more.
- **Don't put all your eggs in one basket.** Social media + email = two separate pipelines. Lose one, you still have the other.

### The Email Math

| Step | Number |
|------|--------|
| Emails sent in 2025 | 6.5 million |
| Open rate | ~24% |
| Emails opened | 1.6 million |
| If 10% take action | 162,500 opportunities |
| Value at $1/person | $162,500/year |
| Per-email value | ~$445 |
| If they buy a house instead | $10,000+ per opportunity |

> "Would you send an email for $445 if they only buy $1 worth from you? That's what I mean."

- The math only improves if your list grows or your offer is worth more than $1.

### Building and Feeding the Database
- **Lead magnets:** Free templates, trackers, prompt packages → email required to download
- **Open houses:** Sign-in sheet → name + email → plugged into CRM
- **Existing inbox:** Sort and assign anyone who ever emailed you (buyer / seller / sphere)
- **Continuous growth:** Keep feeding. The list grows slowly but surely.
- **Cap at one email/day.** More than that becomes noise. Start with one per week if needed, then scale up.

### Use Cases for Email
- Events and community invitations
- New listings (million-dollar listing announcement)
- Broker opens (invite local agents)
- Book promotions or digital products
- Pitching a community, coaching program, or eXp

### Track Everything
Gogo tracks open rate, volume sent, and click-through. Tracking tells you: Is my subject line working? Am I landing in spam? Is the content resonating? Without tracking, you don't know what to fix.

**Note on metrics:** This video states ~24% open rate and 6.5M emails in 2025. Another source (I Send One Email a Day / GoGet'Em training) cites 43.68% open rate and 4.1M YTD. These likely reflect different time periods or measurement methods; both are Gogo's own reported figures.
