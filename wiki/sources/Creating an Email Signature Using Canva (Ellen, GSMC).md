# Creating an Email Signature Using Canva (Ellen, GSMC)

> Ellen (Team Gogo VA) shows two ways to create a branded email signature in Canva: resizing an existing brand cover, or using Canva's built-in email signature templates. Part 1 of a two-video sequence; Part 2 (Gmail setup) is covered by Kristy Waker.

## Key Takeaways

- **Two paths in Canva:**
  1. **Resize existing cover** — open your social media cover → three dots → Resize page → search "email signature" → rearrange elements → done. Keeps brand consistent across all platforms.
  2. **Use a Canva template** — Templates → search "email signature" → pick one → swap logo, colors, headshot, info → download.

- **Download format:** Always PNG. (PDF won't work in Gmail.)

- **What to include:** Name, job title, email address, phone number, website. Everything that leads people to you.

- **Critical limitation:** A Canva-exported PNG image has **no clickable links**. It looks great but nothing is tappable. The companion video (Kristy Waker) covers how to add clickable text above the image and how to use WiseStamp for a fully-linked signature.

- **Team Gogo's own signatures** are simple and clean — name, title, email, no clutter.

## Quotes

> "I must warn you guys that Canva — when you create the design here and you download it as a PNG — you won't have clickable links in your email signature."
