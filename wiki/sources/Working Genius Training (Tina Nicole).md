# Working Genius Training (Tina Nicole)

> Tina Nicole presents Patrick Lencioni's Working Genius framework to Gogo's team and GoGet'Em community. Covers the six letter-code types, three stages of work, and a live team map applied to Gogo's actual staff.

## Key Takeaways

### The Six Working Geniuses (WIDGET)

- **W — Wonder:** Asks "why?" and "what if?" Ideates at the 30,000-foot level. Loves questioning assumptions and identifying possibilities. Frustration: being forced to execute without context.
- **I — Invention:** Creates new solutions, systems, ideas. The "inventor." Loves innovation; crushed by constraint. Can be a systems engineer or a creative — both generate new ways of doing things.
- **D — Discernment:** Uses gut instinct to evaluate ideas without needing all the information. The "Debbie Downer" who pokes holes in plans — but this is a critical filter function. Craves being trusted; hates being told "prove it."
- **G — Galvanizing:** Rallies people around ideas, pushes into action. The "pushy douchebag" (Tina's term). Craves reaction; is crushed by apathy. Gogo is a G — she wants people to take action now.
- **E — Enablement:** Provides support, responds to others' needs, says "what do you need from me?" Craves appreciation; is crushed by being overlooked. These people hold teams together but can burn out if not recognized.
- **T — Tenacity:** Finishes things. Drives tasks across the finish line. Loves completion; hates leaving things unfinished. The finisher every team needs.

### Three Stages of Work

1. **Ideation** (Wonder + Invention) — generating possibilities
2. **Activation** (Discernment + Galvanizing) — evaluating and launching
3. **Implementation** (Enablement + Tenacity) — executing and completing

Healthy teams need representation across all three stages.

### Gogo's Team Map

- **Gogo:** GT (Galvanizing + Discernment) — rallies people, follows gut, pushes action immediately
- **Gogo is the only G on her team** — which explains why she sometimes feels like the only one pushing forward
- **Application:** Knowing team members' Working Genius helps assign tasks to the right person, reduces friction, and explains why certain people feel drained by certain tasks

### Practical Uses

- **Hiring:** Identify which genius types are missing from your current team before posting a job
- **Task assignment:** Stop assigning Tenacity tasks to Wonder people — they'll start but never finish
- **Retention:** When people work in their genius zone, they bring more energy. When stuck in their frustration zone, they quit.
