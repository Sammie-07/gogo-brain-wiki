# How To Set Up A Real Estate Agent Business (Toby Mathis)

> Toby Mathis, Esq. on the proper legal and tax structure for real estate agents — how to move from sole proprietor to protected, tax-advantaged business entity.

## Key Takeaways

- Most real estate agents operate as **sole proprietors** by default — this is the worst structure for both taxes and legal protection.
- The ideal structure for most agents: **S Corporation** (or LLC taxed as S Corp) — splits income into salary + distribution, reducing self-employment tax.
- Self-employment tax (15.3%) is the biggest tax most agents don't think about — S Corp structure eliminates SE tax on the distribution portion.
- Steps to set up:
  1. Form an LLC (or corporation)
  2. Elect S Corp status with the IRS
  3. Pay yourself a reasonable salary
  4. Take remaining profit as distribution (not subject to SE tax)
- **Deductions available to a business entity**: home office, vehicle, equipment, health insurance, retirement contributions (SEP IRA, Solo 401K)
- Real estate agents with over ~$40K net profit benefit most from S Corp structure.
