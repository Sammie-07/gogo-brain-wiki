# GoGet'Em Training — Camera Confidence and Social Media Posting Workshop

> February community training focused on camera confidence and live hands-on social media posting. Gogo presented 10 on-camera principles (she noted she herself makes several of these mistakes and is working on them), then ran a live workshop where members made their first real post during the call. Covered Instagram Stories captions + links workflow and CRM integration.

## Key Takeaways

### 10 Steps to Camera Confidence

1. **Direct eye contact:** Look directly into the camera lens — not at yourself in the preview screen. On the receiving end, it creates the feeling of direct one-on-one conversation.
2. **Make it about them, not you:** The most common mistake. Reframe from "I'm so excited" → to the audience's problem. "If you keep posting on social media but buyers and sellers aren't showing up in your CRM, I'm going to show you Tuesday how to fix that."
3. **Serve, don't perform:** Content from a place of service feels different from content aimed at looking good. The audience feels the difference. When you focus on serving instead of performing, confidence naturally increases.
4. **Stories are temporary:** "It's only 24 hours. Who cares?" Remove the psychological weight. Lower the stakes internally, show up anyway.
5–10. Additional steps covered live (batching content, lighting, framing, removing background distractions, starting before you're ready).

### Live Posting Workshop

- **Facebook Business Page:** Members practiced making a live post on their Facebook business page during the call. Key: regular post, not an ad.

### Instagram Stories — Captions + Links Workflow

**To add captions and a clickable link to a Story:**
1. Shoot your video (Story format)
2. Swipe up after recording → select the **Captions filter**
3. Instagram listens to the audio and auto-types your words on screen
4. Swipe up again → add your **link**
5. Result: Story has both captions (accessible, stops-the-scroll) and a clickable link

**If you forgot captions on an already-recorded Story:**
1. Download the video to camera roll
2. Re-upload it as a new Story
3. Add captions filter + re-add the link
4. Note: embedded links don't carry over when downloaded — must re-link manually
