# From Discovery to Deal — Website Copywriting for Social Media (GGTC)

> Guest training in the GoGet'Em Community by an SEO specialist (20+ years experience; seocoaching.com/gogo). Core premise: social media gets the click; your website closes the deal. Covers message consistency, the 3-second rule, niche-specific pages, EEAT, and a 3-question website audit.

## Key Takeaways

### The Social → Website Journey
Social media and your website are two parts of one machine — not separate:
- **Social:** Discovery. Gets attention and the click.
- **Website:** Conversion. Closes the deal — a call, a form, a reply, a purchase.

A well-optimized website page is written once and compounds ROI for years. It attracts both social referral traffic and organic search traffic simultaneously.

### Message Consistency (Critical)
**Startled or confused people leave and never come back.** If your social post says one thing and your website says something else, you've broken the journey.

Rule: **The landing page must match the energy, location, and promise of the social content that sent them there.**

| Social post says | Website page should say |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| "I just helped a client sell in 8 days above asking price" | "Thinking of selling in [City]? Here's how I consistently sell fast and over asking price" |
| "5 things every first-time buyer needs to know" | "Are you buying your first home in [City]? Here is what your agent should have told you already" |
| "I've helped 300+ Nashville families buy and sell — here's my story" | Page with same numbers, same city, same personal energy |

Never link to your homepage generically. Link to the **most relevant matching page.**

### The 3-Second Rule
When someone lands on your website, you have **3 seconds** to make clear:
1. Who you help
2. Where you help them
3. What they need to do next

*"Excellence in real estate"* — too vague. No one knows who you serve or where.

*"Helping first-time buyers in Nashville navigate their first home purchase"* — specific, immediately understandable.

### Niche-Specific Pages
Build dedicated pages for specific client types and problems:
- First-time buyers
- Relocation clients
- Probate sales
- Condo investors

Each page captures its own search traffic AND makes the visitor feel the page was built just for them. The more specific, the more trust.

### EEAT — Show, Don't Just Tell
Google (and clients) rank content by: **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness.**

Don't say "I'm the best agent." Show it:
- *Experience:* "Over 10 years helping clients in [area]. Here's an example of how I solved [specific problem]."
- *Authority:* Industry credentials, book authorship, published content.
- *Trust:* Specific numbers, named results, transparency about process.

People do business with people they **know, like, and trust.** Trust is the most important. Experience + expertise + authority = trust.

### CTAs — Specific Beats Generic
"Contact us" and "Reach out for more info" = lost conversions. Confused people leave.

**Instead:** *"Book a free 15-minute neighborhood strategy call."*

The clearer the action and the more specific the outcome, the higher the conversion rate.

### The 3-Question Website Audit
Fix these three things and you've outperformed most realtors:
1. **Headline:** Does your homepage headline clearly state who you help and what they should do? If not, rewrite today.
2. **Social links:** Does every link from social go to a matching, relevant page — not just your homepage?
3. **CTA:** Is your call to action a specific action with a clear, expected outcome?
