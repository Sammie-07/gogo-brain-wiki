# How to Host Open Houses

> Part of [[Gogo Bethke]]'s *Gogo's Social Media Challenge 2026* series. Full open house SOP — from MLS listing deadlines through post-event follow-up — with the framing that open houses are the bridge between social media's broad reach and in-person conversion.

## Key Takeaways

- **Open houses bridge social and in-person.** Social media casts a wide net; the open house is where you narrow it to people actively on the market and convert them face-to-face.
- **Gogo is retired from active selling.** She no longer holds a license. Her personal peak production was 40–50 transactions/year. Now she teaches and leads Team Go + the [[GoGet'Em Community]].
- **Combined Team Go + GoGet'Em Community volume: 10,000+ transactions/year.**
- **MLS by Thursday.** Zillow, Realtor.com, Trulia take 2–3 days to update. Miss Thursday = miss the weekend.
- **Cast the net everywhere.** Every social platform + email database for open house marketing.
- **Door knock 20–50 homes** around the property with a lender and/or another agent. Script: invite neighbors to "control who moves in next door."
- **40 directional signs + helium balloons.** Two 7-foot standing banners with your photo — impossible to miss.
- **Study the listing cold.** Know everything: roof age, septic, water softener, what conveys. If you don't know, you're not the expert.
- **Prep sellers:** pristine home, hide valuables, remove personal photos (safety for single-occupant households).
- **Bake cookies** (or have sellers make bacon before leaving) — ambiance and smell make it feel like a home.
- **Stand at the door** — Louis Vuitton–level greeting. Never sit on the couch on your phone.
- **Business card trick:** "I ran out" → ask for their number, text them yours. You control follow-up; giving a card is handing over control.
- **Sign-in for seller security** — if they won't sign in, they don't tour. Protects you and the seller.
- **Security sweep before leaving:** check and lock every window on the main floor (people unlock windows during tours to return at night). Lock all doors, turn off lights and gas. Call seller to confirm home is secure.
- **Follow up before leaving the driveway.** Selfie-style video texts, by first name, to every attendee: number of couples today, two are very interested, "if this is your home, act tonight." Clear call to action. Offer to represent unrepresented buyers.
- **Follow up again multiple times the following week.** Update on contract status; pivot to similar listings.
- **"If you want what the top 1% has, you have to do what 99% is not willing to do."**
