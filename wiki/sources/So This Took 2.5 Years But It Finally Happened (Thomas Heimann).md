# So This Took 2.5 Years But It Finally Happened (Thomas Heimann)

> Gogopreneur content featuring Thomas Heimann — owner of Realty Partners brokerage — joining Team Go after 2.5 years of relationship. Illustrates eXp's economic advantages over traditional brokerage ownership and the long-game of recruiting.

## Key Takeaways

- **Thomas Heimann:** Owner of Realty Partners brokerage. Joined Team Go after a 2.5-year cultivation relationship. Example of long-game recruiting patience.
- **Traditional brokerage economics:**
  - Fewer than 30 agents = losing money
  - 35–40 agents needed just to break even
  - Overhead, E&O, office, staff all eat into margins before the owner earns anything
- **eXp economics:** Profitable from day one. No physical office overhead. Rev share kicks in immediately when you sponsor an agent.
- **Capping agent bonus:** When a sponsored agent reaches their cap, the sponsor receives a **$4,000 bonus** in EXPI stock.
- **7-tier rev share:** Revenue flows up to 7 tiers of sponsors — not just direct sponsor but the entire upline benefits when agents produce.
- **"Buy one get three free":** Gogo's framing for eXp's rev share leverage — sponsor one agent and benefit from everyone they recruit below them.
- **2.5-year timeline:** Reinforces that relationship-based recruiting is a long game. Some of the best recruits take years of consistent contact before committing.
