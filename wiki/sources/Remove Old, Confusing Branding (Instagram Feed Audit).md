# Remove Old, Confusing Branding (Instagram Feed Audit)

> Gogo's direct instruction on treating your Instagram feed as a business storefront — and how to clean it up if it isn't one yet.

## Key Takeaways

- Your Instagram feed is your **storefront**. It must communicate one thing: real estate. Confusion = lost clients.
- Personal content (kids, pets, food, yoga, travel, sports) belongs in Stories or a private personal account — never the feed.
- Limit personal posts in the feed to **2 holidays per year maximum** (e.g., Christmas, Mother's Day).
- **Don't start a new account** — it's too hard to rebuild an audience. Clean up the existing one: delete every post that has nothing to do with real estate.
- Kids/pets are allowed if the **post itself is about real estate** (e.g., getting a referral while sitting at a kids' game). The subject of the post must be real estate — not the child.
- Stories are safe for personal moments — they disappear in 24 hours and don't define your storefront.
- Privacy argument: once your account reaches millions of views, exposing your children to strangers is a real risk. Keep them off public feeds.
- You can show personality (yoga pants, travel aesthetics) as long as what you're **talking about** is real estate.

## Quotes

> "Think of it when someone lands on your Instagram as your storefront. If I had a physical store and I was selling real estate, but my storefront window had a mannequin in yoga outfits, sushi, a cat, a dog, three kids, and pictures from Italy, what would you think I'm selling?"

> "Your feed is your storefront. Your stories appear for 24 hours and then disappear."

> "If you're proud of your kids, post it in your stories, not your feed."

> "It's not about little Johnny winning a game; it's about having a closing while sitting at the game."
