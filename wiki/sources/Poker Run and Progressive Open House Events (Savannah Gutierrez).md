# Poker Run and Progressive Open House Events (Savannah Gutierrez)

> Savannah Gutierrez (Brenham TX, Dallas-Fort Worth market) teaches GoGet'Em members her community event and open house system — including the poker run format, progressive open houses, neighborhood domination strategy, and the small details that win business.

## Key Takeaways

- **Poker run format:** Buyers visit multiple listings in sequence. At each stop, pick up a playing card. Turn in cards at the final house — best poker hand wins a prize. Keeps buyers moving across multiple properties in a fun, social experience.
- **Alternative names:** "Progressive open house" (different food/bites at each stop instead of cards). Savannah also does "Views and Barbecue Blues" — scenic properties + pulled pork sliders at the final house.
- **Why the event format works:** Give people a reason to come. The home becomes secondary — they're having fun, building relationship with you. Engagement that a standard open house can't create.
- **Event partners to invite:**
  - Home stager or interior designer (display pieces, decor conversation)
  - Local restaurant bringing samples ("the new pizza place just opened — try it at our open house")
  - Financial advisor (refinance and rate information)
  - Insurance agent (rates are high; anyone who can help save money is a draw)
- **Signage investment:** Use thick corrugated plastic signs with your logo, not cheap arrows. "Somebody called me once wanting to list their house and they said our signage was the only one that was out there correctly and looked real nice — and it was the whole reason they were picking us." Fiji water at the open house won another listing. Small details matter disproportionately.
- **Open house requirements:** Agents on Savannah's team must do 6 of 8 possible open house days/month (both days for 3 weekends). Non-negotiable. "If you are not walking away with leads, get some coaching."
- **Neighborhood domination strategy:**
  1. Pick your community (Savannah chose her own master plan community; also markets to 4 others in DFW)
  2. Join or create the neighborhood Facebook page
  3. Grow the page if needed — drop postcards or flyers to invite neighbors
  4. Consistently show up as THE expert for that area
  5. Her community grew from 150 to 2,800 homes in 10 years — she's been there the whole time
