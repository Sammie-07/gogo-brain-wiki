# Speed to Lead — Building a Follow-Up System That Converts

> A #teamgogo / [[GoGet'Em Community]] live training co-taught by guest [[Jason Matthews]] ([[Goldbar Staffing]]) and [[Gogo Bethke]] on escaping the "real estate roller coaster" — trading uncontrollable referral income for a systemized sales-and-follow-up operation run on speed-to-lead, a setter/closer/overseer team, automation, and cross-trained VAs.

## Key Takeaways

- **Get off referral-hostage income.** Referral-based months swing wildly ($30K then $8K — the "seesaw effect"). Build a controlled business where you know exactly where every deal comes from. But don't over-correct into being the person who runs everything, has no time, and plateaus.
- **Start with an Ideal Client Profile (ICP).** Screen by income level and likelihood to close so you never "work with a [difficult client] just for a commission." Ties directly to Gogo's [[Niche Finder]] / "second brain."
- **The funnel math:** ~**100 leads → 5–6 real opportunities.** A database of 10 phone numbers cannot grow a business — volume in the top of the funnel is non-negotiable.
- **Three buckets = the health of any business:** (1) new/qualified leads, (2) conversion, (3) nurture/past-clients. Track all three in a CRM and diagnose problems by which bucket's numbers are weak — a marketing problem, a sales-pitch problem, or a nurture problem are three different fixes.
- **Five-step sales process and three roles:** the **Setter** (responds in 30 sec–5 min while the lead is hot), the **Closer** (justifies value and closes), and the **Overseer / sales manager** (tracks the three numbers). Master *closing* yourself first, then delegate *setting*, then step up to *oversee*.
- **The scaling ladder** (close bigger sources of leads, not one at a time): one buyer → close **loan officers** for bulk buyers → close **developers** for 50 listings at once → close **broker-owners** for an army of setters → close a **credit union / bank** for all their pre-approvals. Gogo's Michigan credit-union example fed pre-approved buyers to Team GoGo agents for referral fees.
- **"Buy Back Your Time" ([[Wealth Building|Dan Martell]]).** Jason built a Claude-powered assessment that computes your hourly rate, profit margin, and the *silent cost* of doing low-value work. Live example — Melissa: $140K gross, ~$20K net (14% margin), 54 hrs/week, ~20 hrs on admin worth ~$7/hr → doing that admin herself silently costs **~$70K/year**.
- **Speed-to-lead / 5-minute rule.** Every DM, text, and email is answered within 5 minutes during business hours; the first hour of the morning clears overnight messages. Gogo's team enforces it with a 3-strike write-up (third strike = fired).
- **VA ROI reframe.** Stop asking "what does it cost" ($800–$1,500/mo) and ask "what will it make." A VA generating 2 deals/mo at $6K = $12K (10× the cost); 2 leads/week ≈ 8 deals/mo ≈ $48K. "Give me $1,200 and I'll give you back $48,000."
- **SOP-first training.** Record every task once into a Google Drive SOP library ("I do it / you watch → you do it / I watch → you do it alone"). Training a VA takes up to **90 days** — plan for the ramp.
- **[[Productivity|Red light / green light]].** Green = money-making activities only you can do (on-camera content, being the brand); red = stop and delegate. Gogo: "I ain't doing it unless it's making me $4,200/hour."
- **Automation before headcount.** Automate with cheap AI/tools ($20–29/mo) first, then hire VAs. [[ManyChat]] engages leads and sets appointments 24/7; **GoHighLevel (GHL)** is the single hub (CRM, texting via a smart number, invoices, contracts, websites, social, DMs, email). Upload your entire phone database and tag everyone (SOI / buyer / seller / closed) so VAs instantly recognize past clients.
- **Run it like a business.** Hire at **75% capacity** (not when your head is already spinning); always keep **two cross-trained VAs** so illness or a quit doesn't break operations; a VA should be YOUR full-time employee (even through an agency), never a rotating rented worker; weekly Monday meetings with a per-platform update; "it's like having kids — you have to hold them accountable."

## Quotes

> "You don't want to be a referral hostage, but you also don't want to be the person that's running everything, never has time, and is just plateauing. We call this the seesaw effect in sales."

> "Most people look at how much does this cost versus how much money is this going to make me… Give me $1,200 and I'll give you back $48,000. Would you do it every month?"

> "You can only take yourself to a ceiling. You will not go past that unless you ask for help."

> "You know why you haven't implemented the systems? You don't have the skills. Nor do I. My life is organized because I hired a bunch of amazing, patient people that love to sit in front of the computer and push the buttons."

> "Are you running a part-time business or full-time? A full-time business with a part-time system — that's why it's broken."
