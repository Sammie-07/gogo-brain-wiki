# The Circle Coaching — Leadership Load, "The Opposite Can Be True" & Meeting Cadence (de-identified)

> Private [[The Circle|Circle]] coaching call. **NDA — raw transcript not retained; all member PII removed.** Only [[Gogo Bethke]]'s generalizable teachings are captured.

## Key Teachings (de-identified)

### Leadership & building an organization (folds into [[Real Estate Teams]], [[Mindset]])
- **Pre-pave the frustration of leadership.** Most people won't take advantage of free training/mentorship even when handed to them. High-achievers wrongly assume everyone shares their drive — warn yourself in advance so disappointment doesn't derail you.
- **Meet people where they are — and let some stay.** "Some people are happily miserable. And you have to let them." You control only the quality of what you put out.
- **Reciprocity of belonging:** "If you want a village, you have to be a villager." To *get* referrals/support you must *give* — show up and contribute; you can't extract from a network you never feed.
- **Zero-tolerance for "this could've been an email."** An in-person meeting must be worth the drive. The moment a member frames team time as a burden, that's a values mismatch — the right people look forward to it. Frame team days as the *best* day of the week.

### Team-meeting / in-person cadence system (folds into [[Real Estate Teams]], [[Productivity]])
- **Piggyback your meeting on an existing anchor** (e.g., a weekly org training) so members already come for value.
- **Rotating monthly rhythm** gives *you* structure while feeling *fresh to them* — e.g., Week 1 operations/brokerage updates; Week 2 state/regulatory updates; Week 3 scripts/role-play/tech/Q&A; Week 4 an open higher-level training.
- **Meetings are working sessions + community, not lectures** — gather, teach briefly, then co-work (stuff envelopes, pop-bys, cold-call together, role-play) and hang out. **Watch them work** (review CRM, follow-up, client process) and coach in real time, since their work reflects on your reputation.
- **On/off calendar rhythm** — don't strand a lone 1-hour meeting mid-week; cluster so agents get "one day on, one day off." Consolidate 90-day onboarding into fewer, denser days.
- **Community markets itself** — film the fun/productive team days; prospects ask to join; members bring recruits to experience the culture. "My house became the office."

### Mindset reframes (folds into [[Mindset]])
- **"The opposite can be true" worksheet:** write a one-page essay by that title — list ~5 genuinely-felt negative beliefs, then force your brain to write the *literal opposite* of each and argue it might be the real truth (via Tony Robbins: "the opposite is more than likely the truth"). A coping story ("I'm not enough") often inverts to the real cause ("I'm too much for them to handle"). **Truth is just an accepted thought** — people hold opposite "truths" about the same thing, proving these are chosen beliefs.
- **The points/karma system:** life scores actions like a credit score — put out 10 points of good and the universe *must* return 10; others' harm is a separate small "ding" that doesn't subtract from what you're owed. Do your 10 points daily and trust the return (Zig Ziglar: help enough people get what they want).
- **Adversity is curriculum** — hard chapters are material to study so you can later teach others. "If you really want to learn it, teach it."
- **"It's lonely at the top"** — surpassing 99% makes you different from 99%; isolation is structural, not failure.
- **Childhood programming, the elephant & hypnotic rhythm:** the first ~7–8 years set core beliefs. The chained-elephant never tries to escape as an adult though it easily could — ask which childhood "chains" still bind you. Per *Outwitting the Devil*, ~98% of daily thoughts repeat yesterday's ("a broken record") on the subconscious, so we can't fix it consciously — a third party (coach, therapist, transformational event) is sometimes required. "If we could fix ourselves, we would have."
- **Reading:** *Think and Grow Rich* (reread; lands differently each time) and *Outwitting the Devil* (Napoleon Hill). Consider an immersive transformational event for deep identity work.

### Content creation & delegation (folds into [[Personal Branding]], [[Productivity]])
- **Edit natively in the platform** — the algorithm favors content edited/posted through Instagram's *Edits* app.
- **Get "Meta Verified"** (~$15/mo per platform, a business expense) — unlocks features (incl. Shared Access), protects against impersonation, signals credibility.
- **Shared Access (requires Meta Verified):** add up to 2 people who can post/edit on your behalf *without* your password and can't delete the account — cleaner than sharing login.
- **Two-phone discipline:** a dedicated business phone whose media auto-uploads to a shared cloud folder assistants can access; keep personal content off it entirely.
- **Curate before delegating:** don't hand editors your whole camera roll — shoot broadly, then "heart"/select only approved media into a **shared album**. iPhone-to-iPhone shared albums beat cloud drives on speed and resolution.
- **Shared-drive default for AI output:** when a team runs one AI account, set a shared drive folder as the default save location so the whole team gets automatic access (no per-file requests) — and have assistants join the setup call to implement it.

### Choosing advisors
- **Never take advice from someone who hasn't "been there, done that"** — knowing the rules by the book ≠ having lived them.
