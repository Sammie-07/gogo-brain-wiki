# Holiday Events for Real Estate Lead Generation (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo's system for using seasonal holiday events — partnered with vendors who sponsor the giveaways at no cost — to generate leads, grow social media followers, and stay top-of-mind with past clients. Includes the Thanksgiving pie delivery lesson learned, Pictures with Santa, and trunk-or-treat.

## Key Takeaways

- **One event per quarter max.** More than that and you can't execute them properly. Recommended cadence: Easter egg drop (spring), pool/movie night (summer), Thanksgiving pie pickup (fall), Pictures with Santa (winter).
- **Always partner with vendors for prizes.** Lenders, title companies, movers, painters, plumbers, cleaners — these businesses exist because of your referrals. Call in the favor. Example: mortgage company offered free appraisals for the first 10 buyers from trunk-or-treat. Cost to Gogo: zero.
- **Invite past clients + "cheerleaders."** Cheerleaders = people who refer clients to Gogo but have never been clients themselves. Each invitee can bring one guest. This compounds the reach.
- **Use a title company's office as your venue.** Eliminates venue cost. Gogo did Pictures with Santa at her title company's space.
- **Never hand-deliver to 50 clients.** Lesson learned: first Thanksgiving, she tried to hand-deliver pies across a 3-hour radius. Took a month and a half. Pivot: pre-order pies to the title company, send emails, have clients come to you.
- **Require social follows before the QR code.** Before allowing someone to scan the QR code to claim the free item, guide them through following your Instagram and Facebook accounts right there in person. "I'm not giving you something for free if you're not going to support my business."
- **QR code captures leads.** Everyone who scans = name + contact info in your database.
- **Follow up the next day.** Call or text every person who engaged. Ask where they are in the buying/selling timeline: now, 90 days, 6 months? Move every conversation to an appointment or coffee meeting.
- **Start planning 6 weeks early.** Time needed for vendor coordination, flyer + QR code creation, event space booking, hiring Santa, sourcing prizes.
- **Team can attend when you can't.** As Gogo scaled, she couldn't always be there. Her local team attended on her behalf and still generated clients.
