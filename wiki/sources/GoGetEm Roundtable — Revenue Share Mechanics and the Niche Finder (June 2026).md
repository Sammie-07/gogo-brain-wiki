# GoGet'Em Roundtable — eXp Revenue Share Mechanics & The Niche Finder Tool

> First monthly [[GoGet'Em Community]] Roundtable mastermind — [[Gogo Bethke]] breaks down eXp revenue share mechanics (FLA vs. FLQA, level unlocks, bonuses) and gives a live demo of the AI-powered Niche Finder tool, with a mortgage-market update from Jason Dupree.

## Key Takeaways
- New format: **Roundtable** mastermind every **third Wednesday of the month** — open Q&A, share what's working/not working, and sustain community between in-person events. [[Beth Silverman]] block-calendared it for the rest of the year.
- **Mortgage update (Jason Dupree):** rates ~**6.6%** across the board; market warming into summer but transitioning into a **buyer's market** — push buyers to act early. First Fed meeting under the new Fed Chair was that day; Friday (Juneteenth) closed market, so get deals to loan partners by Thursday.
- **REMLO program (via Texana Bank):** become a licensed loan officer in all 50 states day one under the bank's charter — no 20-hour course, no SAFE exam; paid **0.5% of the mortgage amount** on your transactions' loans. Qualification minimum: **6 buy-side transactions** (you become a bank employee). REMLO of the Month: [[Melissa Albert]], +**$5,800** last month.
- **FLA (Frontline Active):** an agent personally sponsored to you at eXp who pays the **$85/month** fee. May never sell — "dead weight."
- **FLQA (Frontline Qualified Active):** qualifies via **$5,000 in GCI/commission collected** — NOT 2 transactions (could be 5 leases at $1,000 each, a referral, any income type). Each $5,000 commission qualifies the agent for **6 months**, then they fall off and must re-qualify.
- **Level-unlock math:** Level 1 opens with **1 FLQA**; then every **5 FLQAs** opens the next level — 5→L2, 10→L3, 15→L4, 20→L5, 25→L6, **30→L7**. (Old plan required 40 for L7; eXp 2.0 lowered it to 30.)
- **Bonus structure overriding the math:** ICON agents have all 7 levels open automatically; **capping agents** have levels 1–5 open; **brand-new agents** have levels 1–3 open. Group consensus (Gogo updated this live): **levels 1–3 are open all the time**, not just the first year.
- **First-year cap bonus:** new capping agents also get a **$4,000 bonus** per qualifying FLQA in the first year.
- **Your personal production is irrelevant to revenue share** — Gogo hasn't sold a house in **3.5 years** and isn't even an FLQA to her own sponsor, [[Kurt Shewell]]. RevShare depends on your downline's production and your FLQA count, not your own sales.
- **Conversion rule of thumb:** ~half your FLAs become FLQAs (Gogo cited **98 FLAs / ~46–47 FLQAs**). "If you need 30, go get 60."
- **Off-boarding mechanic:** departed agents stay in your dashboard for a **12-month** grace period (changed from 6 months) to re-name the same sponsor; pending/new-construction deals can keep them showing up to **2 years** — distorts dashboard counts.
- **Co-sponsorship:** the sponsor (not the co-sponsor) gets the FLQA credit and the $4,000 bonus; Gogo's understanding is a co-sponsee's downline stays under you if the co-sponsee leaves (to confirm with [[Kurt Shewell]]).
- **Niche Finder tool ($24.99/use):** a **10-question** questionnaire run through Gogo's **"second brain"** — every presentation, podcast, and video she's done, embedded and linked to Claude. Output: niche statement, **3 Instagram bio options** (<150 chars, no dashes/bullets), **8 personalized marketing ideas** (use 2–3), SEO strategy (title tag, meta description, 5 long-tail keywords), a **12-week blog content calendar**, and a **90-day marketing plan** (month 1 foundation, month 2 momentum, month 3 scale) with daily in-person/Stories/Feed actions. Run separately per avatar (buyer vs. seller vs. agent attraction). Free verbiage/prompt available, but won't draw on Gogo's data.
- **Niche philosophy:** "When you're selling to everyone, you're selling to no one." Niche changes with your life phase; burnout often means you've outgrown the clients you still serve.
- **Pivot is mandatory:** market forces (war, gas prices, inflation, stuck rates, canceled contracts) require pivoting — "if you don't pivot, we become dinosaurs."
