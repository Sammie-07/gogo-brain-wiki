# Maximizing Profit in Real Estate — Three Levers (Lou Raven and Elise Palacios)

> Lou Raven and Elise Palacios (Reinvest Real Estate, CT) present a second GoGet'Em session focused exclusively on increasing profit — the "I" in their PITY framework. Three specific tactics that require no additional transactions.

## Key Takeaways

- **Context:** This is their second GoGet'Em appearance, focused on the "Increasing Profit" component of the PITY framework (Planning, Implementation, Tracking, **Increasing Profit**).
- **The four profit levers:** Four ways to increase profit in a real estate sales business:
  1. Sell more houses (most common advice — also the most exhausting)
  2. Increase your average sales price
  3. Charge more for your services
  4. Reduce the commission you pay your brokerage
- **Why "sell more" is the worst lever:** "To me, that sounds horrible." More volume = more time, more stress, more overhead. The other three levers don't require a single additional transaction.
- **Elise's origin story context:** Ran a cleaning business — physically demanding. Her goal became how to earn more from the same work, not how to do more work. That mindset shift drives this whole presentation.
- **Working smarter, not harder:** Lou and Elise's shared thesis — the agents who succeed long-term are those who extract more from what they're already doing, not those who grind harder.
- **The PITY system:** This session is specifically about the "I" (Increasing Profit). Their prior GoGet'Em session covered P (Planning), L (Legacy — or the PLAN framework), and T (Tracking).
- **Practical application:** All four levers are within an agent's control. Commission structure, brokerage cap choices, pricing strategy, and service value positioning are decisions — not fixed facts.
