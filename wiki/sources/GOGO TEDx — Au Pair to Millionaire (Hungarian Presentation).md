# GOGO TEDx — Au Pair to Millionaire (Hungarian Presentation)

> TEDx-style presentation delivered in Hungarian/Romanian for a Transylvanian audience. The content mirrors Gogo's "Au Pair to Millionaire" talk but told in her native language for her home country audience.

## Key Takeaways

- Presentation is in Hungarian; title translates to "You can take the girl out of Transylvania, you can't take Transylvania out of the girl"
- Subtitle: "From an Au Pair to a Millionaire" — delivered as the 7 lessons learned at home vs. in America
- Core message: "If I hadn't lived through communism, I wouldn't have anything to compare a good life to. There's something good in every bad situation."
- All the same quotes and stories appear in their Hungarian versions: elephant story, airplane story, cages with open doors, Henry Ford, George Lucas, Rockefeller, Zig Ziglar
- Delivered to a Romanian/Hungarian audience making it culturally relevant to people who grew up under the same conditions
- Final line (Hungarian translation of her standard closer): variation of "you don't have to work harder, you have to work smarter"

## Notes on Content

This presentation contains the same philosophical framework as [[sources/From an Au Pair to a Millionaire]] but delivered in Hungarian. The TEDx format suggests this was a formal speaking engagement in Eastern Europe (Hungary or Romania/Transylvania). It confirms Gogo actively speaks to audiences in her native language, maintaining connection to her roots while sharing the same entrepreneurial philosophy she teaches in English.
