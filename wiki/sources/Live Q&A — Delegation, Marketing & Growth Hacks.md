# Live Q&A: Delegation, Marketing & Growth Hacks

> Week 4 (final) of the paid 4-week Business Mastery webinar series. Pure Q&A format. Covers ManyChat complex automation mechanics, VA management in practice, agent attraction philosophy, eXp sponsor selection, team building strategy, GHL pricing, lender social media strategy, and Bradley's lead capture hack.

## Key Takeaways

- **ManyChat quiz complexity:** 1,700+ steps; 4 weeks research + 2 weeks build. Gogo made every decision point herself ("if they say yes, what should it say?"). Automation glitch lesson: first and second messages must go out simultaneously to avoid external automation triggering a premature reply.
- **"Know who knows":** Gogo has never personally logged into Zapier, ManyChat, GHL, or Skye (eXp transaction platform) in 5+ years. She knows what the tool does and what she needs; her team figures out how.
- **VA team geography:** Brazil, Pakistan, India, Philippines. Kristy (US, Director of Operations) is the only US-based person. All VAs work Gogo's Eastern time zone.
- **Feedback in public:** Gogo calls out mistakes in front of the group and asks the person to say out loud what they did wrong. Forces comprehension; reduces repetition.
- **Hours validation math:** Gogo estimates how long a task should take, does the math, and flags discrepancies. If a VA claims 97 hours for a task Gogo knows takes 3 hours/day, she questions it. Lying = immediate firing (no second chance on character).
- **Parallel VA training:** Always hire and train two simultaneously. Same meetings, same onboarding. If one quits or is slow, the other covers. Cost is essentially the same (one more person on Zoom). Minimum: $700/month for two part-time VAs.
- **Hiring couples:** Works if you have 3+ VAs total (others cover vacations). Problematic if you only have two VAs and they're a couple.
- **Agent attraction:** Never chase. Post results (rev share deposits, cap-back notifications, milestones). Add "comment PARTNER" CTA. ManyChat delivers them to a landing page. If they don't book, she doesn't follow up. 102 frontline agents; never made a recruiting call.
- **Content frequency = results frequency:** Post agent attraction content 3x/month, get 3x-a-month results. Vulnerability accelerates results.
- **eXp sponsor selection:** Cannot change without leaving for 6 months. Choose based on: (1) who explains the opportunity best, (2) complementary skills, (3) long-term enjoyment. Location irrelevant -- eXp operates in every US state and 27 countries.
- **Team building:** 80/20 rule -- 80% of agents do 20% of business. Prefer 2-3 rockstars over 12. "Graduate" underperformers to solo agent status instead of firing.
- **Ideal retained agent:** High closing skills, no self-lead-generation ability. They need your leads; they'll never leave because they can't fish for themselves.
- **GHL pricing:** $97/mo for 3 sub-accounts; $297/mo agency plan (Gogo's) for unlimited sub-accounts. GHL requires building from scratch; kvCORE is plug-and-play for agents.
- **VAs learn via courses:** Gogo pays for VAs to sit through courses, then holds a 15-minute summary meeting. Continuous learning proxied through the team.
- **Lender strategy:** Post success stories on social media instead of scheduling coffee meetings. "Stories sell. Facts tell." Nobody cares about today's rate unless they're actively buying.
- **Bradley's lead capture hack:** Require name, email, and phone number before showing the checkout page. Converts anonymous price-curious visitors into warm leads in the nurture sequence.
- **GoGet'Em Community:** $49/month, first month free. One live training per month plus weekly content. All recordings archived in GHL membership area.
