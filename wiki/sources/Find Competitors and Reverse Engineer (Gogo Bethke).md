# Find Competitors and Reverse Engineer (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo reframes "competition" as opportunity: very few local agents are actually good at social media marketing, which means the top is reachable. Homework: identify 3 local competitors, study them deeply, extract what's working, implement it.

## Key Takeaways

- **Local social media competition is thin.** Ask yourself: who are the top 3 social media marketers in your immediate local market? Not the Serhants or Selling Sunset — your actual backyard. Most agents struggle to name 3. That gap is your opportunity.
- **"It's really not that inundated in your own market."** The perceived saturation is largely an illusion — the agents who are visible simply show up consistently.
- **Homework:** Write down 3 names of local competitors killing it on social media. Then study each one this week.
- **Study method ("intensive research of an individual"):**
  - Go back to 2008 on their profile (scroll all the way back)
  - Platforms: Facebook, Instagram, website
  - Write bullet-point notes: what's working, what's a great idea, what you should start doing
  - Note what you could do better or more of
- **Three-pager notebook method:** One page per competitor — name at top, bullet points of observations below. Flip through them and extract the best ideas to implement.
- **Two outcomes from this exercise:**
  1. You discover specific tactics that are working → steal and implement them
  2. You realize they're not doing anything extraordinary — just being consistent → your only job is to out-consistent them
- **"I'm the world's best copycat."** Gogo's explicit philosophy: don't reinvent the wheel. If someone already built a road, walk on it. Adapt, improve, and apply.
- **Advantage:** You're not just copying — you're bringing your own personality, consistency, accent, humor, or whatever makes you different. Consistency + differentiation beats any competitor who's merely coasting on an early start.
