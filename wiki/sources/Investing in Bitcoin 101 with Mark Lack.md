# Investing in Bitcoin 101 with Mark Lack

> Day 5 of Gogo's money week inside the GoGet'Em Community. Mark Lack is a top-10 US influencer Gogo met at a Tony Robbins mastermind. He is 34 years old, effectively retired, and holds tens of millions of dollars in Bitcoin after studying over 3,000 hours of Bitcoin content. This session completely reversed Gogo's position on Bitcoin. His mentor is Michael Saylor (MicroStrategy), who took the company from a $1.5B to $25B+ market cap by using Bitcoin as a treasury asset.

## Key Takeaways

**The Fiat Currency Thesis**
- 180 fiat currencies recognized in the world. The US dollar has lost 99.8% of purchasing power since creation.
- A house bought for $20,000 is now worth $2 million -- but the house is 50+ years old. The dollar went down, not the house up. Real estate appreciation is dollar depreciation in disguise.
- When confidence in fiat collapses (Zimbabwe, Venezuela, Egypt, Turkey), citizens wake up to 30%+ overnight purchasing power losses. Zimbabwe had trillion-dollar bills -- not a joke.
- The US government will always print money. It cannot stop. Therefore the dollar will always go down. Therefore Bitcoin will always go up in dollar terms.

**What Bitcoin Is**
- Simultaneously an asset, a network, a protocol, and an ideology.
- The only finite commodity in existence. Total supply: 21 million coins. That number will never change. No government, company, or person can print more. Ever.
- Mark calls it the "apex commodity." Gold is the top competitor but is inferior: gold cannot be digitally audited, digitally transferred, easily fractionalized, or self-custodied without cost. Bitcoin solves all four.

**The Internet Parallel**
- When the internet launched, experts said it was for government and banking only. It took decades for regulation and businesses to restructure. The early stages looked like chaos.
- Bitcoin is at that same stage -- the internet of money. Most financial products are being built on top of Bitcoin, the way AI companies are APIs on top of ChatGPT.
- iPhone came with open iOS that let developers build apps on top of it, increasing its value over time. Bitcoin has the same structure (Lightning Network, etc.).

**The Bitcoin ETF**
- Approved January 2024 in the US. BlackRock launched IBIT -- the largest financial institution in the world with $12T AUM.
- IBIT became the number one ETF by assets in the entire history of ETFs. Nothing else is close to second place.
- The ETF is purchasing $100M to $1B of Bitcoin per day. That is new demand arriving every day that did not exist before.

**FASB Accounting Rule Change (2025)**
- FASB changed rules in December 2024 effective for 2025: publicly traded companies can now use fair value accounting for Bitcoin.
- Previously, companies could only report Bitcoin going down (impairment losses). They could not show Bitcoin going up. Zero incentive to hold Bitcoin on a corporate balance sheet.
- Starting 2025: if Bitcoin goes up, companies show it going up in quarterly reports. Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, Tesla, and others will start adding Bitcoin to their balance sheets. Enormous new demand.

**Why Bitcoin Will Keep Going Up**
- Mechanism 1 -- money printing is infinite: More dollars chasing the same 21M coins means the dollar price goes up forever.
- Mechanism 2 -- capital reallocation: Approximately $330 trillion in global wealth. If just 1% of bonds ($300+ trillion) flows into Bitcoin, that alone takes Bitcoin from $58,000 to $1.2 million. At 2% it is $2.4 million. At 5% it is $7 million.
- Mechanism 3 -- rich people never sell scarce assets: BlackRock does not sell their real estate. They hold, borrow, buy more. Supply available on the market shrinks as large holders accumulate.

**Price Predictions (Mark's Opinions)**
- At time of recording: $58,000. Bitcoin hit $74,000 (all-time high) before the April 2024 halving -- historically unprecedented.
- End of 2024: $80,000-$100,000 (conservative)
- 2025: $150,000-$200,000 (2025 is historically Bitcoin's best year -- the year after the halving)
- 2029: $600,000-$700,000 (next halving cycle peak)
- 10 years: $1M+ per coin
- 20 years: $10M per coin (based on government money printing trajectory modeling)

**The Halving**
- Every 4 years, the new supply of Bitcoin created per block is cut in half.
- Less new supply + growing demand = historically massive price increases 12-18 months after each halving.
- All of Bitcoin's gains in any 4-year cycle happen in approximately 10 days. If you sell and miss those 10 days, you miss the entire gain.
- The only strategy: hold long-term, never sell.

**Bitcoin vs. Real Estate (For Non-Wealthy Investors)**
- Real estate problems for non-wealthy: cannot diversify (one property = concentrated risk), cannot compete with experienced investors with off-market deals and bank relationships, today's interest rates make most deals unprofitable.
- Bitcoin advantages for everyone: invest from $1 to $1B with zero barriers, no landlord duties, 24/7 liquidity, perfectly fractionalized, decentralized (no government can confiscate), mathematical scarcity enforced by code.
- Mark's personal strategy: accumulate Bitcoin, take 40-80% LTV loans against it, use proceeds to buy income-producing real estate, real estate cash flow pays off the loan. End result: paid-off real estate + paid-off Bitcoin + sold neither.

**Why the Blockchain Cannot Be Hacked**
- The Bitcoin blockchain has never been hacked in 15+ years. China tried to ban it and failed. Russia cannot hack it. The US government cannot hack it.
- Now requires more computing energy than every government, company, and intelligence agency combined.
- Vulnerability is always the user device or credentials -- not the blockchain itself. Example: credentials stored as a screenshot in phone notes.

**How to Buy Bitcoin**
- Entry level -- Bitcoin ETF: Buy IBIT through any brokerage (Schwab, E*Trade, Fidelity). Good for retirement accounts. No wallet management required.
- Intermediate -- Coinbase: Download app, complete KYC (driver's license), link bank or Apple Pay, buy any dollar amount, set recurring purchase, enable two-factor authentication.
- Advanced -- self-custody: Move Bitcoin off Coinbase onto a hardware wallet (Trezor, ColdCard, or Ledger). You hold the private keys, not Coinbase. For large holdings: multisig setup requires multiple key holders simultaneously -- even at gunpoint, only one key holder cannot access the funds.

**Bitcoin vs. Altcoins**
- Bitcoin is a store of value -- like digital real estate. You do not sell your Apple stock to buy coffee.
- Other cryptocurrencies (Ethereum, Solana) are venture capital bets. Some succeed but most go to zero. Mark does not recommend them for the average investor.
- Bitcoin is unique: fixed supply, no issuer, complete decentralization, 15+ years of proven security. No other cryptocurrency has all four.

**The Beachfront Property Metaphor**
- Bitcoin is digital beachfront property. Finite amount of beachfront. Rich people buy at any price because they know no more will ever be created. As more people pursue the same fixed supply, the price goes up permanently.
