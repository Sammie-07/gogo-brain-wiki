# How to Own Your Price Point and Attract the Right Buyers

> Your niche price point is 100% controlled by what you post. $200K investor special = investors. $1M lakefront = luxury buyers. Pick your range, find those homes, profile those people, enter their world, create content.

## Key Takeaways

- **Price point tied to content:** Post a $200K investor special → get investors. Post a $1M lakefront → get luxury buyers. "It is in your control."
- **4-step price point framework:**
  1. **What price range?** Pick your number (e.g., $500K).
  2. **Where are those homes?** Write down 3–5 neighborhoods or sides of town.
  3. **Who are those people?** Jobs, age, hobbies, where they hang out.
  4. **What do they want to hear?** Create content around that (golf schedules, country club events, school district news, community activities).
- **Enter their world physically:** If the niche is a country club community at $500K+, get a membership. Show up. Meet one → they refer their mother-in-law, their buddy who wants to live nearby, their colleague.
- **Become the information provider:** Know the golf schedule, the Easter Bunny event, the HOA meeting — and post it. You become the go-to resource for that community, not just a realtor.
- **Consistency + niche = authority:** Post $500K homes → you get $500K buyers. Million dollar homes → million dollar buyers. "If you post a $500,000 house, you get 500,000 buyers. Million dollar house, you get a million dollar buyer."

## Quotes

> "Your specialty, your niche, your price point is totally tied to what you post."

> "If you want to be known as the lakefront queen, then should you post flats downtown Detroit? No, you should post lakefront stuff."

> "If you post a $500,000 house, you get $500,000 buyers. Million dollar house, you get a million dollar buyer. It is in your control."
