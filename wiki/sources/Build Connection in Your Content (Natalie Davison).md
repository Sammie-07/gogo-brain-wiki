# Build Connection in Your Content (Natalie Davison)

> Part 2 of Natalie Davison's GoGet'Em training series. Teaches a 6-step Content Story Frame for turning personal incidents into audience-connecting content.

## Key Takeaways

- **Humans think in narrative pattern.** With insufficient information, they default to the negative. If you change brokerages without explaining why, people assume the worst. Control your story or others will invent one.
- **Your brand is full of stories** that serve your audience — you just need to find and structure them.
- **The 6-Step Content Story Frame:**
  1. List 20 key incidents (first week, proudest moment, biggest doubt, hardest lesson…)
  2. Write one story out in full — as it happened
  3. Identify three adjectives for how you felt (e.g., terrified, doubtful, hopeful)
  4. Identify the lesson learned
  5. Identify what is BETTER because this happened
  6. Identify how this story impacts your buyer persona / ideal client
- Content that serves your audience AND your business simultaneously is the goal. Value given = value received.
- People buy products and services that support the stories they already tell themselves. Your stories must align with theirs.
- Natalie's story example: Two days before her first live event, her mentor canceled. The venue said "people aren't coming to see you." She stayed up two nights, showed up, served 40 strangers — many became her best long-term clients. Lesson: Fear is protecting you, but something quieter is more important.
