# Real-World Negotiation Hacks

> Gogopreneur podcast "deep dive" episode — AI hosts present Gogo's negotiation playbook, drawing on her own lessons and Chris Voss's *Never Split the Difference* (available in her Amazon store).

## Key Takeaways

- **Negotiation is everywhere** — not just business deals. Negotiating with your kids about lollipops uses the same brain circuits as a hostage situation. The amygdala doesn't distinguish the stakes.
- **Tactical empathy** (Voss framework): not sympathy or compassion — just the ability to identify what the other person is feeling and say it out loud. You don't have to feel it; you just name it.
  - Gogo used to hate the word "empathy" — Eastern European upbringing made feelings seem irrelevant. She was literally using ChatGPT to inject empathy into her sales writing.
- **Mirroring:** Repeat the last 3 words someone said. Lowest-effort technique to make people feel heard, build rapport, and get them to elaborate.
- **Magic wand effect:** Proactively call out the negative emotion or assumption you think the other side has. ("It seems like you feel I slapped you in the face with that offer.") Names the elephant — the negativity often evaporates because the person feels seen.
- **Let the other side have your way:** Ask open-ended how/what questions. Guide them to suggest the solution you want, then let them choose it. They think it's their idea.
- **Three conflict styles** (from Voss, endorsed by Gogo):
  1. **Assertive** (fight type) — driven by respect, needs to be heard. Pitfall: too transactional, forgets gratitude. *Gogo identifies as primarily assertive.*
  2. **Analyst** (flight type) — data-driven, needs information. Pitfall: paralysis by analysis, hates surprises, appears cold.
  3. **Accommodator** (make-friends type) — harmony-focused, optimistic. Pitfall: avoids hard truths, creates "train wrecks" by not raising problems early. (*Richard Branson noted as classic accommodator.*)
- **Borrow strengths from all three.** Gogo adopted analyst habits (data/spreadsheets), and accommodator habits (gratitude, from observing her husband's American upbringing).
- **Staying calm under pressure:** Visualize yourself responding calmly — re-shoot the movie in your head. Smile on phone calls (changes physiology and tone).
- **Escape hatch phrase** (Barbara Corcoran tip): "Let me think about it." Buys time, prevents instant emotional reactions you'll regret.
- **"I found something fascinating"** — how Gogo's team frames bad news to her. Reframes a crisis as an interesting problem to solve. Disarms her assertive default reaction.
- **Welcome black swans** (Voss): unexpected surprises in negotiations contain the most valuable information. If you're not being surprised, you're not listening hard enough.
