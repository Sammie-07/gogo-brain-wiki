# Be the Name They Already Know: Building a Dominant Business in a Small Market (Jeannie Steele)

> A #teamgogo / [[GoGet'Em Community]] guest training hosted by [[Gogo Bethke]] (with [[Kristy Waker]] and a [[Jason Dupree]] mortgage minute). Guest [[Jeannie Steele]] — a solo agent who captured near-total market share of an 8,000-person Montana county through a **give-first**, community-embedded brand. "She did not buy her way in. She gave her way in."

## Key Takeaways

- **The numbers:** #38 in the US by closed transactions and ~#85 by volume among eXp individual producers (2025); **#1 by transactions, #5 by volume inside #teamgogo** (~1,700 agents). ~85 closings solo, nearly **$30M**, almost all inside one **8,000-person county** (Broadwater County, MT) — she captured roughly **85 of ~86 transactions** in the county (near-total market share).
- **Give-first, never cold-call.** She refuses to cold-call or door-knock. She leans into what she loves (crafting): she and her 4 kids handmake **~100 gifts a month** and walk them to Main Street businesses — **unbranded**, to avoid "soliciting."
- **Slow but steady compounding.** A branded Kleenex with a funny saying sat in a couple's glovebox for **4 years** before they called. "Not the fastest, but very steady."
- **Institutional gratitude.** After a deputy was killed she began bringing holiday gifts to the sheriff's department (29 people + 1 K-9); after a family health scare she added ~100 gifts each holiday to the hospital, plus assisted living.
- **Signature annual events = become a town tradition.** Free catered food at her physical Main Street office during the **Ghost Stroll** (Halloween; grew from 200 to ~1,000 trick-or-treaters) and the **Christmas Stroll**. (Catered — to offload liability.)
- **Community boards as lead gen.** Joined the county planning board and board of health (both by invitation) — subdivision/planning knowledge directly fed her builder niche.
- **Builder/land niche = her avatar.** Works with 5 builders; buying land to build drives transaction *count* up while lowering avg price (the gap between her transaction rank and volume rank). Example: took a stalled 80-acre subdivision, split it into minor subdivisions, paired the seller with a builder for spec homes.
- **Physical office matters in rural markets.** Older/rural clients won't sign electronically; handshakes close deals — a physical presence is essential.
- **"I don't compete."** In 12+ years she competed for only 5 listings and won zero. Per *The Full Fee Agent*: if they're interviewing you, they've already chosen — so she wishes them luck and keeps her marketing. **Speed is her edge:** "I list very quickly — don't have a day, let's roll."
- **Non-negotiable fee script.** Compensation (buyer-side) is negotiable, but if asked "what will you take?" on *her* fee she stands up: *"Mine's not negotiable. Take it or leave it… I am not the agent for you."*
- **[[Lead Generation|HighNote]] open-tracking.** Uses HighNote for listing/CMA/buyer presentations partly because it pings her when a prospect opens the doc — she "gets dinged at 3 a.m." and knows overpriced sellers will return: *"Now we're chasing the market. You were $100K overpriced the day you signed."*
- **Photography as a competitive moat.** "It's not the best agent who wins, it's the best marketer who wins." She trained a local sports photographer from scratch, paid him above market to stay accessible for 3–4 listings/week, now spends $30–40K/yr, and puts pro photos even on $20K land listings — forcing the whole market to upgrade.
- **AI/systems rebuild.** After losing a 5-year assistant she's rebuilding with AI — rebuilt her seller "move-out form" in **Claude** in ~14 min and is building an AI "brain." Gogo demoed her own second brain that clusters everything she knows so her team can query it.
- **[[Jason Dupree|REMLO]] cross-sell (mortgage minute):** agents with 6+ buy-side transactions can become a REMLO (licensed in all 50 states), earning ~0.5% on the mortgages their transactions generate — plus HELOCs, refis, and lending to their own downline's non-qualifying agents (~$2,000/file left on the table at a $400K average price).
