# Landing Pages: Why You Need Them and How to Use Them Weekly

> Gogo explains the "niche down" philosophy behind creating dedicated IDX landing pages — and why driving recurring traffic to evergreen niche links is more powerful than generic posting. Part of *Gogo's Social Media Challenge 2026*.

## Key Takeaways

- **"If you're not known for anything, you're known for nothing."** Niche landing pages make you the go-to agent for a specific property type or area.
- A niche landing page is an IDX feed filtered to a specific topic (lakefront, new construction, open houses, etc.) — it auto-updates as new listings hit the MLS. Build it once; it works forever.
- Gogo's two niches: **Portage Chain of Lakes** (lakefront, Pinckney MI — highest commissions) and **Open Houses** (her highest conversion environment — "put me in front of a human and I'll close them 99% of the time").
- The custom domain (e.g., gogosrealestateteam.com) forwards to the KVCore/BoldTrail website via domain masking — the clean URL shows, the ugly eXp default is hidden.
- These niche links become the consistent destinations for your weekly social media posts — you drive traffic to them repeatedly because they always have fresh content.
- Choose 2–3 niches based on: what makes you the most money, what you enjoy, what you're already known for locally.
- Kristy Waker's videos cover the technical build; this video covers the strategic WHY.

## Quotes

> "If you're not known for anything, you're known for nothing."

> "These are the links that you are going to keep generating traffic back to because these are the things that make you the most amount of money, you enjoy, and that's what you want to be known for."

> "I don't have to rerun a new report and send it to somebody. I'll just bring them here because this is what I want to be known for. This is my niche."
