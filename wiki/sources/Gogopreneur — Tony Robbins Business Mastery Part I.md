# Gogopreneur — Tony Robbins Business Mastery (Part I, Episode 11)

> Gogopreneur podcast Episode 11. Gogo hosted ~20 #teamgogo agents at her Florida home for a five-day Business Mastery event (Tony Robbins). Part I captures the team's goals and expectations going in. Part II covers what they took away.

## Key Takeaways

- **She has done UPW (Unleash the Power Within) 7 times** as of this recording (2023). Business Mastery was ~7–8 years prior; she now owns **9 different companies** and hears the training completely differently.
- **The same training lands differently at each level.** "Your brain has to grow with you. When you listen to training, you might get 10% of it because you don't have that part of your brain developed yet. Now I want to see what I get this time."
- **Her biggest goal at this event:** Stop being involved in every small aspect of every business. Give team members freedom to make decisions on their own without her sign-off — as long as it's for the benefit of the business. "I do not think I gave them enough freedom to make decisions on their own."
- **Gogopreneur brand:** Two Instagram accounts — @gogosrealestate (real estate) and @gogopreneur (entrepreneur brand). Gogopreneur.com is the podcast website. Also available on YourHomeTV.
- **Team members visible in this episode:** Kristy Waker (been with Gogo ~5+ years as of this recording), Natalia (production coordinator — makes sure episodes are promoted, editors have what they need), Alex (project manager for Black Dog Video, ensures deadlines).
- Full episode on YouTube; companion Part II also on YouTube.

## Quotes

> "I've already done Business Mastery about seven years ago for a totally different piece, and I didn't even know what I walked into. And I walked out of there as a totally different person. Today we own nine different companies and the problems are different. The message is kind of the same but I hear it differently and it applies differently."

> "I am constantly involved in every step and it's not like I'm a micromanager — I am not. But I don't think I gave enough freedom to the people who worked for me in certain positions to make those decisions on their own."
