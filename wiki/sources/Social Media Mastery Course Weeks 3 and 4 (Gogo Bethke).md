# Social Media Mastery Course — Weeks 3 & 4 (Gogo Bethke)

> Weeks 3 and 4 of Gogo's paid 4-week Social Media Mastery Course. Week 3: tracking, numbers, and GoHighLevel deep dive. Week 4: open Q&A covering ManyChat, Zapier, delegation, and Instagram Story strategy.

## Key Takeaways

### Week 3 — Tracking, Numbers, and GoHighLevel

- **"Numbers and spreadsheets and collecting data and why on earth we do that."** Gogo opens Week 3 with this framing — tracking is the bridge between effort and results.
- **GoHighLevel buckets deep dive:**
  - Each business line gets its own sub-account ("bucket") inside GHL
  - Examples: GoGet'Em community, agent attraction funnel, social media quote content, Michigan team operations
  - Previously, Gogo had chaos: multiple CRMs, multiple bank accounts, no unified view
  - GHL at ~$200/month consolidates everything into one login, one system
  - One VA manages all buckets — single login, centralized control
- Tracking is not about micromanagement — it's about knowing what's working so you can double down and cut what isn't.

### Week 4 — Q&A (Final Session)

- **ManyChat:** Gogo personally set up one keyword trigger as a hands-on exercise to understand how it works. Delegates all further ManyChat builds to her team.
- **Zapier:** Gogo has never logged into Zapier directly. "I know enough about Zapier to understand what it does but I don't need to be the one setting it up." Embodies the delegation principle — understand the tool conceptually, hire the execution.
- **Delegation principle (from Romanian professor):** "Our brain capacity is limited. We don't need to know everything. We just need to know the person who knows." This was taught to Gogo by a professor in Romania and she has applied it as a core operating principle throughout her career.
- **Instagram Stories strategy:** Reviews should be shared as Instagram Stories under 1 minute (Stories capped at 60 seconds) — keep review videos tight and punchy.
- Week 4 format: pure open Q&A. "I answer whatever you ask."
