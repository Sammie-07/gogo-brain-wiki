# Same Username Everywhere

> Part of [[Gogo Bethke]]'s *Gogo's Social Media Challenge 2026* series. Companion to [[Same Photo Everywhere]] — the username/brand name consistency principle. Own your name on .com and Instagram before anything else, then be the same everywhere.

## Key Takeaways

- **Same username = brand recognition.** Name, photo, everything the same everywhere — McDonald's, Home Depot, Target. Your name must work identically.
- **Gogo's two brands:** *Gogo's Real Estate* (original, real estate focus) and *Gogo Bethke* (personal brand). She is slowly migrating everything to Gogo Bethke as she outgrows the real estate niche into speaking, podcasts, bootcamps, and the [[GoGet'Em Community]].
- **Lesson:** Build a personal brand alongside your business brand early — you'll eventually outgrow the niche one.
- **Two non-negotiable platforms to own:**
  1. **yourname.com** (GoDaddy) — only one exists in the world
  2. **Your Instagram handle** — only one with that exact name
- Facebook and LinkedIn are lower priority — duplicate names can coexist.
- Own the name even if you're not ready to build — someone else can buy it first.
- **Availability check:** GoDaddy for .com → Instagram search for handle. Namechk exists but Instagram direct search is more reliable.
- **Auto-renew on GoDaddy** (~$11/year). If you lapse, someone can buy your domain out from under your live website.
- **New brand/venture checklist:** Before building anything — check GoDaddy + Instagram. If available, buy both immediately. Then breathe.
- **Pro tip:** Buy your children's name.com now. ~$11/year. They can build on it later.
