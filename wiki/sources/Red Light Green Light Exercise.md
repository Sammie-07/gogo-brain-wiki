# Red Light Green Light Exercise

> Short-form tutorial. [[Gogo Bethke]] explains her clipboard/notebook exercise for auditing every daily task and categorizing it as either money-making (green) or delegatable (red).

## Key Takeaways

- **The setup:** Carry a clipboard or dedicated notebook from the moment you wake up. Draw a vertical line down the middle — Red on one side, Green on the other.
- **Red light = stop.** Needs to get done, but not by you. Hire it out.
- **Green light = money.** Only you can do it (or it generates more than the cost to have someone else do it). Stay here.
- **Include everything** — not just business tasks. Grocery shopping, errands, personal admin all count.
- **The hiring calculus:** If a task costs more in your time than it produces in revenue, it's a red light. Example: 4 hours of grocery shopping vs. 4 hours of generating 10 clients.
- **Gogo's green lights:** Coaching calls, deal negotiation, social media content, writing emails.
- **Gogo's red lights:** Calendar scheduling (hasn't done her own in 7+ years), logistics, admin back-and-forth.
- The exercise reveals what to hire for — and helps you make the business case for your first (or next) hire.

## Quotes

> "Green is money. Red means stop."

> "If it doesn't make me money, I'm not it."

> "I cannot hire out me going to my coaching calls or me coaching my clients. That has to be done by me."

> "If nothing is making you money, you're only doing red-light things and you need to hire for green."
