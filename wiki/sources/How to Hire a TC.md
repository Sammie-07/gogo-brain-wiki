# How to Hire a TC (Transaction Coordinator)

> Gogo's guide to hiring a Transaction Coordinator — her recommended first hire for any agent looking to scale.

## Key Takeaways

- **"If you don't have an assistant, you are the assistant."** — The TC is the first hire that frees an agent from administrative work so they can focus on dollar-productive activities.
- **TC = Transaction Coordinator:** Manages the paperwork, deadlines, communication with title/lender/other agent from contract to close.
- **Why TC first:** Every closed transaction involves ~150-200 touchpoints. Doing all of them yourself is the fastest way to cap your income.
- **Hiring approach:**
  - Can hire a local TC (in-person or remote)
  - Can use a virtual TC service
  - Gogo recommends starting virtual to test workflow before committing to full-time
- **Pay structure:** Typically per-transaction fee ($300–$500/closing) rather than salary — low risk to start.
- **What to delegate:** All post-contract admin — opening escrow, ordering inspections, tracking contingency dates, preparing closing docs, coordinating all parties.
- **What you keep:** Client relationship, negotiation, new lead generation, listings, showings.
