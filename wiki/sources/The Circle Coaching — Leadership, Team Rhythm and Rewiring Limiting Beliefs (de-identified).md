# The Circle Coaching — Leadership, Team Rhythm & Rewiring Limiting Beliefs (de-identified)

> **NDA note:** This page captures only Gogo's generalizable coaching frameworks from private [[The Circle]] calls. All client names, locations, businesses, numbers, and personal circumstances have been removed. The raw transcript is not stored anywhere in the brain.

## Key Takeaways

- **Leadership emotional reality:** most people will not act on free training and mentorship — high achievers wrongly assume everyone shares their drive. "Meet people where they're at," and let some stay "happily miserable." You control your own effort, not other people's results.
- **Give to get:** community is reciprocal — you must partake, show up, and contribute before value flows back. *"If you want a village, you have to be a villager."*
- **The "10 points of good" model:** put 10 points of good into the world and you're owed 10 back; a bad day is a small "ding" that gets repaid, not a subtraction from what you earned. Frames setbacks so they don't feel like being shorted.
- **Service is the engine** (Zig Ziglar): "You can have anything you want in life if you help enough other people get what they want." Reframe hardship as curriculum — "if you really want to learn it, teach it."
- **"It's lonely at the top" is a feature:** being a top-1% producer by definition makes you different from 99% of people. If everyone were that driven, driven would be average.
- **Vet advisors by lived experience, not credentials:** only take money/tax/coaching advice from someone who has "been there, done that" (e.g., is your financial advisor actually wealthy; do they own investment property?). Textbook knowledge without lived results is "blind leading blind."

### Running team meetings that people show up for

- Anchor meetings to a **fixed recurring rhythm** (e.g., the same weekly training day) so planning is easy on you but feels fresh to attendees. **Rotate themes** across the weeks: scripts & role-play, brokerage/state/regulatory updates, operations/tech/social media, open Q&A.
- Make the meeting a **community, not a chore** — co-work, mailers, cold-calling, food and fun together. No "this could have been an email." The right people look forward to it; anyone who can't invest ~4 hours a week isn't the right fit.
- **Film the team** learning, working, and having fun — prospects who see that energy ask to join. Meetings double as recruiting marketing.
- Give the team a **reusable asset** (e.g., a year-long training outline) so recurring meetings are low-effort to plan and never feel unstructured.

### Delegating content the right way

- Get **Meta-verified** to unlock Instagram **shared access** — an assistant can post and edit without your password (and verification signals legitimacy and protects against impersonation).
- Use a **work-only device** that auto-uploads footage to a cloud folder (e.g., Dropbox/shared album); **"favorite" the approved shots** into a shared album so editors only ever touch content you've pre-vetted.
- Platforms' algorithms favor content **edited natively** in their own apps.

### Rewiring limiting beliefs

- **"The opposite can be true" exercise** (via Tony Robbins): write a limiting belief you hold as true, then write its exact opposite and an essay defending the opposite. A "truth" is just a thought you accepted and repeated.
- **Elephant-and-stake / "hypnotic rhythm":** beliefs accepted in early childhood stay wired in even after they stop being true; ~98% of daily thoughts are repeats, under 2% are new. Most people never break the loop alone — which is why immersive breakthrough events and outside help (coach, therapist) matter. Treat mindset work, verification, and coaching as business expenses, not vanity costs.
