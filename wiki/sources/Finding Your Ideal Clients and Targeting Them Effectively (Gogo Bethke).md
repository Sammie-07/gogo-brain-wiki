# Finding Your Ideal Clients and Targeting Them Effectively

> Once you've chosen your niche, the next step is community infiltration — find every group of people who care about that niche and get yourself in front of them. Gogo's four-question framework using lakefront as the live example.

## Key Takeaways

- **Niche content first:** Whatever you specialize in, create content around ALL aspects of that niche — not just real estate. Lakefront = water temperatures, bacteria alerts, local news, fishing tournaments, prices, new listings hitting the market. Everything about the lake.
- **The four questions:** Once you know your niche, answer these in order:
  1. **Who are they?** Who has a natural interest in this niche? (Lakefront = fishermen, boaters, water sports people, fish communities)
  2. **Where are they?** Every community has a home — Facebook groups, clubs, tournaments, local events
  3. **How can I find them?** Join those groups. Show up at those events. Become a face.
  4. **How can I talk to them?** Sponsor, host, admin, bring the Coors Lights — any mechanism to get in conversation
- **Community infiltration — go one step further:** Don't just join the club. Call the admin. Ask: "Can I speak? Can I be a sponsor? Can I hold events? Can I sponsor the tournament?" They always say yes.
- **Live example:** Bass fishing tournament every Wednesday on the lake → call the organizer → bring Gatorades/water/Coors Lights → become a fixture in that community → they all know you as the lake agent.
- **ChatGPT shortcut:** "I'm in Pinckney, Michigan. There's a group of lakes I want to specialize in. What should I post? Who are the people I need to talk to? Where do I find them?" → ChatGPT gives you the full playbook.
- **Slow down first:** "If you just slowed down for five minutes and really thought about who has an interest in [your niche], you'd start writing them out immediately. You're smart."

## Quotes

> "You just want to get yourself in front of those people who have an interest in that lake if you want to be known for lakefront property."

> "Slow down enough to know what you want and then ask yourself these questions: Where are they? Who are they? How can I find them? How can I talk to them? And then start doing those things."

> "The more specific you are, the better ChatGPT can help you."
