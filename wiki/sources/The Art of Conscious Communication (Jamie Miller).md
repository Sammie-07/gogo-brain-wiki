# The Art of Conscious Communication (Jamie Miller)

> Team GoGo open monthly community meeting. Jamie Miller — communication expert. Topic: conscious vs. unconscious communication — how most people communicate on autopilot and what it costs them in business, relationships, and leadership.

## Key Takeaways

- **Conscious vs. unconscious communication.** Most communication is autopilot — default patterns learned in childhood, activated under stress. Conscious communication = deliberate choice of words, tone, and presence.
- **Awareness before change.** You can't change what you don't see. Step one: identify your patterns (what works and what doesn't).
- **Communication as leadership.** How leaders talk to their teams determines team performance. Unconscious leadership communication creates confusion, fear, and underperformance. Psychological safety = product of conscious leadership language.
- **Words create reality.** The stories we tell ourselves and others become lived experience. "I have to prospect" vs. "I get to connect with people today" — same action, different emotional reality.
- **Self-communication matters.** The way you talk to yourself is also conscious communication. Self-talk under stress defaults to least conscious patterns.
- **In real estate:** client communication (how you say things matters as much as what you say), team culture (clarity + consciousness), market conversations (confident communication creates client confidence).
- **Why Gogo brought Jamie in.** Gogo herself hired Zee Slingsby for a "communication reset" — recognizing that even seasoned teachers benefit from dedicated attention to how they speak. Jamie's work reflects this same belief.
- **Interactive format.** Jamie uses two-way guidance: teach → open for questions around situations participants face. Everyone wins when questions are asked in real time.

## Quotes

> Jamie's opening question: "Before I start teaching you guys how to communicate more consciously — how many of you actually believe your ability to communicate is critical for what you want to achieve in life?"
