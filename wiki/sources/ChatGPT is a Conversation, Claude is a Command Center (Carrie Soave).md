# ChatGPT is a Conversation. Claude is a Command Center.

> A live workshop replay in which [[Carrie Soave]] positions Claude as an AI operating system (not a chatbot) and walks entrepreneurs and realtors through its five layers, an autonomy spectrum, and the setup that runs her own company.

## Key Takeaways
- **Chatbot vs. operating system**: ChatGPT answers questions on demand, generates content, and does image/video/voice — but "it stops" at copy-paste; *you* are the bottleneck. It is assistance, not infrastructure. Claude "adds infrastructure" and takes action inside your apps.
- **Claude's differentiators**: coding superiority, PhD-level reasoning, and a massive context window (feed it far more data than ChatGPT).
- **Five layers of Claude**: (1) Claude chat + connectors + skills; (2) Claude in Chrome (browser extension); (3) [[Claude Cowork]] (desktop AI employee with full file-system access); (4) Claude Dispatch (control Cowork from your phone); (5) Claude Code (terminal-based agentic coding). Most people only know layer 1.
- **Connectors** turn Claude into your business's connected data: 100+ available including Gmail, Google Drive, Calendar, Slack, Asana, HubSpot, Close CRM, Stripe, PayPal, Gamma, Figma, Canva, Calendly, Fireflies, Credit Karma — plus newly added full Microsoft (Outlook/Excel/PowerPoint) connectors. Claude auto-pulls the right connector without being told; it acts *inside* another app.
- **Claude in Chrome**: the browser becomes an employee — sees what you see, clicks what you'd click, records workflows (e.g., pull comps from realtor.com/TREB and compile a market report; scrape competitor pricing/positioning for a competitive analysis).
- **Claude Cowork = delegation, not chat**: breaks complex work into subtasks, runs parallel work streams, delivers finished outputs to your file system, and can *schedule/repeat* tasks — i.e., automation of autonomous workflows. Example: pull Stripe revenue + Google ad spend, calculate ROAS by campaign, build a five-slide deck, save to Dropbox before a 9 a.m. meeting.
- **Autonomy Spectrum (L0–L3)**: L0 chatbot (type/respond, copy/paste — where most people are); L1 assistant (connectors read data, draft outputs); L2 agentic (Cowork + Dispatch plan, execute, deliver multi-step tasks — what the workshop demonstrated); L3 fully autonomous (scheduled agents running without you; AI as governance layer — where her paid "AI Solution" takes you).
- **Operators vs. Spectators**: operators build scale without headcount, own infrastructure, let AI execute while they govern, and compound leverage; spectators rent tools, patch gaps, react, and manually copy-paste. The gap widens weekly.
- **Cost-of-manual-work math**: 15+ hours/week on AI-handleable tasks ≈ $78,000/year before lost deals and cold leads — over $200,000 in lost productivity.
- **Cowork folder structure**: a `Claude Cowork` folder with subfolders `about me`, `outputs`, `projects`, `templates`. The `about me` folder holds markdown files (about me, company + org charts, writing style) generated collaboratively by asking Claude to interview you. Good outputs get saved as reusable templates.
- **Global instructions** (Cowork-tab settings, separate from universal custom instructions): e.g., "before every task, read every file in about-me.md; never read outputs or templates unless pointed to a specific file" — because every unnecessary read costs tokens/money.
- **Markdown = token efficiency**: give Claude `.md` files; they are more "bite-sized," use fewer characters, and prevent context rot / token bloat.
- **LLM-portable business profile**: she asks ChatGPT to export custom instructions + memories + relevant past conversations into one document, then uploads it to any LLM (Claude, Perplexity, Grok) and updates it monthly as her business evolves — insurance against a deleted account.
- **Claude plan pricing**: the ~$200/month Max plan is recommended for serious infrastructure ("you'll save thousands on salary"); Carrie runs ~$140/month (Canadian pricing); don't go below the $20/month tier or you'll hit session limits and run out of credits. Models: **Opus** for high-level CEO work, **Sonnet** as the everyday model (less token usage, quicker), **Haiku** for fast, low-thought responses.
- **Use cases**: realtor listing package from a CMA (MLS-compliant + Instagram lifestyle + email versions, 25 best photos auto-selected); AI-staffed "employees" (AI COO, CMO, sales manager, content manager) that run lead flow, content, market reports, and CRM updates. Adjacent tools mentioned: [[Whisper Flow]] (voice dictation, "haven't typed in a year"), HeyGen + Seedance for AI video twins.
