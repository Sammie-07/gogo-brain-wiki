# Finding Your Ideal Client for Buyers and Sellers (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo uses a fishing/whale metaphor to explain why most agents chase the wrong clients — they're fishing in the wrong pond. Full ChatGPT prompt framework to define your ideal client down to age, income, motivation, and social platform.

## Key Takeaways

- **Fishing in the wrong pond:** If you want million-dollar clients but your content targets $25K tiny homes, you'll never catch a $3–4M whale. Your content determines who you attract. You must align your marketing with the client you actually want.
- **Most agents haven't defined their ideal client.** They chase commissions instead of pausing to identify who their lowest-hanging fruit actually is. Once defined, you can move your entire marketing plan around them.
- **Spaghetti vs. clockwork:** Without an ideal client, marketing is spaghetti on the wall — random commissions when things stick. With one, it works like clockwork: you know who they are, where to find them, and what to say.
- **The ChatGPT Prompt — 4 sections:**
  1. **Demographics** — Age, gender, marital status, family size, income level, occupation
  2. **Psychographics** — Desires/goals in buying/selling, pain points, desired lifestyle (city/suburb/luxury/land), values when choosing an agent
  3. **Buying & Selling Motivation** — Why are they transacting now? First home, growing family, empty nester, relocation? What are their biggest challenges? (e.g., cash-poor first-timers → negotiate appliances into deal)
  4. **Social Media & Online Behavior** — Which platforms do they use? What content engages them? What real estate questions do they ask online?
- **Closing prompt to paste into ChatGPT:** *"Based on these details, what specific content should I create to attract these clients? And where should I focus my marketing efforts — online platforms, lead generation, ads, lead magnets?"*
- **Platform-to-client matching:** Elderly clients → not TikTok. Young first-timers → TikTok. Knowing your client tells you where to spend your time.
- **The 7-year rule:** People are on the market every ~7 years on average. For 6.5 of those years they don't care about real estate. Sprinkle in entertaining non-RE content to keep them following you through the off-years. Otherwise they unfollow after closing.
- **Gogo's own ideal client evolution:** At 25–35, her clients were young moms she met at school pickups (shared life stage). Now mid-40s, her clients are established, can afford larger homes. Completely different demographics — same principle applied.
- **Lowest-hanging fruit = people just like you right now.** You share their life stage, their problems, their social circles. That shared experience is the trust foundation.
- **Redo the exercise every 5–10 years** as you and your audience change. What worked 10 years ago may fail today — not because the strategy is wrong, but because you've changed and no longer inhabit the same world as your old ideal client.

## Quotes

> "If you want to sell million-dollar homes, but you keep talking about the starter tiny houses, you're never going to catch a $3–4 million whale. You're fishing in the wrong pond."

> "Most realtors are just too busy trying to chase the next commission. They don't slow down to figure this out."

> "We all have to go to our lowest hanging fruit and your lowest hanging fruit is who you are right now."

> "You might be wondering why something that worked 10 years ago doesn't work today — it is because you changed, your audience changed, which means you have to change with it."
