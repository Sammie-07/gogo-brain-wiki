# The Circle Coaching — Listings as a Lead Engine, Niche Clarity & the Value Ladder (de-identified)

> **NDA note:** This page captures only Gogo's generalizable coaching frameworks from a private [[The Circle]] call. All client names, locations, businesses, numbers, and personal circumstances have been removed. The raw transcript is not stored anywhere in the brain.

## Key Takeaways

### Listings as the lead engine

- **"You list, you last."** Listings are the durable asset and the top of every lead funnel. Build a strong personal brand so you never depend on a third-party portal to reach clients or fulfill fiduciary duty.
- **Every listing should produce two buyers and three commissions:** the buyer of that home, plus a buyer who buys a comparable home nearby after visiting the open house (≈50% co-op commission earned without doing the listing work — the "triple dip").
- To get more buyers, **get more listings** and have a licensed teammate/buyer agent hold them open — you don't have to sit open houses personally, and you still capture the referral/split.
- **Fiduciary duty on the listing side** = sell for the most money, in the least time, with the least hassle. Master that and external market/portal chaos stops mattering.
- When production drops in one area, energy has shifted elsewhere — **re-allocate time and marketing back** to the neglected area (want more buyers → make buyer-focused content and activities).

### Buyer-generation cadence & open-house follow-up

- A full marketing week: a **"tour of the day" video ~3×/week** (across three chosen specialties), **open houses both weekend days**, and buyer content (rate updates, tips) **2–3×/week**.
- **Open-house follow-up system:** before leaving the driveway, record individual **30-second personalized videos to every visitor, by name**, using the same script — update on turnout/interest, invite them to make an offer through their agent, and offer to represent or show similar homes if unrepresented. "Buyers are liars" — follow up with everyone, even those who claim to have an agent.
- Hold listings open every weekend until sold; run a **Monday team meeting** reviewing weekend opens (how many held, traffic per house, follow-up completion).

### Niche clarity

- **Niche down one segment at a time** — run the niche exercise separately for listings, then buyers, then any sub-segment; never blend three niches in one pass. The more specific the inputs, the sharper the output (bios, marketing ideas, a 90-day posting schedule a VA can execute). See [[Niche Finder]].

### The value ladder / funnel

- Build a **value ladder**: free (top) → low-ticket → mid-ticket → high-ticket. **"Nothing is free"** — free content is exchanged for data (name, email, phone). You don't own your social platforms, but you **own your email list** — give freely online to capture emails, then run an email sequence to convert.
- **People buy the middle tier** (free feels beneath them; the top feels risky) — position what you most want to sell as the middle option.
- **Price signals value** — a dollar amount attached to a product becomes its perceived worth; under-pricing premium content makes people distrust it. Keep the funnel simple; don't proliferate products (more offers = constantly marketing everything).
- **Match the offer format to the audience's real constraint** — if the ideal client is time-starved, deliver via recorded/app-based/on-demand content (or late-night live sessions), and sell the recording afterward as an evergreen asset.
- **Diversify income pillars** (commissions, team splits/referrals, revenue share, coaching, affiliate, stock/equity) all feeding one "bank account." **Own your numbers** — state them plainly, don't hedge.
