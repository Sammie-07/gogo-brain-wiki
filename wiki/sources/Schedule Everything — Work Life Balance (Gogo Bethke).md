# Schedule Everything — Work-Life Balance (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo's training on the calendar as the master tool for achieving a full life — business, family, health, and friendships — without sacrificing any of them. Core principle: schedule freedom, or someone else will schedule over it.

## Key Takeaways

- **"We can have it all":** Gogo's thesis — a thriving business, a healthy marriage, a healthy weight, real friendships, and present parenting are all achievable simultaneously. The mechanism is the calendar, not willpower.
- **Schedule freedom as "scheduled freedom":** Freedom doesn't mean no schedule — it means knowing exactly when you're free, and knowing that freedom is protected. "I still love my freedom. It just has to be a scheduled freedom."
- **Calendar color-coding system:**
  - Red = work appointments (must attend)
  - Blue = family or kids
  - Green = travel (out of home)
  - Purple = birthdays
  - Yellow = guests in the house (blocks availability for calls)
  Google Calendar on desktop and phone — phone is the primary view, desktop only to click links.
- **Schedule friends or lose them:** "If I don't put it in my calendar, somebody else is going to take the spot." Gogo schedules her closest friends explicitly — missing a best friend's birthday because she didn't know the date was a turning point.
- **Pre-scheduled appointment slots:** Every repeating appointment type gets a permanent slot — podcast every Tuesday at 11am, buyer consultations, listing presentations. People book into pre-existing slots; Gogo doesn't schedule reactively.
- **Add an accountability partner to tasks you'll skip:** For workouts or tasks she dislikes (like reading contracts), Gogo adds another person to the appointment. If she's the only one on the appointment, she'll reschedule. If someone else is waiting, she shows up.
- **Delegate calendar management:** Alejandra (executive assistant) manages the calendar. For general call requests, Gogo never looks at her own calendar — she routes callers to Alejandra who blocks options and sends confirmation.
- **Romania connection:** Gogo references Romania as part of her personal context — the discipline to schedule everything was shaped by her upbringing.

## Quotes

> "I literally schedule time with my friends. If I don't put it in my calendar, somebody else is going to take the spot."

> "I still love my freedom. It just has to be a scheduled freedom."

> "If you're the only one on the appointment, you'll find 10 other things to do. Add someone else — now you'll show up."
