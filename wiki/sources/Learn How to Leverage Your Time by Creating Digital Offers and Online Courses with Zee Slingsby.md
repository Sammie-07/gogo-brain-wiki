# Learn How to Leverage Your Time by Creating Digital Offers and Online Courses with Zee Slingsby

> Day 2 of GoGet'Em Community money week. Zee Slingsby — Gogo's course creation coach — teaches the full framework for creating and selling digital products and online courses. Opens after Gogo reveals the group received $244,000 in tax credits in less than 24 hours ($580,000+ by Day 2 morning).

## Key Takeaways

**Why Digital Products**
- Market boomed after 2020; projected to grow through at least 2035; not yet stabilizing
- 100% your own asset — no employer, no platform owns it
- Work from anywhere; time invested once pays forever
- Creates qualified leads for existing business
- No fulfillment cost (digital downloads deliver themselves)
- Zero time trade-off per additional sale once created

**Types of Digital Products**
- Online courses (video-based, platform-hosted, self-paced = passive)
- Digital assets / PDF guides (created in Canva, auto-delivered via email)
- Lead magnets / freebies (free digital products that grow email list)
- Mini-courses vs. signature courses (different price points, different depth)

**The Pre-Launch Strategy — Sell Before You Finish**
- Start marketing before the product is built
- Create an RSVP landing page (any email platform has this) to collect interested names/emails
- Offer founding member / early-bird discount for pre-launch buyers
- Disclose: "Access available on [date] — early price for trusting me now"
- Benefits: marketing is energizing when you are excited; if someone buys, the fire is lit under you to finish; if nobody buys yet, no emotional sunk cost
- Gogo's bootcamp social media course started at $49 pre-launch; sold for $3,000; early buyers kept $49 forever
- Zee launched all her courses on early bird — sold before they were done — across four signature courses, six mini-courses, four masterminds
- The 30-day pre-launch story guide: daily checklist of what to talk about on Stories during pre-launch

**MRR Warning**
- Do not buy someone else's course to resell (master resell rights / MRR)
- Zee client: started with MRR, saw no long-term success; created her own course, brought in $38,000 in the first two months

**Platform Recommendations**
- Stan Store: easiest to get started same day. 30% higher conversion than other platforms per Amy Gregory. $99/month or free trial. MP4 upload from Zoom or Loom — no reformatting.
- Etsy: lower ROI, harder entry, but useful for researching what people will pay for in your niche
- Amazon KDP: for printable products — Amazon prints and ships, you upload the file. No inventory.
- Canva Pro: design all digital assets. Templates exist for journals, recipe ebooks, planners, anything.

**Target Audience — The Three Boxes**
- Box 1 (Pain): what thoughts are running through your ideal client's head when their head hits the pillow? Get granular. Not "struggling to sign clients" — more like "I posted and nobody cared, I must not be good at this, I can't pay my bills."
- Box 3 (Outcome): where do they want to be? Not "a commission" — more like "I feel confident, I know where I belong, I can plan a vacation."
- Box 2 (Program): what you teach them to get from Box 1 to Box 3
- Marketing mistake: most people only talk about Box 2. Nobody resonates. Talk to Box 1 and Box 3.

**Pricing Tiers**
- Low ticket ($9–$197): self-explanatory product name, click-to-buy, no sales call required. Emotional impulse purchase. Revenue comes from volume or upselling.
- Mid-range ($500–$700): expect some DMs with questions. May or may not require a live component.
- High ticket (mastermind, 1:1): definite time trade-off — you are trading hours for money.
- Zero time trade-off at any volume for low/mid self-paced products

**Income Math Examples**
- PDF guide at $197 x 30 sales = $5,900 (zero fulfillment, zero time trade-off)
- Same guide x 100 sales = $20,000
- Course at $1,500 x 5 sales = $7,500
- Keep selling as long as the content is relevant; graduates become the audience for next-level offers

**Digital Asset Ideas from Research (Etsy)**
- Printable planners specific to niche
- Real estate commission calculators, buyer guides, seller guides
- Elf on the Shelf kit (one client crushes it every Christmas)
- Journal prompts, habit trackers
- Nursery wall art prints
- Baby shower games
- European vacation packing list

**Social Media Strategy for Course Creators**
- Feed / Reels: bring new people to your page
- Stories: convert existing followers to buyers. If you have a following, this is where sales happen.
- 90-day rule: consistent activity for 90 days before seeing real ROI when starting from scratch. Inconsistency for 365 days ≠ consistency for 90 days.
- 100 story views = 100 people. More than most door-knockers reach in a week.
- Paid ads: add fuel to fire only after organic marketing is proven. Do not run ads to an unproven product.

**Email Marketing**
- High ROI; automatable; best for upselling and cross-selling
- FlowDesk: Zee's recommended platform. Create RSVP landing pages, automated sequences.
- Lead magnet grows the email list organically from social media
- Automated nurture sequence starts immediately when someone joins; no manual work required

**Coaching Boundaries**
- Do not offer free coaching without clear boundaries — 1–2 sessions max with expectation of testimonial
- Gogo pre-charges coaching clients 3 months ahead; they have skin in the game and cannot no-show without forfeit
- At sufficient scale: close 1:1 coaching entirely. Group programs serve more people per hour.
- "An hour of my time is an hour of my life." Gogo would need to charge $10,000/hour for 1:1 to be worth it at her current income level.

**Niche and Personal Brand**
- A personal brand can contain multiple facets (real estate + mom + fashion) — this is your personal brand and can all coexist
- Course niching: the more specific the niche, the more a course stands out. "Physical therapist for level-10 gymnasts" beats "physical therapist course."
- Brick-and-mortar businesses can extend into digital worldwide audience — dentist with 2,000+ students teaching how to cure cavities naturally
