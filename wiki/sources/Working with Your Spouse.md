# Working with Your Spouse: Tips and Challenges

> Gogopreneur podcast. Gogo and Dwayne co-host a Q&A walking through how they manage life, business, and marriage as a couple. Most detailed public record of Dwayne's background and their division of roles.

## Key Takeaways

**Dwayne's background:**
- 20 years in Corporate America (Automotive industry). Left after Gogo's revenue share income could replace his salary — though he actually quit "a little bit earlier" than planned, which fired them both up to close the gap faster.
- Retired at age 40. Described as: "She works, he entertains for a living and goes fishing."
- Oldest son is **Type 1 diabetic** — company health insurance was a key reason Dwayne stayed in Corporate America as long as he did. When eXp Realty launched health insurance, that cleared the way for him to exit.

**Division of labor:**
- **Gogo:** makes the money, all coaching/content/business operations, all meetings.
- **Dwayne:** all household finance — pays all bills, insurance, property taxes in escrow, checks mail, manages personal investment tracking. Handles kids' schedules, doctor appointments, school logistics.
- Both have high **DiSC "D" (Dominant)** personalities. At home, Dwayne deliberately reduces his D and lets Gogo lead. *"The reason we lasted this long is because at work I'm a high D, but when I come home I reduce my D."*
- "That's not my job" rule: if it's in someone else's lane, Gogo says so immediately and means it. *"I make the money; you spend the money."*

**Financial management:**
- Both names on every bank account (business and personal) — full transparency, no hidden money.
- Purchase threshold for discussion: approximately $350–400+.
- Monthly numbers meeting (Gogo + Kristy) for business finances.
- Quarterly board of directors meeting — Dwayne is invited but acknowledges spotty attendance.
- Dwayne builds personal finance spreadsheets tracking all properties: equity, loan balances, interest rates, assets vs. liabilities.
- "If you don't track it you can't grow it" — applies to net worth and property equity, not just income.

**Kids and business:**
- Oldest: **Koby**. Youngest: **Duke** (name confirmed here).
- Pay kids to work in the business — legitimate up to ~$12,000/year in Michigan before exhausting the threshold.
- When Duke was a teenager: filmed a reel for Gogo, got 3.8M views. Paid ~$800 for ~10 minutes of work.
- Long-term vision: one day hand the business to the kids. Gogo thinks one will be the "numbers person" (behind scenes) and one will be the "front-facing" person.

**Marriage philosophy:**
- Key advice: **forgive AND forget** — from Gogo's grandfather's 50+ year marriage. Keeping score of past grievances poisons the relationship.
- **Honor each other's ideal life.** Gogo's revelation: if she expects Dwayne to want the same things she wants, she has to be willing to go bass fishing every day. Instead: accept each other's vision of an ideal life.
- **Support is non-negotiable.** *"If your spouse is not your biggest cheerleader, then who is?"*
- Accept each other as you both change — don't stay attached to who the person was at year one.
- Push each other: "contentment" is the enemy of growth.

**Living situation:**
- Primary home: Florida (large house, has Mandy as house assistant full-time).
- Michigan home: family property Gogo's father-in-law built, ~19 years in family. As of recording: listing for sale, plan is to buy 3 townhouses in South Carolina with proceeds.

## Quotes

> "I work. He entertains for a living and goes fishing."

> "I make the money, you spend the money."

> "That's not my job. And I know it sounds mean — but in the end of the day, if I start adding small little b-size pieces outside my lane, I have to relay it to him. Just twice the work."

> "If your spouse is not your biggest cheerleader, then who? Entrepreneurship is freaking hard."

> "Honor the other person's idea of an ideal life."
