# Unlocking Negotiation Secrets with Chris Voss

> Gogopreneur episode with Chris Voss — former FBI lead hostage negotiator and author of *Never Split the Difference*. Full negotiation toolkit adapted for real estate and business contexts.

## Key Takeaways

- **Chris Voss:** Former FBI lead hostage negotiator. Author of *Never Split the Difference*. Now teaches negotiation to business professionals.
- **Tactical empathy:** Identify how someone feels without taking on that feeling yourself. Say "it sounds like you feel X" — not "I understand how you feel." Identification ≠ sympathy.
- **Mirroring:** Repeat the last 3 words (or key words) of what someone said as a question. Encourages them to elaborate. Keeps you listening, not talking.
- **Labeling:** Name the emotion explicitly. "It feels like this is really frustrating you." Labeling diffuses the emotion by making it visible and acknowledged.
- **3 conflict personality types:**
  1. **Assertive** — wants to be heard; give them the mic first
  2. **Analyst** — needs data and logic; skip emotion, lead with facts
  3. **Accommodator** — wants harmony; never mistake silence for agreement
- **Amygdala hijack:** The brain's threat response doesn't distinguish between a saber-tooth tiger and a difficult boss. Same physiological response. Tactical empathy bypasses it.
- **31% smarter in positive frame:** Sean Achor research — people in a positive emotional state are 31% more cognitively effective. Creating positive frame in negotiation = opponent makes better decisions for both parties.
- **"I found something fascinating":** Gogo's own bad-news delivery protocol — Chris Voss validated it on the episode. Opens with curiosity, prevents emotional spike.
- **Barbara Corcoran insight:** "Give me time to think about it" — the most powerful response when pressured. Never make a decision under pressure.
