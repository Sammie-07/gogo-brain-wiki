# Revenue Share — How It Works (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo's personal account of how revenue share works at eXp, why she chose it over profit share, her actual monthly numbers from 2019–present, and why she recommends tracking it from day one.

## Key Takeaways

- **Why eXp:** Went from Real Estate One → Keller Williams (for profit share) → eXp (for revenue share). Realized: "If something is not profitable, there is no profit to share." Revenue share pays regardless of brokerage profitability.
- **How it works:** Attract agents to eXp. When those agents do transactions, eXp collects revenue. A portion of that revenue is paid back to the agent who attracted them — on or around the **20th of every month**, in USD.
- **Timeline reality:** Nobody switches brokerages on day one. It takes months for agents to join, start transacting, close deals, and for your revenue share to begin — be patient.
- **All agents attracted organically** — 100% from social media, no paid ads.
- **Gogo's actual revenue share numbers (shared under disclaimer — for context only):**
  - Joined eXp: November 1, 2018
  - First revenue share check: **March 2019 — $1,422.75** (covered her $1,300 mortgage)
  - April 2019: $2,362
  - May 2019: $5,573
  - Year 1 total: **$44,000**
  - Year 2 total: **$119,000**
  - Grows every year; seasonal (peaks June–August, low in December)
- **Revenue share is one of three income streams** that made Gogo a millionaire and multi-millionaire: (1) commissions, (2) digital products, (3) revenue share
- **Two audience types on your social media** — you have no control over who follows you. Some are buyers/sellers (commission income); some are agents (revenue share income or referral income). Both exist in the same audience. Why not monetize both?
- **Revenue share = income stability.** Commissions are volatile (could be $90K one month, $0 the next two). Revenue share hits on the 20th every month like clockwork.
- **Blank tracker available** below the video — download, make a copy, track from day one. "The only reason you should ever look back is to see how far you've come."
