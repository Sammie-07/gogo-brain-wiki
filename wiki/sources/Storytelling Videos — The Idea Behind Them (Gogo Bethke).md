# Storytelling Videos — The Idea Behind Them (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo explains the philosophy and strategy behind storytelling videos (Reels/long-form posts — not Instagram Stories). Why to create them, the algorithm length guidelines, 10 specific topic ideas, and the vulnerability principle that makes them work.

## Key Takeaways

### Why Storytelling Videos
1. **Content:** You need it — storytelling is one of the most sustainable content formats.
2. **Attract your people:** *"I'm looking for the people who are looking for me."* Storytelling filters your audience. When people see who you truly are — your work ethic, ideas, achievements, journey — those who align will self-select in. The result: clients who feel like new best friends, not difficult obligations.

> "When you are working with people who are just like you, you never work a day in your life."

3. **Algorithm time-on-profile:** The longer viewers stay, the better the algorithm rewards the post.

### Length Guidelines
- **Ideal:** Under 1.5 minutes (Instagram's sweet spot for Reels)
- **Over 3 minutes:** Instagram restricts distribution to existing followers only — and even then, only a small portion
- **Gogo's take:** She posts long when she has a lot to say. *"The people who need to see it will see it."* — but under 1.5 min is the algorithmic ideal

### Clarification — Storytelling Videos ≠ Instagram Stories
Storytelling videos = **Reels or long-format posts** that live on your profile permanently. Instagram Stories (the 24-hour circles) are a separate, different format.

### 10 Storytelling Video Ideas
1. The moment you realized home means more than a house
2. The hardest day of your career — what happened, how you solved it, what you learned
3. The client who reminded you why your work matters
4. Things you believed about real estate that turned out to be completely wrong
5. The moment you stopped chasing perfection
6. The sliding door moment — one decision that changed your entire life
7. The client who changed you and made you better
8. The first time you felt like an expert
9. The sacrifices nobody sees
10. The vision of the life you are building and who you are doing it for

*Also valid:* First client you ever fired. First client who fired you. What your first closing paid for. Your immigrant story. Your children / homeschool decision. A hobby that defines your community (hockey team → sold every player a house).

### The Vulnerability Principle
People unfollow polished. People stay for real.

Gogo shared a vision she had at a Dr. Joe Dispenza event — worried people would think she was crazy. Instead, a follower DM'd: *"There are not many people who are willing to be vulnerable and transparent in such a public way as you have shown to be."*

> "People really appreciate when you show your true side — your wins and your weaknesses and your dreams and your work ethic to reach those dreams. They kind of ride with you on the journey."

**The trust chain:** Tell your truth → people get to know you → they care for you → they trust you → they buy whatever you're selling → they're your customer for life.

> "Allow people to get to know you. When they get to know you, they love you. When they love you and trust you, they'll buy whatever you are selling and you gain them as a customer for life through trust."

### How to Start
- **Talk from the heart.** Don't script — let something real surface.
- Start with one video. Just one.
- The follow-up GSMC videos cover prompts and specific frameworks for finding story ideas.
