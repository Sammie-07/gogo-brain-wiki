# Social Media Marketing Mindset + Lead Capturing

> February GoGet'Em live training where Gogo Bethke teaches 10 camera-confidence principles, then runs a hands-on workshop showing agents how to turn a CRM listing link into a lead-capturing social post with automated follow-up.

## Key Takeaways
- **10 camera-confidence principles:** (1) don't reintroduce yourself in every post — followers already chose you (only introduce in cold-audience ads); (2) look yourself in the eye on selfie videos so it feels direct to the viewer; (3) stop apologizing before you speak — it makes people question your value; (4) talk to one person, not the internet — call out the specific target in the first sentence; (5) open with tension, not information (weak: "Today I want to talk about buyer leads"; strong: "If you're posting and no one is reaching out, this is the mistake you're making"); (6) stand/sit like you belong there — posture changes psychology; (7) don't over-smile or nervous-laugh — authority comes from controlled energy; (8) speak ~15% slower than feels natural; (9) stop re-watching yourself 20 times — it wastes billable time; (10) make it about them, not you ("if you're struggling with…" not "I'm so excited to share…").
- **Hourly-rate math to kill re-watching:** take last year's total income, divide by 2,080 (work hours/year), then by 60 for a per-minute rate — every rewatch flushes those dollars.
- **Lead-capture mechanic — the missing middle step:** never just paste a raw listing link. Record a short personal video first, then attach the listing link. Posting a bare link gets no traffic (only pity likes from mom/best friend).
- **Grab the link from your CRM:** every agent has a company website with a built-in CRM (database + automated response system). Pull a listing link from [[KVCore/BoldTrail]], Lofty, or the front end of your IDX site (e.g. kristywaker.com). Match the listing's price/type to your niche — a $100K listing attracts $100K buyers.
- **Getting the link from computer to phone:** email/text/message it to yourself; Chrome three-dots → "Create QR code" or "Send to your devices"; on Mac/iPhone use AirDrop or universal copy-paste (copy on phone, paste on computer). Shorten long links with **[[TinyURL]]** (tinyurl.com).
- **Platform link rules:** Facebook allows a direct link in a **feed** post (add video + link in the description); Instagram does NOT allow feed links, so use **Stories** with the **Link sticker** (Stickers → Link → paste URL). Once posted, cross-post the link to LinkedIn and Google.
- **Video best practices:** recap the listing details in the written description too — most viewers watch muted (in a bathroom stall at their corporate job), so cater to both listeners and readers. Add on-screen **captions** (record video → swipe up → Captions filter). Put a plain call to action ("Click the link to see the listing") above the link. "It's really about me and my services… you need to be in it."
- **Lead capture in action (BoldTrail):** first-ever visit shows full listing; after that a visitor sees only ~3 photos, then a "log in with Facebook / Google or enter email + phone" gate. Agent can require phone number. Login pulls in name/email/phone; agent is notified (text instant, dashboard sometimes lagged). This reveals who is looking so you can follow up.
- **Lofty lead-capture settings:** Content → Websites → Settings → Lead Capture → "registration popup trigger rule." Configurable by number of listings viewed or time. If registration isn't turned on, a lead-capturing website captures nothing.
- **Self-built IDX sites (e.g. Panda IDX) usually lack live IDX** (full MLS feed) and real lead capture — live IDX is expensive and rarely on personally-built sites; you'd have to talk to whoever built it.
- **Auto-responder setup — make it sound like YOU:** all CRMs ship generic drip messages that scream "system." Rewrite them, one text and one email at a time (first fires within seconds, then end of day, day 1, day 7, day 14…). The FIRST automated message should be a **video** with a personal intro + call to action. Shoot TWO versions: **vertical** for the text-message auto-response, **horizontal** for the email/computer auto-response.
- **Preferred-vendors list (lead magnet + reciprocity):** husband's idea — you expect everyone to use you as their realtor, so return the favor. Build a directory of 3 vetted vendors per category (movers, painters, title, cleaning, handyman, etc.), starting with friends/family who have side businesses, then supporting local. Give it as a free gift the moment someone hits your account — instant value, credibility, and trust (can be built in Canva). Gail also suggested a white-paper/tips freebie and quarterly newsletters.
- **Market reports / CMAs as follow-up fuel:** set automated market reports (Gogo prefers every 29 days = monthly). System auto-sends CMAs, mostly on the listing side. For past clients, plug their actual address into BoldTrail so they get updates on their specific home. Use the equity gain as a conversation opener ("I just made you $70K in a year — want to pull a HELOC and buy an investment property?").
- **Tracking:** track monthly brand numbers (views/followers per platform) in the **brand tracker** and leads-per-platform in the **lead tracker** — views are worthless (vanity) unless they convert to leads and US dollars; a million views from 17-year-olds who can't buy real estate is worth nothing.
- **Done-With-You package:** upgrade above the default do-it-yourself package. Team NEVER logs into your accounts or asks for passwords (liability) — they guide you or your VA on live twice-weekly tech-support calls while you push the buttons. **$4,800/year**, payable up front, in 2 payments of $2,500, or 3 payments of $1,750; 12 months of access from purchase. Recommendation for the time-strapped: hire a VA and send them to the Done-With-You calls.
- **Housekeeping:** use the community chat (team available M–F 9–5) or support@gogetemcommunity.com; download the app; do the one-time onboarding call; join the 100 Audit Club for a personal brand audit (10 audit calls this year, 10 agents each); use your affiliate link (10% recurring — 10 friends = free account). Nearly 95% of companies/credit cards have an affiliate/"invite a friend" program — get paid every time you market something you love.
