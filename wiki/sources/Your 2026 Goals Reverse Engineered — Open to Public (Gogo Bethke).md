# Your 2026 Goals Reverse Engineered — Open to Public (Gogo Bethke)

> GoGet'Em Open Monthly Training (last Wednesday of each month, 1pm ET, team.team/open). Gogo Bethke teaching. Title: "How to Eat an Elephant One Bite at a Time." Open to anyone — realtors and non-realtors. 70-slide session recorded with lifetime access.

## Key Takeaways

- **Big obnoxious goals.** "If your goals are not scaring you shitless, you didn't set big obnoxious goals. You settled." Most people set lower goals to avoid the risk of failure — they protect their ego at the expense of their results.
- **The elephant metaphor.** Massive scary goals become achievable by eating them one bite at a time — daily/weekly/monthly reverse-engineered actions from the annual target.
- **Reverse engineering math.** Commission-based math for realtors: start with annual income goal, work backward to monthly, weekly, daily actions. Non-realtors adapt the same math to their income model.
- **Open to Public format.** Last Wednesday of every month, 1:00 p.m. Eastern, team.team/open. Goal: let people "try the shoes on before you buy them" — see GoGetEm in action before joining. Regular members also attend every Wednesday at 1pm.
- **Session structure.** 70 slides. Gogo: "Don't freak out about taking notes on everything — just write what speaks to you. That's the thing you need to be working on."
- **Lifelong learner.** "I want to know enough to be dangerous in just about everything that interests me." Attends 3-day events, masterminds, asks questions at every Q&A. Gogo attended a stock investing seminar the same weekend as this training.
- **Time is not money.** "Time is money. Come to find out. But then I also learned that it's not — if time was money then how come some people make billions where others make pennies? It's the same 24 hours."
- **Self-awareness on motivation.** Most people settle because they don't want to disappoint themselves — they know they won't give 100%, so they lower the bar. Gogo names and rejects that pattern.
