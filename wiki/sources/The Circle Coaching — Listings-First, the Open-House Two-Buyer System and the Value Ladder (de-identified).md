# The Circle Coaching — Listings-First, the Open-House Two-Buyer System & the Value Ladder (de-identified)

> Private [[The Circle|Circle]] coaching call. **NDA — raw transcript not retained; all member PII removed.** Only [[Gogo Bethke]]'s generalizable teachings are captured.

## Key Teachings (de-identified)

### Niche & positioning (folds into [[Niche Finder]])
- **Run the [[Niche Finder]] one niche at a time** — never blend segments (listings vs. buyers vs. upsizers) into one report. Answer the 10 questions for one segment, then re-run for the next. Specific answers (price point, seller age band, motivation) produce a sharper niche + **8 marketing ideas** + a **90-day posting schedule** a VA can load into a scheduler.
- **Team leaders still need a defined listing-side niche** — it feeds the whole team even if buyer agents catch "all buyers."
- **Solve the avatar's objection *inside* the offer** — if your ideal client is "too busy to learn," build the offer around that (async/recorded, late-night live, "watch anytime") and say so directly.

### Listings-first strategy (folds into [[Lead Generation]], [[Real Estate Teams]])
- **"You list, you last."** As long as you're the listing agent, you stay durable in any market.
- **Every listing should produce two buyers → three commissions:** the seller side, the buyer of *that* home, and a buyer who buys a comparable nearby (already shopping that area/price at the open house). Buyer agents holding it open lets the rainmaker "triple dip" with no extra prospecting.
- **Listing-side fiduciary duty:** sell for the most money, in the least time, with the least hassle. Master that and market noise stops mattering.
- **Own your brand and your data** so you don't depend on third-party portals for leads or distribution.

### Open-house two-buyer system (folds into [[Open Houses]], [[Lead Generation]])
- **Write weekend open houses into the listing agreement** — held every Saturday and Sunday until sold.
- **Personalized video follow-up to every attendee, by name**, recorded in the driveway before leaving (~30 sec, same rhythm): greet by name → thank them → give the update (traffic count, interest) → "if you want to offer, tell your realtor" → "if you don't have one, I'd love to represent you" → "if this isn't the home, I'd love to show you others in the same area and price range." **Never one blast** — one individual video per attendee/couple.
- **Monday 9:00 a.m. team meeting** to review the weekend: homes held, traffic counts, and whether *every* attendee was followed up. "Buyers are liars" — follow up with all of them regardless of what they claimed. Delegate open houses to a licensed team member and still earn the split (~50%) without taking family time.

### Re-activating a neglected income stream (folds into [[Productivity]], [[Mindset]])
- **When you pour focus into one area, another gives** — production drops where your attention left; the fix is simply re-allocating time/energy/marketing back.
- **Buyer-generation weekly menu:** "tour of the day" video 3×/week (pick ~3 specialties) + open houses both weekend days + buyer social content (e.g., rate updates) 2–3×/week → fills seven days and produces "more buyers than you know what to do with." Do more talking-head video; keep niching down.

### The value ladder / funnel (folds into [[Wealth Building]], [[Personal Branding]])
- **One bank account, many income pillars:** commissions, affiliate income, team referral/split fees, revenue share, equity/stock programs, coaching.
- **Funnel tiers:** *Free* (webinars, social, lead magnets) → *low ticket* (~$25–$80/mo membership) → *medium* → *high ticket*.
- **"Free" is never free — you collect data (name, email, phone).** "We are never free. We are very expensive." You don't own the social platforms but you own your email list — give generously online to grow it; an email/follow-up sequence converts opt-ins up the ladder.
- **People buy the middle** — structure the ladder so the product you most want to sell is the middle option (free feels beneath them; top feels risky before trust).
- **Counter-lesson — price signals value.** "A dollar amount tied to a product in people's mind — that's the value of the product." A low-ticket membership can be *harder* to sell when your audience is anchored to your premium pricing. **Don't run multiple products at once** — focus on one.

### AI "second brain" & accountability loop (folds into [[The Circle]], [[AI for Real Estate]])
- Gogo built an AI "second brain" trained on ~15 years of knowledge *and* her voice; members chat with it any time for after-hours questions. The [[Niche Finder]] runs two ways — free via your own AI with provided prompt verbiage, or paid via her trained brain (priced because it consumes her data). Members are encouraged to **build their own AI brain** by feeding it their coaching transcripts.
- **Accountability dashboard / blueprint loop:** each member gets a portal with a "blueprint" built on a clarity call. Weekly cadence: Tuesday coaching → homework to the dashboard → Friday check-in → mark tasks complete with **detailed notes** (incl. blockers) so the AI tracks progress over time. Year-end review surfaces done vs. outstanding and informs renewal.

### Leverage & scaling (folds into [[Real Estate Teams]], [[Mindset]])
- **"2× is harder than 10×"** — a 2× goal lets you keep doing it yourself; a true multiple forces systems and leverage. Math: four team members × 40 hrs = 160 hrs/week running without you.
- **"Subtract before you add"** — decide what you'll stop doing to make room to grow. "Growth without it is just more chaos."
- **Own your numbers** — state them plainly and be proud; don't hide behind vague figures.
