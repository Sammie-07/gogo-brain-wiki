# Why Local Event Reels Matter for Realtors

> Gogo's framework for filming local events, building community positioning, and turning every event into content that drives the next one.

## Key Takeaways

- **Community positioning:** Local events make you the neighborhood connector — people think of you not just for real estate, but for plumbers, school districts, referrals. You become the face of the area.
- **Storytelling backbone:** Wide shots, crowd energy, setup details, and transitions keep viewers watching. B-roll is everything — quick snippets of food, drinks, people, entry, signup, stage. Not slow sweeping buffet shots.
- **Social proof through faces:** Show who showed up. "Monkey does, monkey do — if they see Jessica at your event, they'll come next time."
- **Detail shots matter:** Food, spread, giveaways, brand elements, décor. Capture winners, reactions, candid moments — that's your emotional content.
- **Music sets the tone:** Use fun, trendy music — not melancholy elevator music.
- **Always invite to the next event:** Pre-schedule all events for the full year. When filming the reel, you already know the next date — include a signup link in the reel itself.
- **Consistency builds anticipation:** Show up regularly and your audience starts expecting it. That's when you become unforgettable.
- **Authenticity beats production:** Phone, good light, genuine energy is enough. Interview clients on the spot at events — "Why did you work with me? What are you excited about?"
- **Testimonials at events:** Build a running testimonials page. Tag clients when posting — they'll share it.

### B-Roll Best Practices
- Bring a teenager or professional videographer
- Brief them: you want shots even when you're NOT talking to camera
- Quick snippets > slow pans
- Capture: setup, food, bartender, giveaways, crowd, interactions, hugs, winner moments

### Event Scheduling Framework
- Pre-plan the full year (e.g. quarterly)
- Each reel ends with CTA + link to next event
- Consistency creates anticipation and FOMO
