# Tax Planning For Real Estate Investors 101 (Toby Mathis)

> Toby Mathis, Esq. on the fundamentals of tax planning for real estate investors — capital gains, rental income, depreciation, and structuring investments to minimize tax liability.

## Key Takeaways

- **Tax planning definition**: organizing and managing finances to reduce tax liability — using all available deductions, credits, and investment structures.
- **Key real estate tax tools**:
  - **Depreciation**: write off the cost of property over 27.5 years (residential) or 39 years (commercial) even while it appreciates in value
  - **Capital gains rates**: long-term gains (held 1+ year) taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20% — far lower than ordinary income rates
  - **1031 Exchange**: defer capital gains by rolling proceeds into a like-kind property
  - **Cost segregation**: accelerate depreciation by reclassifying building components for faster write-offs
- **Entity structure** determines how much tax you pay — LLC, S Corp, or partnership each have different implications.
- The goal of tax planning: keep more of what you earn, reinvest the savings, and compound over time.
