### Commission Model

**Before capping** (example: $300k house, 3% commission = $9,000):
- Minus 20% to eXp: $1,800
- Minus $60 E&O (risk management)
- Minus $25 broker review fee
- Minus 5% stock option (optional)
- Agent nets: ~$6,759.25
- ~9 transactions at this price point to reach $16k cap

**After capping** (same $300k transaction):
- No 20% split; $250 transaction fee instead
- Minus $25 broker review fee
- Minus $60 E&O
- Minus 5% stock option
- **Agent nets: $8,231.75** (add $595 transaction fee if charged = $8,826.75)
- $250 reduces to $75 at ICON status

**Fees:**
- $149 startup (includes first month of $85); then $85/month ongoing
- $25 transaction fee + $60 E&O per transaction — only paid when closing
- Referral-only (inactive) license: $50/month — hold license, no active selling; up to 3 personal deals/year; can still build revenue share downline and access health insurance

### ICON Program

- Cap at $16k → close 20 additional transactions in same anniversary year
- Earn all $16,000 cap back as EXPI company stock
- Post-ICON transaction fee drops from $250 to $75
- Example: Yasmin Rogers — one of Gogo's earliest agents, 7x ICON in 7 consecutive years

### Stock Awards (Free — Not the 5% Option)

- First closing each year: $200 in EXPI stock
- When you cap: $400 in EXPI stock
- When your sponsored agent closes their first transaction: $400 in EXPI stock
- 5% stock option (separate, voluntary): buy EXPI at 5% discount using 5% of each commission check

### Natural Cap Matching

Agents leaving other brokerages mid-anniversary-year can request a natural cap match:
- Requires: proof of last commission at 100% + contract showing current anniversary date
- State broker's call — Gogo says it's approved "every time" in her experience
- Strategy note: if the agent can cap AND icon before their anniversary, Gogo recommends paying the cap and earning it back rather than bringing over a partial cap

### Mentorship Program

- New agents' first 3 transactions: 60/40 split (not 80/20)
- The extra 20% goes to company; eXp gives mentor 10% of that 20%
- Example: mentee's $10k commission → mentor earns $1,000 (10% of the 20% extra split)
- After 3 mentored transactions, agent graduates to 80/20 with everyone else

### REMLO (Real Estate Mortgage Loan Originator)

- Become a licensed loan officer alongside real estate work
- Get paid on the mortgage when representing buy-side clients
- Average REMLO earns ~**$2,000 per file** in additional commission
- Qualification: at least 6 buy-side transactions/year
- Canada: not yet available

### eXp Revenos (Lead Division)

- Provides leads + certification programs: relocation, Affinity, Bundle Select, Honor Military Rewards, Institutional Investment, Express Offers, REO (foreclosures)
- Some require certification; some charge a fee and take a portion of commission in exchange for leads
- Revenos commissions paid out: $247,000 (2021) → ~$1,000,000 (2024)

### eXp Hub (Replacing Slack)

- eXp launched Hub as its internal communication platform — roughly 3 years old as of Feb 2026
- Previously used Facebook Workspace (discontinued by Facebook with 90-day notice); migrated to Slack (only ~30k of 90k agents followed)
- Hub is being built in-house and is still evolving — features being added constantly
- Gogo says all communication is migrating to Hub

### eXp Specialized Divisions

- **Fast Cap**: Training program every ~6 weeks to help agents reach cap fast; covers newest tech, systems, objection handling, accountability. Gogo recommends for all agents regardless of experience level.
- **Team Leader Academy**: Recommended for anyone building a local team; covers profit margins, team contracts, split structures (traditional teams often run at 6% profit = $60k on $1M GCI)
- **eXp Luxury**: Certification for agents in qualifying price ranges; separate meetings, trainings, and luxury packages
- **eXp Commercial**: Separate company (higher cap, higher cut); access to LoopNet; residential license can do commercial to a limit (~$2M E&O cap per deal)
