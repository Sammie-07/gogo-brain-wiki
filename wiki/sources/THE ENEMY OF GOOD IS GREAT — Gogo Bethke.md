# THE ENEMY OF GOOD IS GREAT — Gogo Bethke

> Short ~2-minute video clip from The Brain🧠 on the concept of opportunity cost — when you're capable of earning $2,000/hr, doing $45/hr work is a bad trade.

## Key Takeaways

- **The enemy of good is great** — good opportunities steal time from great ones. Both are real; the problem is which one you choose.
- When you've built your skillset to the point where you can make $2,000/hr on listings, spending time on $45/hr tasks is losing money — even though $45/hr is great for someone else.
- Practical example: buyer agents at 50/50 split make $200/hr. Show me another job without college education that pays $200/hr. For THEM, that is a great opportunity. For you, it's a good one.
- At a high enough level, you have OPTIONS. The job is to choose the great one and delegate or eliminate the good ones.
- "When you spend your time on good opportunities, you're stealing time from the great opportunities because you're not going to have time for them."

## Quotes

> "The enemy of good is great. You created great opportunities for yourself, but every time you're choosing a good opportunity, you're losing money."

> "When you spend your time on good opportunities, you're stealing time from the great opportunities because you're not going to have time for them."
