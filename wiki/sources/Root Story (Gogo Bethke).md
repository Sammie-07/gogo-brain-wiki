# Root Story (Gogo Bethke)

> Gogo explains the Root Story — a pinned Reel that introduces you to new followers in 10 one-sentence bullet points. How to create it using a ChatGPT prompt, how to shoot it, and why it must be pinned to your profile permanently.

## Key Takeaways

### What the Root Story Is
- A short Reel that answers: Who are you? Why should someone follow you? What do you bring to the table?
- Format: **10 bullet points, one sentence each** — fast, memorable, no deep-diving into details
- Lives on your profile **permanently** as one of your three pinned posts
- Purpose: convert new followers into engaged followers the moment they land on your profile

### Why It Matters
When a new follower visits your profile, the first thing they see is your three pinned posts. If one of them is your Root Story, they immediately understand who you are, what you've accomplished, and why they should care — before they've read a single caption.

### How to Create It

**Step 1 — Use the ChatGPT Prompt**
GoGet'Em provides a script/prompt. Answer the questions → ChatGPT outputs 10 concise sentences about you.

**Step 2 — Shoot One Sentence at a Time**
- Read each sentence → put it in your own words → film it (one sentence per clip)
- Do NOT read word-for-word — speak naturally
- Gogo's honest note: she's too wordy to do this naturally, so she reads the ChatGPT sentence, absorbs it, then says it in her own words
- B-roll can replace some clips (previously shot footage with your voiceover)

**Step 3 — Edit into One Reel**
Assemble the 10 clips into a single Reel. End with a clear call to action ("Follow me if you're building something big").

**Step 4 — Pin to Profile**
After posting, pin to your profile. It must be one of your three pinned posts. It lives there forever (or until you update it).

### Gogo's Root Story (Example)
> "...I didn't know a single human being on this whole continent. Today my real estate organization named Team Go is 1,660 agents strong and we closed over 12 billion dollars in sales in 7 and a half years. I became an entrepreneur because of this quote: 'If you don't build your dream, someone is going to hire you to build theirs.' English is actually my third language, so the accent is here to stay. I've been happily married for 22 years, and we are raising two teenage boys. Before real estate, I worked every job you can imagine. Babysitter, warehouse, shipping, food safety auditor. I built my entire real estate business mostly from social media. I only work Monday through Wednesday because success should buy you time freedom. I spent hundreds of thousands of dollars on education to learn how to build businesses. And now I spend my time teaching other entrepreneurs how to build empires, too. Follow me if you're building something big."

### Gogo's Own Mistake
Her first Root Story attempt ran 17 minutes. Laura had to stop her: *"Yeah, no, we're going to have to redo this and maybe script it."* The lesson: use ChatGPT to force conciseness.
