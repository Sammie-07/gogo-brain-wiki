# AssumeList

Assumable-mortgage marketplace and transaction-support service that surfaces homes with sub-5% assumable loans and shepherds the assumption to close. Presented to #teamgogo (Jul 2026) by **Nora Simpson** (Head of Education) and **Mike Lorino** (CEO/founder).

## Overview

AssumeList aggregates listings with **assumable FHA/VA/USDA loans below 5%** (many at 2.4–3% from 2020–21), including off-market properties via skip-trace and mailer campaigns, and provides a transaction-management team that handles the lender-side assumption process most agents/consumers can't navigate alone. See [[Assumable Mortgages]] for the underlying mechanics.

## Key Facts

- **Scale:** 44,000+ assumable listings across **55 MLSs / 26 states** (27th launching); only displays homes with assumable loans **below 5%** interest.
- **eXp enterprise license:** [[eXp Realty|Leo Pareja]] purchased an enterprise license → **every eXp agent gets free access** (otherwise ~$80/mo). Agents issue **buyer sub-accounts** and see all client search activity.
- **Access:** AssumeList.com/exp → "I already have a code" → eXp registration code **BZEUEVKM** → register with business + eXp email. Codes rotate ~every 2 days to drive agents to a live training.
- **Auto-listing:** a property in a covered area (e.g., South Florida / Beaches / Miami MLS) is **auto-added once listed on the MLS**, if its loan is assumable and below 5%.
- **Tools:** VA entitlement calculator (PDF report), second-mortgage calculator, and a preferred lender offering the lowest second-mortgage rates for the equity gap.
- **Fees (paid by buyer/seller, not the agent):** **$2,300 flat TC fee** = **$200 upfront setup** + $2,100 at clear-to-close; **no charge beyond $200 if the assumption isn't approved**. Claim: "never a turndown, never longer than 60 days."
- **Integrations:** Follow Up Boss (live); **BoldTrail** native API in progress. Daily Q&A Mon–Fri 11am ET at homes.assumelist.com/learn; support@assumelist.com.
- **Advocacy:** Mike Lorino has pushed the VA Inspector General to pressure non-compliant servicers that stall legally-required FHA/VA assumptions.
