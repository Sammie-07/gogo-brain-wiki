## Additional Strategies (Family Employment Session)

- Hiring children under 18 in a sole proprietorship: wages exempt from SS/Medicare taxes; first ~$13K tax-free via standard deduction; Roth IRA fundable with earned income
- Legitimate child work: social media, admin, photography, video
- Hiring spouse: opens 401(k) and HSA access
- Family LLCs / management companies: shift income to lower-bracket family members
- Documentation required: time records, job descriptions, market-rate pay

## Additional Strategies — Part 2 Session (Tax-Free Family)

From her second GoGet'Em session, Barbara presented "8 Steps to Get the First $400K Tax-Free":

**The math problem she solves:** $400K revenue → $200K expenses → $200K profit → $75K+ in taxes → only $125K in your pocket. "You might as well have stayed at your corporate job."

**Self-employment tax:** 15.3% extra on top of all other taxes — the hidden penalty most new business owners don't know about until year 2.

**Step 1 — S-Corp Election:**
- Eliminates self-employment tax on the pass-through portion
- 35x less likely to be audited as S-corp vs Schedule C
- Barbara recommends making the switch at any income level — even at a loss

**Step 2 — Spouse as Employee (not owner):**
- Owners are excluded from certain benefits; employees are not — so make one spouse an employee
- $500/month salary ($6K/year deduction) unlocks the full stack:
  - **MERP (Medical Expense Reimbursement Plan):** No dollar limit — reimburse any out-of-pocket medical: prescriptions, chiropractor, massages, peptides, red light therapy, cold plunge, sauna, hormones, IVF, adoption. Barbara deducted $200K in cancer treatment costs through her MERP.
  - Double retirement contributions ($70K → $140K if both spouses contribute to 401K)
  - Double home office deductions
  - Business vehicle for spouse
  - Business travel with spouse (Barbara wrote off a month in Europe)
  - Dependent care FSA: $5,000 for daycare, summer camp, before/after school care
  - Aging parents as employees: MERP covers their medical; supplement Medicare through business

**Step 3 — Board of Directors / Board of Advisers:**
- Every entity must have one; add people you love and travel with them (business deduction)
- Odd number of members so you can vote on business decisions
- Under 18 = Board of Advisers (unpaid); over 18 = Board of Directors (can be paid)
- Barbara has a "board bundle" template on her website
