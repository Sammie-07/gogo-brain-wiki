# Thomas Heimann

Tech entrepreneur, AI educator, and [[#teamgogo]] agent partner; founder of [[Cloud Title]] (an AI-powered title company) and author of *Twice Ahead*.

## Overview

Thomas Heimann is a 35+ year technology entrepreneur (an internet/e-commerce pioneer from the early '90s) with 22 years in real estate. He guest-taught a hands-on AI workshop for the [[GoGet'Em Community]], walking agents through building "intelligence briefs," client avatars, and custom GPTs/AI agents. He posts a daily AI news brief and builds AI tools for real estate.

## Key Facts

- Founder/operator of [[Cloud Title]] (AI-powered title company); author of *Twice Ahead* (Amazon).
- Builder of **Sarah** (an AI real estate executive assistant living in WhatsApp, meetsarah.io) and **Apex AI Coach** (apexaicoach.com).
- Runs a 19-agent autonomous AI "org" on OpenClaw; uses Claude Opus for high-level reasoning (e.g., iterating an operating agreement over ~8 rounds).
- **DeepSeek cost economics:** runs ~6 agents (incl. Hermes and the Meet Sarah system) on [[DeepSeek]] V4 — claimed ~Opus-level quality for ~1% of the cost — at just **$142 for all of May**; reserves Opus 4.7 for high-level consulting. Also invested ~$30K in local hardware (~2 TB memory) to run local LLMs with no API fees.
- **Markdown for machines:** always exports briefs in both .docx (humans) and .md (AI) — markdown uses far fewer tokens and parses more cleanly; uses ChatGPT/Claude **Projects** to segregate memory so businesses, health, and personal data never co-mingle.
- **Avatar-building workflow (3 steps):** (1) area market-intelligence report (3-yr trends), (2) ideal-customer identification, (3) synthesize both into a hyper-detailed [[Client Avatar]] (separate for buyers vs. sellers, even per-listing); most important fields are the fears/desires — "the psychological buttons you push."

## Core teaching: three eras of AI

1. **Chatbots** — you do the work.
2. **Custom agents** — you guide the work (e.g., Claude Cowork).
3. **Agentic AI** — agents do the work and hand off to each other ("swarms").

Foundation = three documents: a **Personal Intelligence Brief**, a **Business Intelligence Brief** (one per business), and a named **Client Avatar**. Model guidance: Claude Sonnet for everyday work/content, Opus for big reasoning, Haiku for simple fast tasks. "Garbage in, garbage out" — quality of input determines quality of output.
