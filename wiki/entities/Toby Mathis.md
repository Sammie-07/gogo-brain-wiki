## Second GoGet'Em Session — IRS Myths and Tax Structure

From "Tax Strategy and IRS Myths — Estate Planning Part 2":

- **IRS 87,000 agents myth:** The Inflation Reduction Act funding for 87,000 IRS agents represents replacements over 10 years — not a surge of new auditors targeting taxpayers. IRS audit rates actually dropped 70%+ since 2012 because they stopped rehiring after the Great Recession.
- **Who gets audited:** EITC recipients (poor families without CPAs), Schedule C sole proprietors, and — counterintuitively — not millionaires (low audit risk currently).
- **Sole proprietor trap:** 70% of realtors file as sole proprietors (Schedule C). They lose 94–95% of audits. This is the most dangerous structure for a real estate agent.
- **How to identify:** No separate business tax return + no salary drawn = almost certainly a sole proprietor.
- **S-corp advantages:** 1,000–1,500% lower audit risk than sole proprietors; employment tax savings (even at $25K/yr salary level); accountable plan unlocks deductions unavailable to Schedule C filers.
- **Breakeven:** S-corp conversion pencils out even at $25K/year net — the employment tax savings alone justify the cost and complexity.
- **Estate planning basics:** Covered core documents (will, trust, power of attorney) and when to start — framed as accessible and urgent for any business owner.
