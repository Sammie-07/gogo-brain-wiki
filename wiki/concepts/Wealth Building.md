# Wealth Building

Gogo's framework for building and protecting wealth — not just making income, but keeping it, multiplying it, and building systems so money eventually works instead of you.

> "Just six years ago, I was BROKE. I made a vow to learn everything I could about money so I would never be in that situation again."

## Three Types of Income

Understanding income types is the foundation of wealth strategy. Most realtors only have one (active) and trade time for money forever.

| Type | Definition | Gogo's Examples |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Active** | You must show up to earn it | Coaching calls, commissions |
| **Recurring** | Built once; arrives every month from ongoing system | Revenue share at eXp, community memberships |
| **Passive** | Earns with no active labor | Investment returns, rental income, stock dividends |

**Goal:** Shift the income mix over time from active → recurring/passive. Today ~90% of Gogo's income is passive or recurring.

## Credit Card Strategy

> "Never ever ever use your own money."

Use credit cards for every expense. Pay the balance down on the 1st and 15th of each month -- paying twice per month means you never accumulate APR. Use the bank's money, collect the points, pay it off.

**Gogo's setup:**
- **Delta SkyMiles Amex** -- all personal expenses. Miles earned on every dollar. Romania first-class ticket paid for with miles: $120 in taxes.
- **Chase business card** -- one per company; each company has its own debit and credit card when banking with Chase.
- **Chase 0% APR intro offer** -- when opening a new business, Chase often provides 12-18 months at 0% APR.

Paying with a debit card at a business dinner is a visible signal to Gogo that the person doesn't understand money. Using credit (with a plan to pay it off) is the correct move; the bank's money earns you points, the cash in your account stays invested.

## Tax Strategy and S-Corp Structure

**S-Corp:** Draw a W2 salary from the company. This separates payroll taxes from corporate taxes -- you pay payroll tax only on your salary, not on every dollar the business earns.

**2024 tax brackets (filing jointly):**
- Up to $487K: 35%
- Above $731,200: 37%

**The write-off math:** At 37%, every $1,000 not written off costs $370 to Uncle Sam. If you buy a $1,000 training course, you write it off at 100% -- same effective cost, but you gain the skill. Not buying it just means paying Uncle Sam the $370 with nothing to show for it.

**Car purchase as tax strategy:** Gogo's CPA told her to buy a new car or pay $56,000 more in taxes. She bought a $169,000 car and saved $50,000+ in taxes. The car was the more advantageous outcome.

**Tax caution:** Writing off too much makes your on-paper income look low. This can prevent qualifying for mortgages or investment property loans. Coordinate with your CPA before buying real estate so they know not to zero out your income that year.

## Business Structure: Box in a Box in a Box

**LLC → Holding Company → Living Trust**

Each business and each investment property is in its own LLC. All LLCs are held by a holding company. The holding company sits inside a living trust.

> "If you look up my name, I own nothing."

**Why this matters:**
- **Lawsuit protection:** If someone sues one LLC, they can only reach that LLC's assets -- not the others, not the holding company, not the trust.
- **Privacy:** Personal ownership is not publicly linked to business assets.
- **Estate transfer:** The living trust bypasses probate court. Wealth transfers according to Gogo's wishes without court involvement or delays.

Anderson Advisors creates a visual "blueprint" (entity diagram) for each client showing all companies, their ownership structure, and their percentages. Gogo found Anderson Advisors when she noticed the blueprint on a NASA rocket scientist's screen during a visit.

## Key Tax Strategy: Cost Segregation

A real estate investment tax tool that accelerates depreciation deductions, dramatically reducing taxable income.

**How it works:** Standard depreciation: residential 27.5 years, commercial 39 years. A cost segregation study reclassifies building components into 5-, 7-, and 15-year depreciation schedules. Large deductions can be taken in year one instead of spread over decades.

**Gogo's actual numbers:**
- Property 1: saved $58,973.93 in taxes in one year alone
- Property 2: saved $49,000+ in taxes
- Study cost per property: approximately $3,000
- Net savings far exceeded cost on both properties
- Property 1 also appreciated $22,000 in value that year while rent covered the mortgage
- Three simultaneous wins: tax savings + appreciation + tenant-paid mortgage

**How to do it right:**
- Do not self-report. Hire professional engineers who specialize in it.
- They find land value and component classifications you would miss (example: land value not in public records; had to call the county to confirm)
- kbkg.com has a free preliminary calculator before you commit to a paid study

**Credit card debt payoff framework:**
1. Make a spreadsheet of all credit cards and their APR
2. Cancel all unused subscriptions and recurring bills first (Gogo was paying for Keeping Current Matters for months without logging in)
3. Pay off highest APR card first
4. Use credit for everything going forward; pay off on the 1st and 15th of every month -- paying twice per month means you never accumulate APR interest

## Building Credit

Gogo repaired her credit from 630 to 817 in six years.

**The repair method:** Pay credit card bills on the 1st and 15th of every month -- approximately 3 days before the statement closing date. When you pay before the statement closes, the card reports a near-zero balance to the credit bureaus, which dramatically improves your utilization ratio.

High utilization = low score. Low utilization = high score. The key is timing payment to hit before the statement date, not just before the due date.

Also: check unclaimed.org for forgotten balances -- old utility accounts, insurance policies, and bank accounts that might show as unresolved.

- Good credit = ability to qualify for investment properties, business lines of credit, favorable terms.
- Treat credit maintenance like a business metric -- track it, protect it.

## Savings vs. Inflation -- The Losing Math

2023 US inflation: 3.4%. Average bank savings account interest: 0.49%. Net loss: 2.9% per year in purchasing power on every saved dollar. A $100,000 standard savings account is worth $97,100 in real purchasing power one year later.

Rule: have an emergency fund (6 months of expenses). Everything above that must be invested. Saving money does not create wealth -- investing does.

## Stock Strategy -- 52-Week High and Low

When evaluating any stock, open the app and navigate to the Stats section. Find the 52-week high and 52-week low.

**The rule:** Buy when the stock is down. Sell when it is up. If a stock is at its 52-week high with no particular reason to believe it goes higher, wait.

**Example:** Stock at $40. 52-week high $43, low $30. Still $3 below the high -- approximately 7.5% upside. Worth buying if you believe in the company. At the 52-week high with no catalyst: wait.

**Buy to hold, not to day-trade.** Day trading creates income (taxable). Buy-to-hold creates wealth. Gogo never sells to generate income -- she holds to build wealth and avoid triggering a taxable event.

## Personal Finance Foundations (Barbara Schreihans)

Barbara Schreihans -- founder of Your Tax Coach, Inc 5000 #513, 30 employees, 100+ investment doors -- delivered a full personal finance framework to the GoGet'Em Community. See also [[Mindset]] for her money mindset section.

**Unclaimed money:** Search unclaimed.org with your name and maiden name. Government site with balances tied to your SSN. People have found million-dollar life insurance policies there.

**Auto-transfers:** Set up automatic transfers every payday to savings, tax account, stock accounts, and Roth IRA. Money that leaves before you can decide to spend it is never missed. Start at $5-$20/week -- the habit matters more than the amount. Barbara auto-transferred $5/week into Acorns starting 2018 while in debt; that account was $100K when she checked it. She now has a second $100K stock account.

**Roth IRA math:** $134.61/week invested from age 35 to 65 = $1 million tax-free at retirement. The math is non-negotiable -- the only variable is whether you start. Income limit approximately $300K/year for direct contribution. Set up free on Fidelity.com; you must actually invest the money inside the account (do not leave it in cash). Over age 50: contribute an additional $1,000/year.

**Backdoor Roth IRA:** For high earners above the Roth income limits. Contribute to a non-deductible traditional IRA, then immediately convert to Roth. If your financial advisor has never mentioned this, find a new one.

**High-yield savings:** Move cash from standard savings (0.005% interest) to high-yield accounts (4-5% currently). $100K in high-yield = $5,000/year in passive interest for zero effort. Takes 10 minutes.

**Bi-weekly mortgage payments:** Pay half the monthly mortgage every two weeks = one extra full payment per year.
- On a $1M loan: saves $170K+ in total interest; pays off in 25 years instead of 30
- Some lenders allow it online; others require a phone call

**Solo 401K:** For business owners with no full-time employees with 1+ year tenure. Contribute up to $69,000/year as a business deduction.

**HSA -- Triple Tax Benefit:** Only available with a high-deductible health plan. Contributions are tax-free (reduce taxable income), growth is tax-free, medical withdrawals are tax-free. Strategy: contribute now and let it compound until age 65+; use for medical at retirement. Do not spend it now -- compounding is the entire point.

**FSA for daycare:** For children under 5, pay up to $6,600 of daycare expenses tax-free via a Flexible Spending Account.

**Defined Benefit Plan:** Layer on top of a maxed 401K ($23K/year cap). Barbara has clients contributing $250K+ to their own defined benefit plan per year. Contributions are discretionary -- can skip in bad years. Can be done at year-end (up to when the tax return is filed, even with an extension to September/October). Can take a loan against the plan to buy real estate; interest is paid back to yourself, not a bank.

**Life insurance as wealth vehicle:** Cash-value life insurance provides a payout at retirement age, not just at death. Builds cash flow for retirement beyond standard retirement accounts.

**Gifting strategy and estate planning:**
- Gift up to $18,000/year to any person anywhere in the world, tax-free to recipient
- Couples can each gift $18,000 = $36,000/child/year tax-free
- Above $18,000 to one person: still tax-free to recipient, but you file a gift tax return and it reduces your lifetime estate exemption
- Estate tax exemption: currently $13M per individual ($26M for married couples), expected to drop to approximately $4M
- Gifting now moves wealth out of your estate at no current tax cost
- Money goes into UTMA accounts (guardian-managed investment accounts for minors)

**Kids on payroll (confirmed numbers):**
- Must be doing real work in your business
- Tax-free to them: up to $14,600 (2024) / $15,000 (2025) per child (standard deduction)
- **Children under 18 working for a sole proprietor parent: exempt from Social Security and Medicare taxes** — additional savings on top of the income shift
- Business deduction for you; child owes zero if under standard deduction threshold
- Use the earned income to fund Roth IRA ($7,000/year limit), UTMA accounts, or life insurance
- Roth IRA with earned income from childhood: powerful long-term compound growth with no future tax liability
- Add kids to board of advisors -- they learn business by being in the room
- Board meetings anywhere (Bahamas, Europe) = business travel deduction
- Tax math example: 37% bracket owner pays 16-year-old $12K for real social media work → owner saves ~$4,440 in federal tax, child owes zero, child can fund Roth IRA

**QBI deduction:** 20% deduction for business owners; was set to expire; Trump indicated he would extend it. Coordinate with your CPA to adjust W-2 salary to maximize the deduction.

> "If your financial advisor has never mentioned the backdoor Roth, that is your sign to find a new one."

## Digital Products as Passive Income (Zee Slingsby)

Digital products -- courses, PDF guides, mini-courses -- are zero-fulfillment, zero-time-trade-off income at any volume once created. No inventory, no delivery cost, no scaling ceiling.

**Income math:**
- PDF guide at $197 x 30 sales = $5,900 (zero fulfillment, zero time)
- Same guide x 100 sales = $20,000
- Course at $1,500 x 5 sales = $7,500
- Keep selling as long as content is relevant; graduates become the audience for the next-level offer

**Pre-launch strategy:** Start marketing before the product is finished. Sell before you're done. Create an RSVP landing page to collect interested names/emails. Offer founding member pricing for pre-launch buyers. If someone buys, you have motivation to finish. If nobody buys yet, no emotional sunk cost from unfinished work.

**The Three Boxes framework for positioning digital offers:**
- Box 1 (Pain): What thoughts are running through your ideal client's head when their head hits the pillow? Get granular -- not "struggling to sign clients" but "I posted and nobody cared, I must not be good at this, I can't pay my bills."
- Box 3 (Outcome): Where do they want to be? Not "a commission" -- more like "I feel confident, I know where I belong, I can plan a vacation."
- Box 2 (Program): What you teach them to get from Box 1 to Box 3.
- Marketing mistake: most people only talk about Box 2. Nobody resonates. Talk to Box 1 and Box 3.

**Warning on master resell rights (MRR):** Do not buy someone else's course to resell. Zee had a client who started with MRR with no long-term success; she then created her own course and brought in $38,000 in the first two months.

## Group Coaching and Scaling Income (Zee Slingsby)

Key principles for scaling from $10K/month to $10K/week:

- **Group coaching ≠ inferior product.** Group creates community, peer accountability, and often better outcomes than 1:1. Don't resist it out of false loyalty to a 1:1 model.
- **Paying for proximity.** Clients pay to be close to the person and the room. Price accordingly — proximity has value beyond the information delivered.
- **Retention > acquisition.** Acquiring a new client costs far more than keeping an existing one. Build offers designed for people to stay, not just join.
- **Time > money at scale.** At a certain income level, time becomes the scarcer resource. Group programs serve more people without proportionally more hours.
- **Messaging, not pricing.** If clients aren't converting, the problem is your messaging — not your price. Lowering price signals low value and attracts wrong clients.

> "You don't need anyone to say yes. That energy — when you need the client — they feel it and they run."

## Business Funding and Credit (Zach Seagull — Small Biz Heroes)

Building business credit is a prerequisite for accessing low-cost capital. Most agents operate without intentional business credit — which leaves them dependent on personal credit and paying higher rates (or getting denied entirely).

**The Three C's (banks require all three):** Credit + Collateral + Cash Flow. Post-2008 financial crisis, banks became highly conservative. Fintech and private lenders only require ONE of the three, making them more accessible for growing businesses.

**Business credit foundation (must be consistent everywhere):**
- IRS / EIN letter
- Secretary of State registration
- Dun & Bradstreet, Equifax Business, Experian Business
- Website, Google My Business, 411 directory
- Business checking account, merchant processing account
- No @gmail or @yahoo — use branded domain email

**Build trade lines in batches of 3:**
Net-30/Net-15 vendors that report to bureaus: Grainger, Uline, Quill, Maverick Office, Restaurant Depot. Open 3, let them report 2-3 months, then add 3 more. Repeat until you have a solid profile. Then you can qualify for cars, vans, equipment on EIN only — no SSN, no personal guarantee.

**Four business credit scores to monitor:** Paydex, Equifax Business, Experian Business, SBSS (mix of personal and business). Check at nav.com.

**Funding options by business stage:**
- Startup (0-1yr): 0% credit card stacking (personal credit dependent)
- Growing (1-3yr): Revenue-based / working capital loans

## Tax Credit Tools — 45L Energy Credit and Cost Segregation

Two under-utilized real estate tax tools Gogo uses personally on her rental properties, taught by Cost Segregation Authority (Dawn Lavanway + Eric Oliver).

**45L Energy-Efficient Home Credit (Inflation Reduction Act, permanent through 2032):**
- $500–$5,000 per door for energy-efficient construction placed in service 2023-2032 (inflation-adjusted)
- Applies to: ADUs, fourplexes, SFRs, apartment buildings — anything with eat/sleep/cook/bathroom
- Credit goes to whoever held the construction loan (basis at construction), not the final buyer
- Can amend returns to go back 3 years
- Remodels qualify only if taken to studs with new windows + insulation
- RESNET-certified third-party certification required (~$300-400/door); free analysis before engaging
- Non-refundable; carries forward until fully utilized

**Cost Segregation (accelerated depreciation):**
- Normal depreciation: residential = 27.5yr, commercial = 39yr. Cost seg reclassifies components to 5/7/15yr assets.
- Three IRS tests for short-term classification: (1) removable without structural damage, (2) retired together with connected equipment, (3) unique to business purpose
- 5-7yr assets: carpet, countertops, cabinets, LVP flooring, window coverings, appliance-specific electrical/plumbing
- 15yr assets: parking lots, curbs, gutters, trees/shrubs, irrigation systems, asphalt, retaining walls
- Typically ~30% of depreciable basis gets reclassified
- **Bonus depreciation (2024): 60%** — phases down 20%/yr until zero in 2027. Congress is trying to restore 100%.
- "New to you" rule: used properties qualify for bonus depreciation
- **Catch-up depreciation:** do a cost seg study on properties already owned → bring all missed depreciation forward to current-year return in one shot
- Rate arbitrage: deduct now at income tax rate, pay recapture at lower capital gains rate when selling — not a wash, it's a real spread
- Cost Segregation Authority: 20 years, 25,000 studies, reports designed by CPAs for CPAs, partners with your CPA

## Trust and Legacy Planning (Toby Mathis)

## IRS Audit Reality and Business Structure (Toby Mathis — Session 2)

From the second GoGet'Em session with [[Toby Mathis]] (tax attorney, 300+ properties):

**The 87,000 IRS agents myth:** The Inflation Reduction Act funding represents replacements over 10 years — not new auditors hired immediately. IRS audit rates actually dropped 70%+ since 2012 because the agency stopped rehiring after the Great Recession.

**Who actually gets audited:**
- EITC recipients (poor families without CPAs — IRS wins easily)
- Schedule C sole proprietors (1,000–1,500% more likely to be audited than S-corps)
- Millionaires: surprisingly low audit risk right now (counterintuitive)

**The sole proprietor trap:** 70% of realtors file as sole proprietors. They lose 94–95% of audits. It is the single most dangerous business structure for a real estate agent.

**How to know if you're a sole proprietor:** No separate business tax return AND no salary drawn from the business = almost certainly a Schedule C filer.

**S-corp advantages (reinforced):**
- 1,000–1,500% lower audit risk than sole proprietors
- Employment tax savings (pencils out even at $25K/year net income)
- Accountable plan: deductions unavailable to Schedule C filers
- Structure: you are employer + employee; pay payroll tax only on your salary, not all business revenue

## Four Profit Levers — Maximize Without More Transactions (Lou Raven and Elise Palacios)

From the second GoGet'Em session focused on the "I" (Increasing Profit) in the PITY framework:

**The four levers:**
1. **Sell more houses** — the most common advice; also the most exhausting. Requires more time, more stress, more overhead.
2. **Increase your average sales price** — sell fewer homes but in a higher price range.
3. **Charge more for your services** — increase your commission rate.
4. **Reduce the commission you pay your brokerage** — negotiate a lower cap or a better split structure.

**The insight:** Levers 2, 3, and 4 require zero additional transactions. Most agents only ever reach for lever 1 — and wonder why they're burned out.

> "To me, that sounds horrible. More volume = more time, more stress, more overhead."

**Elise's origin framing:** Ran a physically demanding cleaning business. Her goal became: earn more from the same effort, not do more effort. That mindset drives the profit maximization approach.

**Application:** Commission structure, brokerage cap choices, pricing strategy, and service positioning are all decisions — not fixed facts. Every agent has more control over their profit than they realize.

## SETC — Self-Employed Tax Credit (Alex Moeller, Prosperity Tax Group)

Pandemic-era IRS program for self-employed individuals; taught by [[Alex Moeller]] as Day 1 of GoGet'Em's Master Your Money week. Completely separate from PPP loans, ERC, or unemployment payments.

**What it is:** A non-taxable, non-repayable tax credit for self-employed individuals who paid taxes during the pandemic. The government set aside $70 billion; less than 5% of eligible Americans have claimed it.

**Who qualifies (all three required):**
1. Self-employed during 2020 and/or 2021 — 1099 subcontractor, Schedule C, single-member LLC, partnership on federal return
2. Impacted by COVID-19 — **self-attestation only**, no documentation required
3. Paid net taxes on 1099 income in 2020 or 2021

**Who does NOT qualify:** W-2 employees only (no 1099 income); S-corp owners who paid themselves only as W-2 employees; those with zero net taxable income those years.

**Credit amounts:**
- Single filer: up to $32,200
- Married filing jointly (each spouse with 1099 income): up to $64,400

**The "self-attestation" principle:** The IRS cannot demand proof of COVID impact. If you attest you were impacted (illness, quarantine, kids' school closed, business disruption), that is sufficient. Everyone who went through the pandemic was impacted in some way.

**Credit mechanics:** Based on net taxable 1099 income in 2020–2021. The more taxes paid, the higher the credit. High write-off strategies that reduced taxable income to near-zero will result in little or no credit.

**Process with Prosperity Tax Group:**
1. Pre-qualify in ~7 minutes online (AI calculates amount instantly, down to the penny)
2. Upload actual tax returns (not transcripts; not watermarked copies; PDF format)
3. CPA team files amended returns; IRS processes in 6–12 months standard
4. **Advance Program:** Prosperity Tax Group has partnered with a bank to collateralize the credit against the tax refund — qualified applicants can receive funds in 30–45 days. No other firm was doing this at time of presentation.

**Fee structure:** 0% upfront. 20% contingency fee paid only after client receives money. No charge if no credit recovered.

**Referral value:** Sharing this with self-employed contacts (realtors, barbers, Uber drivers, freelancers, independent contractors) is a high-goodwill action — the referrer earns reciprocal loyalty.

> "This is the government's way of saying, 'Thank you for doing the right thing during the pandemic, working hard — you paid your taxes, and now you are getting some of that money back.'"
