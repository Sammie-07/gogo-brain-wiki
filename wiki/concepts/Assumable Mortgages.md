# Assumable Mortgages

A buyer takes over (assumes) a seller's existing low-rate loan instead of originating a new one at current rates — a lead-generation and affordability lever in a high-rate market. Surfaced in [[#teamgogo]] via [[AssumeList]].

## Core Idea

When rates are high, a home carrying an assumable **sub-5% loan** (many at 2.4–3% from 2020–21) is dramatically more affordable than the same home bought with a new ~6.25% loan. The rate, remaining balance, and servicer stay the same; the buyer steps into the seller's shoes. Marketing an assumable listing ("get this house at 2.43%") drives inbound buyer demand — agents who promote assumables reportedly grow fast.

## Which Loans Are Assumable

Only **FHA, VA, and USDA** loans (not conventional).
- **FHA:** any creditworthy buyer may assume (needn't qualify for a specific FHA program); must live in the home the first 12 months.
- **VA:** assumable even by non-VA buyers and **investors** — if the veteran leaves the tied **entitlement** behind (a VA entitlement calculator quantifies this). A key investor angle: VA loans are the rare assumable path open to non-owner-occupants.
- **USDA:** assumable but restrictive — the assuming buyer must meet USDA rules, notably income **≤115% of local median household income**.

## The Equity Gap (Second Mortgage)

Because prices rose and principal was paid down, the buyer must cover the gap between purchase price and the remaining loan balance. In practice the buyer needs **~10% down**, and any amount above that can be financed with a **second mortgage** — but many lenders don't offer them for assumptions, so a specialized second-mortgage lender matters.

## Savings & Reality

- Monthly savings today are ~**$500–$1,000** (were $2,000–$3,000 a few years ago); some buyers pursue assumptions to save even ~$100/month.
- **Why banks stall:** servicers are legally required to allow FHA/VA assumptions but keep those teams hidden/understaffed — they'd rather write a new full-rate loan than transfer a 2.7% one. A DIY assumption usually fails; a specialist ([[AssumeList]]) navigates the process, and all liability transfers seller→buyer in a federally regulated process.

## Agent Playbook (via [[AssumeList]])

- Market assumable listings with a **green-screen video** in front of the listing photo (address whited out), leading with the rate.
- In covered MLS areas the listing is **auto-added** to the marketplace once on the MLS (if the loan is assumable and below 5%).
- Fees are paid by buyer/seller, not the agent (a flat TC fee; no charge beyond a small setup fee if the assumption isn't approved). See [[AssumeList]] for specifics.
