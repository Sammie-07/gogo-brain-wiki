# Lead Generation

[[Gogo Bethke]]'s approach to lead generation is built on **organic systems** — social media, community presence, email databases, and referral networks — rather than paid advertising. The goal is to move people from social platforms into owned channels (CRM, email list) where they can be nurtured and converted.

> "A lead is not a lead until they are off the app. Get them into your world."

## Fishing in the Right Pond — Ideal Client Alignment

> "If you want to sell million-dollar homes, but you keep talking about the starter tiny houses, you're never going to catch a $3–4 million whale. You're fishing in the wrong pond."

The most common lead generation mistake: consistent effort in the wrong water. You can fish every day and never catch a whale if the pond you're fishing in doesn't have whales.

**Content determines client quality.** The price point, lifestyle, and audience you speak to in your content is the pool you're fishing from. If you want $750K buyers, post $750K listings and content in $750K neighborhoods. If you want $3M sellers, show up in $3M communities — literally and digitally.

**Without a defined ideal client → spaghetti marketing.** Random commissions when something sticks. With a defined ideal client → clockwork: you know who they are, where they are, what to say. The full ChatGPT framework for building this profile is in [[Social Media and Email Marketing#ChatGPT Ideal Client Framework]].

**Lowest-hanging fruit = people like you right now.** Shared life stage = shared trust. Gogo's early clients were young moms she met at school pickups. Her clients today match her current life stage — mid-40s, established, larger homes. As you change, your ideal client profile changes. Redo the exercise every 5–10 years.

## The Off-the-App Rule (Amy Gregory)

A social media follower, commenter, or even DM participant is **not a lead** until you have their name and contact info off-platform. Every interaction on Instagram, Facebook, or any platform must have the goal of moving the person into your CRM or email list.

This is the foundational principle. Traffic without capture is wasted.

## The 78% First-Agent Rule

**78% of buyers and sellers go with the first agent who contacts them.** Speed is the competitive variable that beats almost everything else. Automations make you "first" even while you're sleeping.

**5-minute follow-up rule:** Respond to every new lead within 5 minutes of entering your CRM. After 5 minutes, the probability of conversion drops sharply.

All lead magnets gate content behind an email/contact form. Never give a direct link — always collect information first.

## Neighborhood Community Strategy (Stacey Leslie)

$100M in 2024 with zero ad spend using neighborhood Facebook groups and vendor-sponsored events.

**Core rules:**
- Join or create the Facebook group for your target community
- **Never answer questions publicly** — always "DM me." Drives private relationships.
- Post newsworthy community content first; earn trust before selling
- Engage posts one by one (not simultaneously) — sequential engagement keeps you at the top of the feed
- Sponsor local events through vendors (zero cost to agent)
- Capture contact info via QR code at events

**7-times rule:** Prospects must encounter you ~7 times before they remember you. Community events + social posts + DMs compound toward this number rapidly.

## Referral Generation System (Brandon Barnum)

**HOA opportunity:** 53% of US homeowners live in an HOA (375,000 HOAs nationwide). Becoming the go-to agent for HOA-governed properties is an underserved niche.

**Shalmani Method — 5 Ps of magnetic referral generation:**
1. **People** — who you know
2. **Purpose** — why they should trust you
3. **Passion** — what drives you (authentically)
4. **Personality** — how you show up
5. **Prosperity** — the mutual benefit of referring

**Art of the Ask — 3 steps:**
1. **Set the stage** — prime the conversation ("I'm going to ask you something in a moment")
2. **Listen for referral triggers** — signals someone knows a buyer/seller (moving mentions, life changes, job changes)
3. **Ask in peak referral state** — right after delivering great news or a win, when goodwill is highest

> "Ask in their peak referral state — right after the win, right after the good news. That's when they want to share."

Brandon Barnum grew from $20K to $200K in 18 months through this system.

## Market Adaptation (Aaron Novello)

In flat or declining markets:
- **Lead cash offer programs** attract motivated sellers who wouldn't list conventionally

## Niche Specialization — Pick Your Lane

> "You're never going to get a seller. You create content for buyers. So then you're going to get buyers. Is it that simple?"

The single most common lead generation mistake: posting the wrong content for the clients you want. You attract exactly what you post about. Content = client type, every time.

### Step 1 — Choose Your Lane: Buyers or Sellers

Decide which side of the transaction you want to dominate. Then create content exclusively for that side. If you want sellers, stop creating buyer content entirely until that lane is established.

**Real example:** A coaching client wanted sellers but kept getting buyers. Her entire social media was first-time buyer content, rates, starter homes. Gogo's diagnosis: *"You're never going to get a seller. You create content for buyers."* She switched lanes. The clients followed.

### Step 2 — Specialize Within the Lane

Once you've chosen a side, go deeper. What type of seller (or buyer) do you want?

| Property Type Options |
|----------------------|
| Ranch and land |
| Luxury / lakefront |
| Cookie cutter neighborhoods ($300–350K with kids, dogs, white picket fence) |
| Condos / townhomes / high-rises |
| New construction (caution: builder is the seller, not your client) |

**Volume vs. price argument:** A cookie-cutter neighborhood with 2,500 homes in your price range gives you more clients than a luxury niche under 6% of the market. Know the math before choosing.

### Step 3 — Specialize by Price Point

Your price point is determined by what you post.

> "If you post a $200,000 investor special, what type of buyers do you think you're going to find? You're going to find investors that are looking in the $200,000 range. If you post a million dollar home, luxury on a lake with 100 ft frontage, what type of buyers you going to find?"

**Price Point Framework:**
1. **What price range?** Pick your number.
2. **Where are those homes?** Write down 3–5 neighborhoods or sides of town.
3. **Who are those people?** Jobs, age, hobbies, where they hang out.
4. **What do they want to hear?** Create content around that (golf schedules, HOA events, school district news, community activities, local happenings).

### Step 4 — Enter Their World Physically

*This is what separates the agents who own a niche from those who talk about owning one.*

If your niche is a country club community at $500K+, **get a membership.** Show up. Meet one person → they refer their mother-in-law, their colleague who wants to live nearby, their buddy who golfs with them. You don't cold-call into a country club. You belong to it.

> "If you want to be known as the lakefront queen, then should you post flats downtown Detroit? No, you should post lakefront stuff."

### Step 5 — Infiltrate Niche Communities

*Source: [[Finding Your Ideal Clients and Targeting Them Effectively (Gogo Bethke)]]*

Joining communities is not enough. Go one step further — become a fixture.

**The Four Questions (answer in order):**
1. **Who are they?** Who has a natural interest in your niche? Write them all out.
2. **Where are they?** Every interest group has a home — Facebook groups, clubs, tournaments, local events.
3. **How can I find them?** Search Facebook for local clubs and tournaments. Show up where they gather.
4. **How can I talk to them?** Sponsor events, bring refreshments, reach out to admins, host your own events in their space.

**Lakefront example (live):**
- Bass fishing tournament every Wednesday on the lake → call the organizer → *"Can I speak? Can I be a sponsor? Can I bring Gatorades and water every Wednesday?"*
- They say yes. You show up with Coors Lights after a hot fishing day.
- Every fisherman on that lake now knows you as the lake agent.
- Same logic: join the Livingston County Boaters Club, the water sports group, the fish community Facebook page → ask to be admin or sponsor.

**Also create content around ALL aspects of the niche — not just real estate:**
- Water temperatures, bacteria alerts, local lake news, fishing tournament results, boating regulations
- *"Whatever is going on with the lakes locally, you are going to be posting it all over your social media."*
- Real estate content (prices, new listings) runs alongside this — not instead of it.

**ChatGPT shortcut:** *"I'm in [City, State]. There's a [niche] community here I want to specialize in. What should I post? Who are the people I need to talk to? Where do I find them?"* → ChatGPT outputs the full playbook. The more specific you are, the better the output.

> "Slow down enough to know what you want and then ask yourself: Where are they? Who are they? How can I find them? How can I talk to them? And then start doing those things."

### Step 6 — Find Your Niche Content

Two methods to discover what your niche actually wants to hear:

**Option A — Ask ChatGPT:**
Explain yourself: who you are, how many transactions you've done, your area, your desired niche and price range. *"What type of content should I create for [sellers of cookie-cutter homes in the $300–350K range]?"* GPT outputs ready-to-use ideas.

**Option B — Ask Your Audience:**
Post on Facebook: *"I'm creating content for 2026 and I specialize in [niche]. What questions do you have when thinking about [selling/buying]?"* They'll tell you. Create content from those answers.

### Step 6 — Lock In Your Niche Hashtag Set

Once your niche is defined, use ChatGPT to generate the 5 best hyper-local hashtags for it. See [[Social Media and Email Marketing#Hashtag Strategy]] for the full prompt method.

**The principle:** The more specific your niche, the smaller the competing pool, and the higher you rank for those searches. Don't say "waterfront in Michigan." Say "Chain of Lakes in Pinckney, Livingston County."

> "It is in your control. If you post a $500,000 house, you get $500,000 buyers. Million dollar house, you get a million dollar buyer."

## Niche Landing Page Strategy

> "If you're not known for anything, you're known for nothing."

A niche landing page is an IDX feed filtered to a specific property type or area — it auto-updates as new listings hit the MLS. Build once; drive traffic to it forever.

**How to choose your niches:** Pick 2–3 based on what makes you the most money, what you enjoy, and what you're already known for locally. These become your signature links — the destinations you repeatedly send traffic to from social media.

**How it works in the field:**

- On social media: *"Here's a home I showed on the Portage Chain — interested in everything else listed there? Click this link."*

**Weekly posting:** These niche links become your consistent social media destinations. You generate traffic to them week after week because they always have fresh listing content — you never have to create a new link.

> "These are the links that you are going to keep generating traffic back to because these are the things that make you the most amount of money, you enjoy, and that's what you want to be known for."

## MLS Alert System — Know First, Move First

*Source: [[Get Instant MLS Alerts for New Listings (GoGetEm Community)]]*

Set up a saved MLS search for your niche area and subscribe to ASAP notifications. The moment a new listing hits, you get an email — before most agents even open their laptop.

**Why this matters:** The 78% first-agent rule. 78% of buyers and sellers go with the first agent who contacts them. Knowing about listings the instant they go live gives you first-mover advantage for both buyer clients (send immediately) and prospecting calls to sellers in the area.

**Setup — Flex MLS (same logic applies in any MLS):**
1. Go to **Quick Search** in your MLS
2. Set status filter to **"Coming Soon" + "Active"** only
3. Set your area criteria — school district, city, or specific neighborhood matching your niche. Can also add price range or bedroom filters if desired.
4. Hit **Save Search** → name it clearly (e.g., "Just Listed — Howell School District")
5. Add yourself as a subscriber/contact
6. Set notification frequency to **ASAP** — never weekly. The whole point is knowing the instant it hits.
7. Save → done. Your MLS now emails you every new listing in real time.

> "I don't want it on weekly. I want ASAP. So I know as soon as that listing hits the market, it's going to notify me immediately. That's the most important thing."

**Niche application:** Set up one alert per niche area you're targeting. If you specialize in three ZIP codes, set up three separate saved searches — one per ZIP. You'll know the landscape of what's available before your clients even ask.

## Systematic Gifting as Relationship Prospecting (Katie Lawrence)

Gifting is not random — it is a systematic, consistent relationship-building tool that creates top-of-mind presence without requiring repeated outreach. [[Katie Lawrence]] (30-year mortgage LO, construction loan specialist) teaches this framework in the GoGet'Em community.

**Brand identity first.** Pick something authentic and own it completely. Katie's brand is pink flamingos — agents and clients bring her flamingo gifts, and when they see a flamingo on vacation they think of her. The goal: your name appears in someone's mind in an unrelated context because of the consistent association you've built.

**Gifting framework:**
1. **Brand** — one memorable, authentic identity marker
2. **Plan** — systematic: know who, what, and when; never random
3. **List** — maintained roster of who you're reaching out to
4. **Token gifts** — small, consistent door-openers; consistency beats cost every time
5. **Birthday club** — track every contact's birthday, send something every year; almost no one else does this → automatic top-of-mind advantage year after year
6. **New friendship drip** — ongoing contact rhythm for new relationships; warm them over time before you need anything

**For lenders:** Primary target = Realtors. For agents: primary target = past clients + prospective clients.

> "If I have a realtor that goes on vacation they'll say oh my gosh Katie I saw a flamingo and I thought of you." — Katie Lawrence

## Poker Run / Progressive Open House (Savannah Gutierrez)

[[Savannah Gutierrez]] (Brenham TX/DFW area) runs **poker run open houses**: 5 homes open simultaneously in the same neighborhood or area, each as one "poker stop." Buyers collect a playing card at each stop — best hand wins a prize.

**Why it works:**
- Creates a game/event out of what is normally a passive drive-by experience
- Buyers stay longer at each home because they're engaged in collecting a card
- Multi-home exposure in a single outing → higher buy probability
- Community event feel → other neighbors hear and get curious
- Low cost; vendors (lenders, title) often co-sponsor

Format: 5 homes, 5 stops, printed "poker passport," prize at the end (gift card, restaurant experience). Drives traffic to all 5 listings simultaneously.

## Older Adult Sellers — An Untapped Inventory Source (Erin DiCarlo)

[[Erin DiCarlo]] (Boston market, Dovetail Company, 22-year specialist):

**The "Move First, Sell Later" model:**
- Older adult moves to senior living community BEFORE their home is listed
- Home is then staged and prepped for sale properly
- Higher net sale price + less stress for the seller
- Agent earns: listing commission + referral from senior living community

**Dovetail Company:**

- Partners with senior living operators who pay referral fees for qualified move-ins

**Why most agents miss this:**
- Don't know how to have the conversation (sensitive topics: death, health decline, leaving the family home)
- Avoid families in crisis because it feels uncomfortable
- Miss the upstream referral stream from senior living operators

## Funnels and Automated Lead Systems (Gogo Bethke)

A funnel is a lead capture sequence — a series of pages or automations that convert a social media viewer into a contact in your CRM.

**Gogo's funnel architecture:**
- **Top of funnel:** Social media post or reel → link in bio or link in caption
- **Lead magnet page:** Landing page with one offer (home value, buyer guide, etc.) → must give email/phone to receive it
- **Auto-responder:** Immediate email/text sequence begins (Go High Level or ManyChat)
- **Nurture sequence:** Pre-written email/text drip over 30–90 days
- **CRM entry:** Lead is now in your database and receives ongoing touches

**Key principle:** Every touch point has one job — move the person to the next step. No page tries to do two things.

**Tools:** ManyChat (Instagram/Facebook comments and DMs), Go High Level (landing pages, CRM, email), BoldTrail (IDX squeeze pages), CRM Grow (video-gated capture).

> "A lead is not a lead until they are off the app. Get them into your world."

## Google Business Profile Optimization (Noemi Chavez)

[[Noemi Chavez]] trains the Team Gogo Wednesday GoGet'Em community (1,000+ members) on Google Business Profile — one of the most overlooked free marketing tools for real estate agents.

**The problem:** Most agents set up their profile once, post a few listings, and forget it. They're missing the vast majority of its power.

**What Google Business Profile actually does:** Controls how your business appears in Google Search and Maps when someone searches for a real estate agent in your area. Often the first thing a potential client sees — before your website, before social media.

**What to optimize:**
- Complete business information: hours, categories, services, description
- Reviews: how to ask, how to respond, how to make them work for you
- Google Posts: regular content updates that appear in search results
- Q&A: proactively seed and answer questions
- Photos: proper categories, quality, frequency
- Insights: understand how people find you and what action they take next

**Why it matters:** Clients Google you before they call you. What they find on your profile shapes their first impression and decision. Most agents have no idea what that impression currently looks like.

**Free tool.** ROI on time invested is exceptionally high for any agent who gets local search traffic.

> Her cousin [[Sotero Lopez]] (Team Gogo Wednesday host) asked her to review his profile — she showed him everything he was doing wrong. The transformation brought her in front of the full community.

## Two-Button Link-in-Bio — Filtering Agent Attraction vs. Real Estate

*Source: [[GoGet'Em Brand Audit Call — Amanda Richards and Angela Kane]]*

> "When I click that button, I need to know what I'm signing up for."

Any agent pursuing both real estate transactions AND agent/eXp attraction must separate their call-booking links in their bio. A single generic "Book a Call" button fails to filter — anyone can land there, and neither real estate prospects nor agent prospects know what they're signing up for.

**The fix — two distinct calendar buttons:**

| Button Label | Routes To |
|-------------|-----------|
| "Partner with me at eXp" | Agent attraction / eXp partnership conversation calendar |
| "Real estate inquiry" | Buyer/seller consultation calendar |

**Why this matters:**
- Each prospect self-selects their path before they reach your calendar
- Conversations are pre-qualified before they start
- Prevents random strangers from booking time with no intent or context
- Agent attraction pipeline and real estate pipeline stay separate — different follow-up, different messaging, different offers

Apply this anywhere you have a link-in-bio tool: Linktree, Beacons, Instagram's native link section, or any other platform.
