# Real Estate Teams

[[Gogo Bethke]]'s framework for building, structuring, and running a real estate team — from agent attraction philosophy through contracts, splits, and role assignments.

## Agent Attraction Philosophy

> "I'm looking for the people who are looking for me."

Don't try to attract everyone. Share who you are, your work ethic, and what you stand for — the right people self-select. The wrong people filter themselves out.

**What Gogo stands for** (and attracts around):
- Brand building
- Leveraging systems and VAs
- Building multiple companies and income streams
- Passive income
- Part-time work schedule (Mon–Wed) + time/financial/location freedom

**What she does NOT do** (and filters out):
- Cold calling
- Door knocking
- Buying Zillow/Trulia/realtor.com leads
- Mega-team model with ISC department and 85+ agents

> "If you want to become the best cold calling person on the face of the Earth, don't join my downline. That means you are not my people."

### "Everybody Is Getable"

No agent is truly off-limits. Two approaches:
1. **Find a pebble in their shoe** — identify what's currently bothering them about their brokerage or situation.
2. **Wait it out** — eventually the broker will do something that makes them call you first. Be patient and keep positioning.

> "Everybody is getable. You just need to find a pebble in their shoe, or you just have to wait it out. There will come a time where that broker is going to piss them off so bad that you are going to be the first one they will call."

## Ideal Agent Profile — ChatGPT Framework

> "Just like we have to have an ideal client on the buyers and sellers side, we also need to have an ideal client on the agent attraction side — because your low-hanging fruit are the people who are just like you."

Parallel to [[Social Media and Email Marketing#ChatGPT Ideal Client Framework]] for buyer/seller leads, this framework defines who your ideal agent recruit actually is — so your content speaks directly to them and repels everyone who isn't a fit.

**The low-hanging fruit principle for agent attraction:** The easiest agent to recruit is the agent who is you — one or two steps behind. You speak their language. You've lived their pain. You've handled their objections — not because you practiced, but because you were them a year ago.

**Gogo's ideal agent:** Produces $4–9M annually (already capped, not yet ICON), 2+ years in the business, full-time career (not a side hustle), individual top-producing solo agent. She can speak their language with her eyes closed and doesn't have to hand-hold. She is them, just a few years ahead.

### ChatGPT Prompt — 4 Sections

**1. Demographics:**
- Experience level (minimum years in industry)
- Age (Gogo's: ~35–45)
- Gender (Gogo's: predominantly female, but not exclusive)
- Career stage (full-time vs. part-time — fish for your target even if others self-select in)

**2. Psychographics:**
- What motivates them — financial freedom, work-life balance, higher commissions?
- Career goals — build a personal brand, grow a team, increase transaction volume?
- Pain points with current situation — lack of leads, poor communication, unfavorable splits, low support?
- Values when choosing a team — training, mentorship, flexibility, referral network?

**3. Business Model Preferences:**
- What commission structure appeals to them — splits, revenue share, stocks?
- What support do they want — lead gen, marketing, training, tech?
- What culture do they thrive in — collaborative, high energy, family-like?

**4. Technology & Social Behavior:**
- Tech comfort level (only promise what you or your upline can actually deliver)
- Tools they need — CRM, automations, social media support?
- Platforms they use — Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn?
- Content they engage with — success stories, income proof, agent growth tips?

**Closing prompt:** *"Based on these details, what content should I create to attract agents? What platform should I focus on for recruiting? How can I position my team or downline offer in a way that appeals to them?"*

**Output:** ChatGPT generates a full year's plan — content types, platforms, messaging angles, and positioning.

### The Inbound Model

> "I have never messaged agents out of the 1,660 agents that partnered with me. Literally, every single one of them messaged me."

When you speak to your ideal agent specifically — content, success stories, numbers, lifestyle — they raise their hand when they're ready. You don't pitch; they self-qualify. By the time they contact you, your content has already handled their objections. The conversation is 80% done before it starts.

**Success story strategy:** Share agents in your org who mirror your ideal recruit. Gogo's example: Cat West — came over, became multiple-times ICON, retired her husband, built a mortgage arm, grew a team of 150+. Prospects find themselves in the story: *"If she can do it, I can do it. I want to partner with you."*

**"If you're talking to everybody, you're talking to nobody."** Define the ideal agent → produce content for them → the right agents self-select in. Wrong agents filter themselves out before you waste a call.

## The Three-Way Call System

The three-way call is the closing mechanism in Gogo's agent attraction pipeline. After a prospect expresses interest (via ManyChat "partner" keyword or direct outreach), the Team Go member cannot always close them alone — so Gogo jumps on.

**How it works:**
1. Team Go member identifies an interested agent
2. Member invites prospect to a 30-min Zoom via the three-way call booking link
3. Prospect books → adds the member as a guest (required to complete booking)
4. All three — member, prospect, and Gogo — join the Zoom
5. Gogo closes the agent into eXp under the member's sponsorship

**Lives on:** The "Partner With Me" funnel page as a calendar booking button.

**GoHighLevel calendar setup (key settings):**
- Duration: 30 minutes; intervals: every hour and half-hour
- Scheduling notice: 12 hours minimum (prevents same-day bookings)
- Date range: 14-day rolling window
- **"Require guests for booking":** Must be ON — prevents random bookings. The member must add the agent prospect as a guest to complete the booking. Without this, anyone can schedule a three-way call with Gogo for any reason.
- Notifications: email + SMS for all parties at confirmation, reschedule, cancellation, and reminders — must be manually enabled after the calendar is saved (off by default in GHL)
- Confirmation: redirects to partner page; auto-confirms if calendar is available

**Template:** Available in the GSMC lesson description — swap "Gogo" for your name and update the partner page link.

## Revenue Share — How It Works

> "That is one of my most solid sources of income that made me a millionaire and a multi-millionaire."

Revenue share is income paid by eXp to agents who attract other agents to the brokerage. When your downline agent closes deals, eXp collects company revenue — and pays a portion of that back to you. Pays on or around the **20th of every month**, in USD. Works whether you're sleeping, traveling, or taking time off.

**Why Gogo chose revenue share over profit share:**
- At Keller Williams, she was earning profit share
- Realized: if the brokerage isn't profitable, there's nothing to share
- eXp pays revenue share regardless of company profitability — a fundamental difference

**The income stability argument:**
Real estate commissions are volatile. $90K one month; $0 for two months. Bills arrive on schedule every month — income doesn't. Revenue share fills that gap. *"What if you get pregnant? What if you get hit by a car? What if your parents get sick and you need to go home for three months? What would you do?"*

**Gogo's actual numbers (shared under disclaimer — for reference, not guarantees):**

| Period | Revenue Share |
|--------|--------------|
| March 2019 (first check) | $1,422.75 |
| April 2019 | $2,362 |
| May 2019 | $5,573 |
| Year 1 total | $44,000 |
| Year 2 total | $119,000 |
| Trend | Grows every year |

**Seasonal pattern:** Peaks in June–August (high real estate transaction season); lowest in December. Normal — not a sign of problems.

### Mechanics: FLA, FLQA & Level Unlocks (detailed — June 2026 Roundtable)

eXp pays revenue share 7 generations deep, funded from the company dollar (eXp works off 50% of revenue; ~10% feeds the rev-share bucket). **You never pay your sponsor** — it comes from the company side.

- **FLA = Frontline Active** — an agent personally sponsored to you who pays the $85/month fee. May never sell ("dead weight").
- **FLQA = Frontline Qualified Active** — qualifies by collecting **$5,000 in GCI/commission** (NOT two transactions — could be 5 leases at $1,000, a referral, any income type). Each $5,000 qualifies the agent for **6 months**, then they must re-qualify.
- **Level-unlock math:** 1 FLQA opens Level 1; then **every 5 FLQAs opens the next level** — 5→L2, 10→L3, 15→L4, 20→L5, 25→L6, **30→L7** (eXp 2.0 lowered L7 from 40 to 30 FLQAs).
- **Bonus overrides:** ICON agents have **all 7 levels open** automatically; capping agents have **levels 1–5**; brand-new agents have **levels 1–3** (and levels 1–3 are open all the time, not just year one). First-year agents also earn a **$4,000 cap bonus** per qualifying FLQA who caps in their first year; ~$1,400/frontline agent/year thereafter.
- **Per-generation annual caps:** L1 $1,400 · L2 $1,600 · L3 $1,000 · L4 $600 · L5 $400 · L6 $1,000 · L7 $2,000.
- **Your own production is irrelevant to rev share.** Gogo hasn't sold a house in 3.5 years and isn't even an FLQA to her own sponsor ([[Kurt Shewell]]). Rev share depends on your downline's production and your FLQA count.
- **Rule of thumb:** ~half your FLAs become FLQAs (Gogo: 98 FLAs / ~46–47 FLQAs) — "if you need 30, go get 60."
- **Off-boarding:** departed agents stay in your dashboard for a **12-month grace period** (was 6 months) to re-name the same sponsor; pending/new-construction deals can keep them showing up to 2 years — this distorts raw dashboard counts.
- Gogo's lifetime rev share: **~$4.3M over 8 years** (she + Dwayne ~$5M across two lines).

**How agents joined:** 100% organic from social media. No paid ads, no cold calling to recruit agents. Social media content + consistency = inbound agent attraction.

**Timeline reality:** Nobody switches brokerages on day one. It takes months for agents to join, start transacting, and for revenue to flow back to you. Start now; be patient.

**Two audiences in every social media following:** Buyers/sellers (commission income) and agents (revenue share income or referral income). You have no control over who follows you — both are already there. Content strategy monetizes both simultaneously.

**Blank revenue share tracker:** Available below the GSMC video. Download, make a copy, track from day one. "The only reason you should ever look back is to see how far you've come."

> "I cannot be in a situation where I can't [rely on income]. So it's that guarantee that money is coming in right here."

Ideal = $4–7M producers (know paperwork, taste for money, cap → guaranteed rev share, coachable to ICON) plus "two steps behind you" agents who need systems.

5. **The attraction funnel:** post content → **"comment OR DM 'partner'"** (serious agents won't publicly comment where their broker sees) → ManyChat auto-message → videos → booking calendar. **Block your calendar ~2 weeks out even when free** (manufactured scarcity). Requiring the videos + the wait pre-qualifies prospects → near-100% close by call time.
6. **Three-way call** (you + agent + Gogo) — etiquette below.

9. **Reverse-engineer their numbers** from the MLS or a lender and lead with money: "I ran your production over coffee — you paid RE/MAX ~$37K last year; here you'd bank $16K. I can make you $57K more doing the same thing." (Max Powers "you're bleeding money" cold-DM — 3 states / 3 brokerages / 3 caps.)
10. **Tools:** a dedicated attraction *website* (not a hodge-podge of links), consistent video, wins/screenshots, onboarding systems (Team Gogo's $25 onboarding), and celebrate/gift agents — "what gets celebrated gets duplicated."

### The Multiplication Math (why level 6 & 7 change everything)

Done live with any prospect: put *your* believed 12-month number on line 1, then multiply by 2 down 7 generations (assuming each agent brings just 2). A front line of 50 → 100 → 200 → 400 → 800 → 1,600 → **3,200 on level 7**. Per-generation annual pay (L1 $1,400 · L2 $1,600 · L3 $1,000 · L4 $600 · L5 $400 · L6 $1,000 · **L7 $2,000**) makes that example ~**$10.48M/yr** at full build; even halved (the "50% suck" rule) it's ~$5M/yr. First-year sponsor bonus is **$4,000/capping agent**. This is why attraction must be done at a brokerage with **levels 6 & 7** — where most of the downline and the biggest checks eventually sit. Illustration Gogo uses: **Gene Frederick** (sponsored by **Elizabeth Riley**) carries ~5,000 more agents than his sponsor; that gap is Gene's L7 (Elizabeth's would-be L8) = ~**$10M/yr on level 7 alone**.

## "Monkey Does Monkey Do" — Social Proof Content Strategy

Not every agent attraction post should be a direct pitch. The most powerful recruiting tool is showing agents already in your organization succeeding — and letting viewers draw their own conclusions.

**The principle:** *"Sometimes the best way of convincing someone to take a certain action is by showing them that somebody else already did."*

**"Monkey does monkey do"** — Gogo's coined phrase. People watch each other, get inspired, and mirror behavior. When a prospect sees *Jessica* succeed in your org, the thought forms: *"If she did it, I can do it. I should join too."*

**Content types that demonstrate social proof:**
- Agents achieving ICON status, capping for the first time, building teams
- Team culture moments: masterminds, Friendsgiving, events, vacations together
- Agents at your home, at your condo, at conferences with you
- Celebrating agents in your org in posts and Stories (top producers, top attractors, icons of the month)

**Why Gogo celebrates monthly winners:** Not vanity — strategy. Each shoutout is a recruiting post that never feels like recruiting.

**Content goal:** Plant ideas. Put thoughts into the viewer's head. Let them process. *"All you need to do is put ideas into their head. Let them process it a little bit and when they are ready, they will raise their hand."*

**Mix of content types on Fridays:**
- Direct pitch (2–3x/quarter): straight CTA, "here's why you should join eXp"
- Social proof (most weeks): backstage pass into the org — let the community sell itself

> "I know I'm telling you business is a spiritual game."

## Downline Organization — Social Media Profiles

As your org grows, your personal profile gets overwhelmed with agent shoutouts, celebration posts, and team content. The solution: **a separate Instagram page and Facebook page for your downline/team organization.**

**Step 1 — Name your org.** Gogo's: "Gogo's Real Estate Team" (local team) → "Team Gogo" / "Team Gogo International Family of Agents" (downline)

**Step 2 — Create separate Instagram + Facebook pages.** Link them so posting on one posts on the other automatically.

**What goes on the org page (not your personal profile):**
- Agent achievements (capping, ICON status, team building, production records)
- Team events, masterminds, celebrations
- Benefits the org provides
- Agent Stories and production shoutouts — "Team Gogo all day every day"

**What stays on your personal profile:**
- Your personal brand content
- Agent-related content you specifically choose to feature

**Why separate:**
- Agents feel like they belong somewhere — the org page is their home
- Your personal profile stays focused on your personal brand (buyers, sellers, your voice)
- As the org grows to hundreds of agents, you cannot manage all that volume on one profile

**Sponsor shopping use case:** When a prospect is evaluating whether to join you, send them to the org page. They can see the community, find agents they know, and reach out directly. More credible than forwarding a curated list of names.

**Management:** Full-time VA manages the Team Gogo Instagram page — follows agents, reposts their tags, shares their production, helps agents know each other. *"When you get to the point where you have hundreds of agents and revenue share is coming in, you're more than happy to pay a VA to manage that account for you."*

**Homework:** Name your org. Create the Facebook page and group. Create and link the Instagram page.

## Team Contract

A written contract is non-negotiable. Without one, agents get comfortable and there's nothing to hold them to.

**How Gogo built hers:** Consulted Tina Call and Mark Z (both mega-team leaders at eXp) for starting templates. Took what was useful from each, then added her own requirements — specifically **social media marketing standards**, which neither mega-team template included.

Key areas covered in a team contract:
- Commission splits
- Social media requirements
- Listing/buyers specialist responsibilities
- Accountability and performance standards
- Team restrictions (what agents can/cannot do)

> "To be number one, you have to have a contract and hold your agents to it. If not, they're going to walk all over you."

## Splits and Cap Structure at eXp

Gogo runs a **standard team** at [[eXp Realty]], which allows:
- **Reduced caps** for her agents: **$8,000** vs. the standard **$16,000**
- In exchange, she must charge a minimum of **25%** on every single transaction (no exceptions — eXp legal requirement)

The math: offering a reduced cap is a benefit to the agent. The 25% minimum ensures the team leader's business remains profitable.

> "In order to make sure I'm not just taking agents and giving them a reduced cap while taking company dollar away from the company, I have to take a minimum of 25% on every single deal."

**Run the math before building.** Know your brokerage's requirements, what your team structure costs, and build pricing accordingly. "You're not running a nonprofit, you're running a profitable business."

## Team Roles

Core positions to hire in order of need:

2. **Buyers Specialist** — works with buyers under the team leader
3. **Listing Specialist** — handles listing side from start to finish

Each role has specific responsibilities and accountability expectations. The contract spells these out before hiring.

## Agent to CEO — Business Evolution (John Kitchens)

### 8 Stages of Business Evolution

Agent → Team Leader → Team Owner → Organization Builder → Industry Influencer → Legacy Business

Each stage requires letting go of the identity and habits that made the prior stage successful. The agent who succeeds by being the best salesperson must become an operator, then a leader, then a builder.

### Machine Mindset

The business is a machine. As CEO, your job is to work *on* the machine — designing, improving, and maintaining systems — not to be a component running *inside* it. When you're doing the transactions, you're in the machine. When you're building the systems that do the transactions, you're on the machine.

### Theory of Constraints

Identify the single bottleneck limiting your throughput. Fix it. The next bottleneck becomes visible. Fix that one. Repeat indefinitely. Most agents fail to grow because they attack symptoms rather than the root constraint.

### Traffic → Conversion → Fulfillment

The three levers of every real estate business:
- **Traffic** — lead generation, awareness, inbound interest
- **Conversion** — turning leads into clients
- **Fulfillment** — delivering outstanding service and creating raving fans

Most agents over-invest in traffic while ignoring conversion (scripts, follow-up, nurture) and fulfillment (service excellence, client experience). Income compounds in conversion and fulfillment.

### Vision Architecture

Vision = Core Values + Purpose + BHAG

- **Core Values** — the filter for hiring, culture, and decisions
- **Purpose** — why the organization exists beyond revenue
- **BHAG** — Big Hairy Audacious Goal; 10-year target that makes 90-day goals feel urgent

Without a clear vision, every hiring decision, strategic call, and culture conversation requires reinvention from scratch. Vision makes all those decisions pre-made.

## Working Genius — Team Map (Tina Nicole / Patrick Lencioni)

### The Six Types

| Code | Name | Strength | Craves | Crushed by |
|------|------|----------|--------|------------|
| W | Wonder | Ideation, big-picture thinking | Purpose | Pressure to execute without context |
| I | Invention | New systems, creative solutions | Freedom | Constraint |
| D | Discernment | Gut evaluation, poking holes | Being trusted | "Prove it" |
| G | Galvanizing | Rallying people, pushing action | Reaction | Apathy |
| E | Enablement | Supporting others, team glue | Appreciation | Being overlooked |
| T | Tenacity | Finishing, following through | Completion | Open loops |

### Hiring by Missing Genius

Before posting a job, identify which genius types are absent from the current team. A team missing T (Tenacity) will start things and never finish them. A team missing D (Discernment) will get excited about bad ideas and greenlight them. Hire the gap, not the headcount.

### Gogo's Team Profile

Gogo is GT (Galvanizing + Discernment). She is the only G on her team — which is why pushing people to action always defaults to her. Knowing this is a team-design insight, not a personal failing.

## People & Team Building Systems (John Kitchens — Ep. 149)

### Functional Accountability Chart (FAC)

From Verne Harnish's *Scaling Up*. Every seat has a function (what it exists to accomplish) and a single accountable owner. Not a hierarchy — a function map. Any function without an owner will not get done.

### Agreements vs. Expectations

> "Expectations are what you assume. Agreements are what you committed to."

Team conflict almost always stems from unspoken expectations masquerading as mutual agreements. Write down every agreement: what, by whom, by when. Never leave expectations implied.

### The Enthusiastic Rehire Test

For every team member, ask: "Would I enthusiastically rehire this person right now?" If the answer is not an immediate yes — it's a no. This is the most honest performance review in three seconds.

### Ones and Threes

Team members are scored in binary:
- **1** = performing at or above expectations — invest, develop, retain
- **3** = underperforming — performance plan, limited runway

There is no 2. No "he's kind of fine." If you can't call someone a 1, they're a 3. The system forces honest assessment and prevents drift.

### Compensation Drives Behavior

The comp structure is the behavior design. Commission-only agents optimize for their own production. Revenue share participants think about recruitment. Salary + bonus agents optimize for KPI targets. Before trying to change a team member's behavior, audit whether the comp structure rewards the behavior you want.

## VA Structure — Gogo's Model

- **Kristy (Director of Operations, 14 years):** Finances, bookkeeping, contracts, NDA reviews, tax support, technology, all hiring processes — "TinGogo Mama"
- **Alejandra (Executive Assistant, Argentina):** Calendar, emails, Trello board, travel logistics, attends every call
- **Cross-training mandate:** Four VAs assigned to every critical function so no single departure breaks operations
- **Delegation of hiring:** Kristy and Alejandra hire for their own departments — they're the ones training new hires, so they select new hires

### 90/10 Profit Principle

Target: keep 90% of revenue, spend only 10% on team and systems. Gogo's actual: $1.5M revenue, $143K total team cost (≈9.5%). Run lean before scaling wide.

### Hire Before You Can Afford It

> "Make the hire right before you can afford to make the hire."

Kristy was hired before Gogo could truly afford her. The hire creates the capacity; the capacity creates the income. The math only works in that direction.

## Agent-to-CEO Case Study — Beth Manning

Beth Manning grew from $2.7M to $10M in production in a single year after joining Team GoGo and eXp. She attributes this entirely to two things: plugging into every training (Wednesdays — production, Thursdays — tech, Fridays — agent attraction) and deliberately associating with people performing at a higher level.

Her agent-to-CEO teaching:
- **CEO works ON the business, not IN it.** The shift is identity-level, not operational.
- **Build systems while still producing.** Don't exit production prematurely — cash flow bridge is required.
- **Delegate lowest-value activities first.** Start with tasks that consume the most time at the lowest effective hourly rate.
- **Association is the mechanism.** "It's because of the people I associated with." Proximity to higher performers accelerates growth in ways no course or strategy can replicate.
- **Nervousness is a signal, not a stop sign.** Beth's advice: the more nervous you are about putting yourself out there, the more you should. That discomfort is exactly where growth lives.

## Leverage and Life Design — Kayla Lindsey

Kayla Lindsey (Charlotte, NC) runs multiple six-figure businesses as a single mother while surfing, spending time with her son, and going to the beach. Her principle: making money and having a life are not mutually exclusive — but most agents unconsciously build one at the expense of the other.

The shift from operator to CEO:
- **Systems and processes first.** Output must be able to happen without your personal presence.
- **People who extend your capacity.** VAs, team members, contractors who handle what's below your effective hourly rate.
- **Structural changes, not marginal improvements.** Kayla made specific, deliberate shifts — not incremental tweaks — that freed her time while income continued.

The GoGet'Em community response was so strong that Kayla created a full course on the framework.

## Leadership Determines Business Size (John Kitchens — Ep. 184)

The constraint on any real estate business is not the market, not the economy, not the number of leads — it is the leader. Your business cannot grow beyond the level of your self-leadership.

**Self-leadership first:** Before you can lead a team, lead yourself. Discipline, follow-through, standard-setting — all downstream from the leader's own habits and identity.

**Business elastic to leadership:** When you raise your leadership ceiling, the business expands to match. When leadership stagnates, the business plateaus regardless of market conditions.

**Application:** Honey Badger Nation (Kitchens' 7,000+ member community) is built on this single principle: the agents who grow fastest are those who invest most seriously in becoming better leaders.

## Market Resilience — Staying the Course (Aaron Novello)

In a historically down market (2024 — fewer transactions than 2008), the agents who survive and rebuild are those who maintain activity levels without pivoting or quitting at the worst moment.

- **Don't make permanent decisions in temporary seasons.** Most markets cycle — the agents who stop prospecting when results are invisible miss the rebound.
- **Disappointment seasons are instructive.** The lessons available in hard markets cannot be learned in easy ones. Agents who endure build knowledge and skill that insulates them from the next downturn.
- **Consistency during low results.** Activity without immediate return feels irrational — but it is exactly the investment that pays in the next cycle.

## Husband-Wife Team Model (Kat and Jeremy)

[[Kat]] and [[Jeremy]] — multiple ICONs at eXp — model what a high-functioning husband-wife real estate team looks like when each partner operates in their lane.

**Division of labor:**
- Kat: client-facing, listing presentations, buyer consultations, relationship management
- Jeremy: new construction specialist (700+ transactions), contract review, builder negotiations

**Why it works:**
- Complementary skills without overlap; neither partner doing the other's job
- Shared mission and shared financial stakes create alignment
- Jeremy's 25-year corporate background brings systems discipline; Kat's strength is client rapport

**Lessons for other agent-pairs:**
- Define lanes clearly from day one — shared work without clear ownership creates conflict
- Run buyer consultations together when the client relationship benefits from two perspectives
- The buyer agreement becomes easier to present when both partners present as a unified team

## Niche Team Model — Older Adult Specialists (Erin DiCarlo / Dovetail)

[[Erin DiCarlo]] (22-year specialist, Dovetail Company, Boston) runs what amounts to a full-service concierge team for older adult clients rather than a traditional real estate team.

**Dovetail's structure:**
- Real estate agent (listing and buyer representation)
- Senior living coordinator (connects to independent living, assisted living, memory care)
- Downsizing specialist (decluttering, estate sales, charitable donation coordination)
- Moving coordinator (full physical move management)

**Revenue model:**
- Listing commission from home sale
- Senior living operator referral fee (for qualifying move-ins)
- Ancillary service fees from coordinated vendors

**Why this is a team model, not just a niche:**
- The complexity of older adult transitions requires multiple specialists
- One agent cannot be all of the above without burning out
- Each team member earns from the relationship; the agent doesn't need to do everything

## Faith-Based Agent Attraction — Instagram to Rev Share (Cat West)

*Source: [[What Top Agents Do Differently on Instagram - Month 5 (Gogo Bethke, Allison, Cat West)]]*

[[Cat West]] (Oviedo FL; 8 years in real estate; [[eXp Realty]]; Gogo's business partner ~5 years) is Gogo's proof case that Instagram converts to **revenue share**, not just GCI — the [[#Ideal Agent Profile — ChatGPT Framework|ideal-agent / inbound model]] executed through a faith-based brand.

**Results (last 12 months):** organization of **120 agents**, **28 personally sponsored** (~28 attracted via Instagram), **~$100K revenue share**, **555 agent leads** in database, 10 coaching clients. **Agent avatar (the recruit-side ideal client):** someone selling **$5M+/year, who loves Jesus, and is a mom of young kids.** Cat deliberately wants wrong-fit followers to leave: *"the people who stay are going to be your raving fans and they're going to binge all of your content."* Brand = Christian top-producer coaching realtors *"to scale without burnout."* Bio as storefront: line 1 = who I am, line 2 = who I help, line 3 = lead magnet (3-day faith devotional for realtors). Keyword/CTA is "partner."

**The reels-attract / stories-convert mechanic.** Agent attraction is a long game — the best agents take **6 months to 1.5 years** of relationship-building before joining. The division of labor across content types:
- **Reels attract** — top-of-funnel reach (farming/mailer reels, faith content, "what eXp has done for me").
- **Stories build the relationship and convert** — *"it happens in the DMs."* Lifestyle content proves she's a real person.
- **"People are buying you, not eXp."** Echoed by [[Allison]]: *"Nobody's buying eXp… they're buying you."* The brand and the relationship — not the brokerage pitch — do the attracting, reinforcing the [[#Agent Attraction Automation (ManyChat)|"facts tell, stories sell"]] principle.

This validates the two-audience model: the same following holds buyers/sellers (GCI) and agents (rev share). Cat monetizes the agent side through consistent faith-anchored content, a dedicated "partner" keyword, and a separate attraction booking link.

## Partnership Exits & Full-Service Delegation (Circle coaching)

*Source: de-identified [[The Circle|Circle]] coaching (NDA — no member specifics). [[Gogo Bethke]]'s generalizable teaching only.*

### Partnerships are the first ship that sinks

> "Partnerships are the first ship that sinks."

Few real-estate partnerships survive past **~2–2.5 years** — that's the window where true colors show. The classic fracture: one partner outworks the other (the **over-worker**), while the other clings to a small guaranteed draw and won't reinvest to grow (the **penny-pincher**). Lead the business **with your head, not your heart** — a partner's inability to survive without you is not your problem to solve. "Crab in the bucket": a partner dragging your profit down is a reason to get out and fly free.

### The clean-break exit script

Ending a partnership in three moves:
1. **Lead with your *why*.** "I'm in a season seeking freedom — from stress, financially — and not growing gives me only stress; I've decided to remove myself." Frame it as *your* phase, not their failure.
2. **Make a suggestion and hold it loosely.** "I'd like the brand name, but if it matters to you, take it." **The less you fight for something, the more they hand it over** — the more you want it, the less they want to give it.
3. **Set a hard deadline** to sign the separation.

Keeping the brand is often an **ego decision**: if most production is yours, the brand *is* you regardless of the name.

> "The less you fight them, the more they give it to you; the more you want it, the less they want to give it to you."

**Post-departure referral clause > non-compete.** A non-compete against a *licensed* agent generally won't hold in court; a clause entitling you to a **referral % (25–50%) on business from your sphere or trained agents even after they leave** does. Reframe "they stole my client" as proof you train experts — and you still get paid "without doing the work." *(General education, not legal advice.)*

### Small team as the exit strategy

The preferred model is a **small team, no office/overhead, run your way** — replace yourself with **~2 people** (e.g., a buyer's agent covering weekends, showings, and open houses) so you stop trading weekends. This can out-earn solo years.

**Be a solid boss** — clear expectations + weekly accountability:
- One **weekly team meeting** + a **Friday check-in** where agents submit an activity-tracker ("greatness tracker") screenshot.
- Hold the line (open houses mandatory).
- **Fire what drains you** — agents who won't do the work or self-serve are a net loss; *shrinking* the team can raise both profit and quality of life.

### Rotating meeting cadence & working-session culture

- **Piggyback the meeting on an existing anchor** (e.g., a weekly org training) so members already come for value.
- **Rotating monthly rhythm** gives *you* structure while feeling *fresh to them* — e.g., Week 1 operations/brokerage updates; Week 2 state/regulatory updates; Week 3 scripts/role-play/tech/Q&A; Week 4 an open higher-level training.
- **Meetings are working sessions + community, not lectures** — gather, teach briefly, then co-work (stuff envelopes, pop-bys, cold-call together, role-play). **Watch them work** (CRM, follow-up, client process) and coach in real time, since their work reflects on your reputation.
- **On/off calendar rhythm** — don't strand a lone 1-hour meeting mid-week; cluster so agents get "one day on, one day off."
- **Community markets itself** — film the fun/productive team days; prospects ask to join and members bring recruits to experience the culture. "My house became the office."
- **Pre-pave the frustration of leadership** — most people won't take advantage of free training even when handed to them. High-achievers wrongly assume everyone shares their drive; warn yourself in advance so disappointment doesn't derail you. "Some people are happily miserable. And you have to let them."

### Hire your weaknesses; know your genius; cap your capacity

- **Hire your weaknesses from day one** — "know your weaknesses and hire them out from day one, or you'll hate your job." Gogo hasn't logged into the transaction platform in 8 years; hiring everything out is her #1 reason for low stress.
- **Know your genius, staff the rest** — relationship "quarterbacks" get the appointment and hand off; pair with detail-oriented executors.
- **Capacity reality:** client experience degrades past **~5 concurrent listings** — cap it, **co-list** (50–60% of *something* beats burnout), or build systems.

### Fight your TC to full-service

Avoid transaction coordinators who only do contract-to-close (leaving you to enter MLS, order photos/title). A true **full-service TC** goes signed listing → MLS input → orders photographer, pre-title, pre-inspection. You'll have to **fight the TC company** to actually do it — leverage your volume. Standardize vendors so hand-off is turnkey.

### Leverage math: 2× is harder than 10×

> "2× is harder than 10×."

A 2× goal lets you keep doing it yourself; a true multiple forces systems and leverage. The math: **four team members × 40 hrs = 160 hrs/week** running without you. Pair with **"subtract before you add"** — decide what you'll stop doing to make room to grow: "growth without it is just more chaos."

## Hiring, Trials & Split Structure (Circle coaching)

*Source: [[The Circle Coaching — Genius Zone, Delegation Systems and Building the Right Team (de-identified)]]. Private [[The Circle|Circle]] coaching, NDA — no member specifics; [[Gogo Bethke]]'s generalizable teachings only.*

### You can't change people — hire to strengths

> "You cannot change somebody, no matter how much you show in front of them."

Stop trying to convert a poor-fit hire into a version of you. Keep people **in their lane**: give a reliable single-transaction-type closer *more* of what they're good at and remove friction (hand off the paperwork they resist) rather than forcing a role they'll never grow into.

**Delegate ideation, not just execution.** Slow down and ask "what ideas do *you* have?" — it surfaces concepts your own brain can't reach, shows how each person thinks, and keeps the work energizing for them. (Extends [[#Hire your weaknesses; know your genius; cap your capacity|hire-your-weaknesses]] from execution into thinking.)

> "Slow down enough where you can give time and space to your team and let them bring you things."

### The cost-of-a-mis-hire math

Frame a wrong-seat hire as a concrete lost-revenue number, not a salary line:

**(# lead opportunities given) × (typical conversion) × (avg transaction value) × (commission %) = revenue the wrong person *lost* you.**

Seeing the real dollar figure — not the paycheck — is what clarifies the decision to move on. Pairs with the [[People & Team Building Systems (John Kitchens — Ep. 149)#The Enthusiastic Rehire Test|Enthusiastic Rehire Test]] and [[#Ones and Threes|Ones and Threes]].

### The "glorified assistant" trap

Bringing someone in as an **equal / high-split partner when they can't actually generate business** is an expensive, repeatable mistake. A person who can't self-source is an assistant on a partner's comp — diagnose generation ability *before* setting the split.

### "Let them eliminate themselves" — the time-boxed trial

Avoid the emotional cost of firing by setting a **time-boxed performance trial with a clear, objective bar**. Missing the bar means they remove *themselves* from the privilege.

> "I'll give you 90 days / X open houses. If you don't secure at least one buy-side client, you lose the right to hold opens and keep doing [what you're good at]. It's not me firing you — you eliminated yourself."

**Hire two, keep one.** For a lead-conversion seat, put **two agents on a 90-day trial**, create healthy competition, and track everyone's numbers (opens held, traffic, follow-ups, conversions, closings). Keep the closer.

### Mandatory meeting standards

> "I run a business over here, and this is a job."

**Mandatory means mandatory.** On time every time; message ahead if truly delayed; a no-show or late-without-notice → **warning → exit**, regardless of relationship. **Schedule mandatory calls early (9:00 a.m.)** so later appointments are never an excuse. Seasonal calendar flexibility is a **leadership choice you make deliberately**, not one the team imposes on you. (Reinforces the [[#Rotating meeting cadence & working-session culture|weekly meeting + Friday check-in]] cadence.)

### Diagnose the real need: assistant vs. showing agent vs. VA

Most "I need an assistant" problems are actually **online problems** solvable via screen-share with existing virtual help. Three non-interchangeable roles:

1. **In-person assistant** — a body physically required in the room.
2. **Showing agent** — paid per showing.
3. **Virtual assistant / accountability partner** — online admin + holds your calendar.

**Affordable in-person model:** a stay-at-home parent on a school-hours (~20 hrs/wk) schedule for physical listing tasks. Diagnose which role the pain actually calls for before hiring the expensive one.

### A split structure that rewards self-generation

- **Listing side ~60/40** to the rainmaker (who covers photography, signage, marketing) — a 50/50 listing split leaves the owner unprofitable.
- **Buy side ~50/50** baseline.
- **Self-generated leads** (the agent's own sphere/farming, zero team connection): give the agent **~5–10% more** (e.g., 55/45 in their favor).
- **Team-handed leads:** the more the team hands over, the *less* the agent's share.
- **Define "self-generated" tightly** — a lead met *at your open house* or from *your sign or database* is a **team lead**, not self-generated.

> "Why would you expect to be handed anything in this life? Nothing gets handed."

**Honor existing agreements; roll new structures into the *next* hire's contract** rather than re-trading with current agents. (Connects to [[#Team Contract|the written team contract]] and [[#Splits and Cap Structure at eXp|eXp split mechanics]].)
