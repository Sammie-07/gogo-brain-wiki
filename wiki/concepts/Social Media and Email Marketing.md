# Social Media and Email Marketing



[[Gogo Bethke]]'s approach to marketing ‚Äî built entirely on consistency, showing up as herself, and treating content creation as her primary green-light (money-making) activity. The full system combines organic social, automation, and email into a lead-generation machine that runs whether or not she's at her desk.

## The Storefront Rule ‚Äî Feed Curation

*Source: [[Remove Old, Confusing Branding (Instagram Feed Audit)]]*

> "Think of it when someone lands on your Instagram as your storefront. If I had a physical store and I was selling real estate, but my storefront window had a mannequin in yoga outfits, sushi, a cat, a dog, three kids, and pictures from Italy, what would you think I'm selling?"

Your Instagram and Facebook **feed** is your business storefront. It must communicate one thing at all times: real estate. A confused visitor does not become a client.

**What does NOT belong in your feed:**
- Kids, pets, food, travel, sports, yoga ‚Äî unless the post itself is about real estate
- Anything that would confuse a stranger about what you sell

**What IS allowed:**
- Personal elements (yoga pants, lifestyle aesthetics) are fine **if what you're talking about is real estate**
- Kids/pets can appear if the post is about a real estate moment that happened while they were present (e.g., getting a referral at a kids' game)
- Max **2 holiday posts per year** in the feed (Christmas, Mother's Day, etc.)

**Stories vs. Feed:**
- Stories disappear in 24 hours ‚Äî safe for personal moments, kids, behind-the-scenes
- Feed is permanent and is the first thing a stranger sees ‚Äî treat it like a billboard

**Privacy argument:** Once your account reaches millions of views, exposing your children to strangers becomes a real risk. Keep them off public feeds entirely.

**How to clean up an existing account:**
- Do NOT start a new account ‚Äî rebuilding an audience is too hard
- Go through your existing feed and **delete every post** unrelated to real estate
- Move personal content to a private personal account or Stories going forward

> "Your feed is your storefront. Your stories appear for 24 hours and then disappear."

> "If you're proud of your kids, post it in your stories, not your feed."

## "If You Talk to Everyone, You Convert No One"

The starting point of Gogo's entire marketing philosophy: **define your ideal client first.** Don't target by price point. Target by who you are, what you love, and who you enjoy spending time with.

> "The fastest way to get miserable commissions is to work with people you have nothing in common with."

**Your ideal client = someone similar to you.** You already speak their language, go to their places, share their interests. You meet them naturally.

*Gogo's example:* When she was selling, her ideal client was young families ‚Äî 30s, kids, pets, wanted land/barn, Livingston County Michigan ($350k avg). She met them at school drop-off and zoo field trips. Content should reflect who you are ‚Üí attracts your people ‚Üí naturally repels mismatches.

### Fishing in the Right Pond

> "If you want to sell million-dollar homes, but you keep talking about the starter tiny houses, you're never going to catch a $3‚Äì4 million whale. You're fishing in the wrong pond."

The fishing metaphor is Gogo's clearest framing of why most agents struggle with lead quality. A whale doesn't live in your backyard pond. You can fish all day ‚Äî every day, consistently ‚Äî and never catch one if you're in the wrong water.

**The spaghetti alternative:** Without a defined ideal client, marketing is spaghetti on the wall ‚Äî random commissions when something sticks. With a defined ideal client, the system works like clockwork: you know who they are, where to find them, and what to say.

**Lowest-hanging fruit = people just like you right now.** You share their life stage, their challenges, their social circles. You meet them naturally. That shared experience is the trust foundation ‚Äî and trust converts.

**Redo the exercise every 5‚Äì10 years.** You change ‚Üí your audience changes. Gogo's ideal client at 25‚Äì35 was young moms she met at school pickup. At mid-40s, that group is gone from her life ‚Äî and she'd have no way to reach them even if she tried. Staying loyal to an old ideal client profile is why tactics that worked 10 years ago fail today.

### ChatGPT Ideal Client Framework

Gogo provides a prompt (linked below each GSMC video) with four sections to fill in and paste into ChatGPT. ChatGPT outputs a tailored client profile + platform + content recommendations.

**Section 1 ‚Äî Demographics:**
Age, gender, marital status, family size, income level, occupation. Be specific ‚Äî Gogo's early clients were 25‚Äì35, mostly stay-at-home moms, household income $60‚Äì120K, husbands in trades or engineering. That specificity tells you everything: where they hang out, what content they consume, what they need.

**Section 2 ‚Äî Psychographics:**
- What are their main desires and goals in buying/selling?
- What problems or pain points do they face during the process? (e.g., cash-poor first-timers ‚Üí negotiate appliances into deals)
- What lifestyle do they want ‚Äî city, suburban, luxury, land, rural?
- What values do they prioritize in an agent ‚Äî trust, experience, market knowledge, existing relationship?

**Section 3 ‚Äî Buying & Selling Motivation:**
Why are they transacting right now? Growing family needs more space? Kids moved out ‚Üí downsizing? First home out of a rental? Knowing their motivation tells you what to put in the first 3 seconds of any video or caption.

**Section 4 ‚Äî Social Media & Online Behavior:**
- Which platforms do they use most? (Elderly clients ‚Üí not TikTok)
- What content do they engage with?
- What real estate questions do they ask online? ‚Üí Answer those questions in your posts before they ask

**Closing prompt:** *"Based on these details, what specific content should I create to attract these clients? And where should I focus my marketing efforts ‚Äî online platforms, lead generation, ads, lead magnets?"*

### The 7-Year Rule ‚Äî Entertaining the Off-Market Audience

People are on the real estate market approximately **every 7 years**. For 6.5 of those years, they don't care about listings, interest rates, or market updates.

**If your content is 100% real estate, they unfollow after closing.** Sprinkle in entertaining, personal, or community content that keeps them engaged during the off-market years. When year 7 arrives, they're still following you ‚Äî and they call you first.

## Organic vs. Paid Lead Conversion

Organic social media (Instagram/Facebook followers who know you) closes at approximately **9.5/10** ‚Äî because trust is already built before the call.

Paid leads close at approximately **1/40** ‚Äî because trust hasn't been established yet.

This is why Gogo invests heavily in organic content and organic list-building. The conversion economics are incomparable.

## The 78% Rule and 5-Minute Response

**78% of buyers and sellers go with the first agent who contacted them.** Speed of first contact = conversion. The formula: automations make you "first" even while you're sleeping.

**5-minute rule:** Respond to every new lead within 5 minutes of entering your CRM. The moment that window passes, probability of conversion drops sharply. Automations close this gap.

### The No-Scheduling Rule

> "The algorithm gods don't like [prescheduled posts]. Don't ask me why. I haven't figured out why but they just don't like it."

**Two hard rules:**
1. **Never use third-party scheduling tools** (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, etc.) ‚Äî views are dramatically lower than live posts.
2. **Never use Meta's own built-in scheduling** either ‚Äî same penalty applies.

**Always post in the moment, from your phone.** This is non-negotiable until the platforms change their stance.

### Price = Buyer Quality

What you post is who you attract ‚Äî at every price level.

> "If you post a $100,000 listing, what kind of buyers you going to find? You're going to find buyers on the market for $100,000. If you post a million-dollar listing ‚Äî people looking for a million-dollar property. Your income is in your control."

Post in the price range you want to work in. Post in your niche. Don't post a horse farm if you sell city condos ‚Äî you'll attract the wrong buyers and look off-brand.

## Video Content Strategy

Three recurring video formats Gogo prescribes for realtors ‚Äî each has a defined cadence, format, and lead capture mechanism.

### 1. Neighborhood Market Report Videos (Monthly)

> "When these words leave your lips, they're like, 'Ooh, she sounds so smart.' You become the expert automatically ‚Äî all you did is read the numbers off a piece of paper."

**The premise:** Nobody owns a neighborhood. Free market, free speech ‚Äî show up monthly in any neighborhood you want to dominate. Gogo was told a competitor "owned" Pine Creek. She ignored it, marketed there consistently, and broke in.

**Monthly process:**
1. **Pull the report** ‚Äî MLS data: active listings, days on market, price per sqft, expired listings. Then use ChatGPT: *"Tell me everything about [Neighborhood], [City, State] ‚Äî cool things, restaurants, school districts, community features."*
2. **Print and study** ‚Äî Circle what to mention, cross out what you don't understand. Hold the paper while shooting ‚Äî no need to memorize.
3. **Shoot on location** ‚Äî Go to a recognizable backdrop (neighborhood sign, entry gate, landmark). Bring a person to film you OR use a phone stand. Name yourself at the start; give a CTA at the end.
4. **Keyword CTA** ‚Äî End every video: *"Comment [KEYWORD] and my system will send you the listings and report for this neighborhood."*
5. **Squeeze page** ‚Äî Build one in KV Core/BoldTrail for that specific neighborhood (auto-updates with new listings). Set up ManyChat keyword to deliver it. On Facebook/YouTube: post the direct link. On Instagram: keyword only (no links in feed).

**Consistency is the product.** Month 1 you'll be nervous. Month 3 you'll have commissions from that neighborhood. 12 months in, you ARE the neighborhood expert.

### 2. Niche Property Feature Videos (Weekly)

Pick ONE niche (homes under $175K, waterfront, horse farms, new construction, etc.) and create a video featuring a property in that niche **once a week**. One video means nothing. Weekly = expertise.

**Price reinforces your buyer pool:** Feature $175K homes ‚Üí attract $175K buyers. Feature $3M homes ‚Üí attract $3M buyers. You choose your income level through what you post.

**Three shooting formats:**

|--------|--------|-------|
| **In-person** | Schedule showing; ask listing agent permission to video/feature | Most engaging; agent will say yes (free marketing for them) |
| **Screen record + voiceover** | iPhone: swipe down ‚Üí hold screen record ‚Üí enable mic ‚Üí start ‚Üí narrate while scrolling | Fast, no travel; property on screen + your voice |
| **Green screen** | Screen record WITHOUT voice ‚Üí Instagram Stories ‚Üí green screen filter ‚Üí import video ‚Üí shoot yourself in front of it | Your face on camera with property behind you |

**Listing agent pitch:** *"Even though I don't have a buyer right now, I'd love to tour and feature this property. I have [X] followers in the local market and my goal is to find a buyer for you. Interested?"* They always say yes.

**CTA formula:** Tell them exactly what to do ‚Äî "comment TOUR," "comment KEY," "DM me," "call me at [number]." Set up ManyChat to auto-deliver the link. Also write the CTA in the post description (some people read, some watch).

### ManyChat "Buyer" Keyword Flow ‚Äî How It Works

*(Explained by Sam, GoGet'Em tech team.)*

The "buyer" keyword automation has exactly **3 elements**:

**1. Trigger**
Two entry points:
- Someone comments the word **"buyer"** on any post ‚Üí flow starts
- Someone sends a DM containing **"buyer"** ‚Üí flow starts

**2. Condition ‚Äî Follow Gate**
Immediately checks whether the commenter/DM-sender follows the account:
- **Follows:** ‚Üí receives the message with the buyer link
- **Does not follow:** ‚Üí receives *"please follow to unlock the link"* message with a button. If they click the button without following, the condition re-checks and loops ‚Äî they stay blocked until they actually follow, then they receive the full message automatically.

This follow-gate ensures the buyer link is delivered only to people who follow you ‚Äî meaning every new lead also becomes a new follower.

**3. Message**
The final message contains the buyer website link. Critical rule:

> "Make it sound like you, make it sound like your tone, mannerism, or whatnot so the person interacting with your DMs will not be able to find out that you use the bot. It will still feel like they chatted you."

## Carousel Posts

Carousels are multi-slide posts (up to 10 slides) on Instagram and Facebook. Each slide contains information the viewer must click through to fully consume ‚Äî market data, educational steps, frameworks, lists. The format matters less than the depth of value packed inside.

### Why Carousels Beat Single-Image Posts

The old algorithm metric: follower count. The current metric: **time on profile + saves + shares + comments.**

A carousel forces engagement by design. A viewer who wants all 10 steps must click every slide. Eight minutes on one post sends the strongest signal an account can produce:

> "Back in the day it mattered how many followers you have. Today it matters how much time people spend on your profile, how many times they comment, how many times they share your post, how many times they save your post."

**The save + share flywheel:**
- Carousels are informational ‚Üí people save them to refer back
- Saves + shares ‚Üí algorithm flags the post as high-quality
- Algorithm pushes to non-followers ‚Üí free reach to strangers

**Real example ‚Äî Gogo's pinned "16 Things I Track" carousel:**

| Metric | Number |
|--------|--------|
| Views | 9,000 |
| Likes | 133 |
| Comments | 119 |
| Saves | 48 |
| Shares | 11 |
| Non-follower reach | **17.7%** (~1,600 strangers) |

Instagram pushed the post to strangers because followers saved, shared, and spent extended time on it.

### Slide Structure
1. **Slide 1 ‚Äî Hook:** Must stop the scroll. If this slide doesn't compel a click, nothing else matters.
2. **Slides 2‚Äì9 ‚Äî Content:** Dense, valuable, educational. Each slide must earn the next click.
3. **Last slide (or earlier) ‚Äî CTA:** What do you want them to do? Event invite, free resource, DM, booking link.

*CTA placement:* If unsure they'll reach the end, put the CTA mid-sequence ‚Äî don't risk losing it at slide 10.

### Content Ideas for Real Estate Carousels
- **Monthly market report:** Avg days on market, avg price, local news for your farm area. Title: *"Top 10 most random facts about [County] real estate market."*
- **Buyer education:** "10 steps to buying your first home"
- **Seller education:** "10 steps to selling your home for top dollar"
- **Systems / frameworks:** Multi-step processes, checklists (e.g., Gogo's 16 trackers)
- **Local spotlights:** New businesses, school district news, community events

**Non-negotiable:** Always include yourself in the photos. *"We are selling you and your services."* You at the listing sign, at closing, at the open house.

### Creation
- **Tool:** Canva ‚Äî purpose-built for multi-slide branded graphics
- **Gogo's workflow:** She generates the idea ‚Üí Ellen (Team Gogo VA) builds in Canva
- **Starting cadence:** One carousel per month ‚Üí scale to one per week

### ChatGPT Carousel Content Workflow

Use ChatGPT to generate slide content ‚Äî most effective inside the same session where you've already entered market data or neighborhood stats.

1. Pull market statistics or neighborhood data into ChatGPT
2. Prompt: *"Create a carousel post based on the stats above"*
3. ChatGPT outputs a slide-by-slide outline ‚Äî one piece of content per slide, formatted and ready to use
4. Take the outline into Canva ‚Üí copy/paste content per slide into your branded template
5. Ellen handles Canva design execution (companion video)

**Key advantage:** The research and the content are in one continuous session. No re-entry, no switching tools mid-thought.

**Extended capability:** ChatGPT can also propose the visual carousel layout, not just the written content ‚Äî useful if you want AI to draft the design structure as well.

## Hashtag Strategy

> **Platform update:** Instagram reduced the maximum hashtag count from **30 ‚Üí 5**. All hashtag strategy below is calibrated to this new limit. Be intentional ‚Äî every hashtag slot counts.

**Hashtags = keywords.** Someone searching a hashtag finds every post tagged with it. Your hashtags must match the audience you're speaking to ‚Äî never mix buyer/seller hashtags with agent attraction hashtags in the same post.

**Always geo-tag** the location in every post. This feeds Instagram's local discovery algorithm.

**Stay consistent** ‚Äî always fish in the same pond. The algorithm rewards niche consistency.

### Agent Attraction Hashtag Set (5 max)

| # | Hashtag | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | #[YourOrgName] (e.g. #TeamGoGo) | Makes all your agent attraction posts searchable under your org |

| 3 | #Realtor | Broad agent audience |
| 4 | #Agent | Alternate agent search term |
| 5 | Your market / niche specific | Local or specialty term |

### Buyer/Seller Location Hashtag Set (5 max)

> "The more specific you are, the smaller is the audience, and the higher you're going to show up."

Your 5 buyer/seller hashtags should be tied directly to your 3 core ZIP codes (see [[Personal Branding#Geographic Targeting ‚Äî 3 Core ZIP Codes]]). With only 5 slots, you'll focus on one primary area per post ‚Äî the area the post is actually about.

**Gogo's 5-hashtag set for Brighton, Michigan:**

| # | Hashtag | Why |
|---|---------|-----|
| 1 | `#BrightonMichigan` | City + state ‚Äî essential when city name is not unique (Brighton, England also exists) |
| 2 | `#BrightonMichiganHomesForSale` | Exact search phrase potential buyers use |
| 3 | `#gogorealestate` | Personal brand ‚Äî on every single post, always |
| 4 | `#LivingstonCounty` | County-level reach covering the broader area |
| 5 | `#LivingstonCountyRealEstate` | County + intent keyword |

**Rules:**
- **Your brand hashtag goes on every post without exception.** If anyone hears about you ‚Äî from a stage, a podcast, a friend ‚Äî and searches your brand, your entire post history surfaces. Non-negotiable.
- **Avoid generic hashtags** (`#homeforsale`, `#realtor`): saturated by agents nationwide. Your post gets buried. Specificity = higher placement in a smaller, more relevant audience.
- **Location-only rule:** Use location hashtags only when the post is actually tied to that location. The Instagram/Facebook algorithm scans images ‚Äî a palm tree in a post tagged `#BrightonMichigan` will suppress the post because there are no palm trees in Brighton, Michigan. Don't lie to the algorithm.
- **Save in your Brand Tracker / brand kit.** When you change markets or add a ZIP code, update the brand kit at the same time so your hashtag set stays current.
- With 30 hashtags available (old limit), Gogo covered Brighton + Howell + Livingston County. At 5, she drops Howell and focuses on the higher-commission area. Intentionality is the strategy.

## Client Success Story Framework

> "I had a five-star experience working with you. I hope you had the same. Would you please take five minutes out of your time and leave me a review on these three platforms, because this is how I get to feed my family next month, too?"

## Email Structure Principles

*From Carol (Team Gogo email strategist), GSMC 2026 copywriting month.*

### Inbox = Intimate Space
On social media, people scroll fast and fight the algorithm. In email, they're paying closer attention. This changes how you should write:
- Be personal
- Be simpler (readers won't scroll through walls of text)
- Give them one clear action
- Never apologize for your CTA ‚Äî trust your offer

### The Two Email Formats

| Format | Structure | CTAs | Best For |
|--------|-----------|------|----------|
| **Long storytelling** | Personal story ‚Üí problem ‚Üí solution ‚Üí what's in it for them ‚Üí CTA | 1 (at end) | Trust-building, launches, high-investment offers |
| **Short direct** | Authority statement ‚Üí outcome ‚Üí invitation | 3 (distributed) | Events, simple offers, challenge sign-ups |

**Recent finding:** Distributing 3 CTAs in shorter, more direct emails increases click rate vs. a single CTA at the end of a long email. Team Gogo has been testing and shifting toward this approach.

### REMLO Email Example (Long Format)
- Opens with personal story: Gogo gave a lender $9M of buyers and received nothing back.
- Pain is relatable; many agents have felt used by lenders.
- Numbers anchor authority ($9M is specific and surprising).
- REMLO is introduced as *the solution she found* ‚Äî not a pitch.
- Ends with single red button CTA.

### Social Media Challenge Email Example (Short Format)
- No storytelling ‚Äî opens with positioning statement: *"I never cared about likes on social media. I only ever cared about what they turn into."*
- Speaks directly to the reader's desired outcome (revenue, not vanity metrics).
- Short paragraphs, punchy sentences, three CTAs spread throughout.

### Principles for Every Email
1. **Storytelling + numbers** = trust + intrigue. Story alone moves them; numbers make them believe.
2. **State what's in it for them** ‚Äî clearly, early, simply. Don't bury the benefit.
3. **Confident CTA** ‚Äî don't hedge, don't apologize. You're offering a solution, not imposing.
4. **Simple subject lines** ‚Äî data shows simple subjects drive more opens and clicks.
5. **Serve, don't sell** ‚Äî readers can feel desperation in copy. Write from *"I have a solution to your problem"* not *"please buy my thing."*

> "When you're worried about serving people, your own mindset is going to change and it's going to reflect in your copy as well."

## Email Subject Line Tactics

*From Carol (Team Gogo email strategist), GSMC 2026 copywriting month.*

**Mobile-first rule:** Most opens happen on a phone. Subject lines get cut off. Keep the core message in the **first 4‚Äì6 words** so the meaning survives truncation. Even if they can't see the full line, they should understand enough to want to open.

**Core goal:** Make them feel something ‚Äî curiosity, FOMO, opportunity, intrigue. Ask: *"How do I want this person to feel when they read this subject line?"*

### Subject Line Strategies

| Strategy | Example | Why it works |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Casual / personal** | "Come hang out with me tomorrow night" | Feels like a friend texting. Inspires curiosity. |
| **Short + direct** | "Free training: Create your AI twin" | Gets the point in 5 words. Zero ambiguity. |
| **Bold statement / rhetorical** | "What actually makes money on Instagram?" | Challenges assumptions. Reader needs the answer. |
| **Friendly tip** | "Just a little tip on sales for you" | Low pressure, collegial. High trust signal. |
| **Ironic / pattern interrupt** | "Shortest email ever" / "Do not open this email" | Reverse psychology. The irony compels the open. |
| **Income highlight** | "How dem agents add 1,600 plus per deal" | Specific number + direct relevance to agents. |
| **Open loop** | "This is how‚Ä¶" / "This is why‚Ä¶" | Implies the answer is inside. Reader wants to close the loop. |

**Emojis** in subject lines read as casual, friendly, and human. Use 1‚Äì2 selectively. They help the email stand out in a sea of corporate text.

- *"Give me a subject line with 4‚Äì6 words maximum that inspires curiosity."*
- *"Write a subject line that highlights an income opportunity for real estate agents."*

## Website vs. Funnel

- **Website** ‚Äî digital home base. Multi-purpose. Builds credibility for multiple audiences. More complex, longer to build, higher cost. Example: gogobethke.com.
- **Funnel/Landing page** ‚Äî single-purpose conversion path. One specific visitor ‚Üí one specific action. Simpler, faster to build, cheaper. "Kind of boring ‚Äî because they only serve that one purpose."

Use websites for brand establishment. Use funnels for specific campaigns, lead magnets, and product launches. Don't try to make a website do what a funnel should do and vice versa.

> "On top is your brand. In the middle is the meat. In the bottom is the call to action."

- **Middle (Meat):** Videos, deeper info, testimonials, proof. For skeptical visitors who need more before committing.
- **Bottom:** CTA button again ‚Äî calendar link or contact form. Closes the loop.

### The 3-Competitor Study Method

**Step 1:** Write down 3 names ‚Äî agents in your market who are clearly winning on social media.

**Step 2:** This week, go deep on each one:
- Scroll back to their earliest posts (Facebook, Instagram, website)

- Page 1: Competitor A ‚Äî name at top, bullet observations below

- Page 3: Competitor C

**Two outcomes from this exercise:**

## The Social Media Machine ‚Äî One-to-Many Funnel

*Source: [[The Fundamentals - Social Media Machine and Branding (Gogo Bethke)]]*

## Copywriting ‚Äî The COPYWRITING Framework

*Source: [[Master Your Copywriting and AI Brand Voice (Gogo Bethke)]]*

Copywriting is "the way we communicate" ‚Äî its purpose is to **speak the language of your ideal client** so the right people find you. Do the ideal-client and ideal-agent homework first: *"You can't write to someone you haven't defined."*

> "When we get to speak the language of our ideal client, we find our ideal client. And when you find your ideal client, they're ideal for a reason because they're just like you."

### Email Structure ‚Äî Hook ‚Üí Problem ‚Üí Story ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí CTA
*(Fernie's framework.)* Agents fail on **messaging, not consistency.** Structure every email in five beats:
1. **Hook** ‚Äî *"The hook is everything. If they don't open it, nothing else matters."*
2. **Problem** ‚Äî name the pain
3. **Story** ‚Äî make it human and relatable
4. **Solution** ‚Äî your answer
5. **CTA** ‚Äî one single, explicit call to action (DM, call, or text + number). One CTA, not five options, or people do nothing.

**Email is a shortcut, not a replacement.** You still must know what to say and who you're talking to; ChatGPT then drafts in seconds. *"ChatGPT is your shortcut, not your replacement."*

Pre-batch emails on a weekly theme calendar so you never stare at a blank screen. Gogo's team sends one email/day:

### Custom-GPT Brand Voice
[[Kristy Waker]] built a shareable custom GPT ‚Äî **"My AI Brand Voice"** ‚Äî on Gogo's account. Paste 3‚Äì5 pieces of your own writing (emails, social posts, Zoom transcripts) via "Fast-Track Voice Setup," or use "Guided Interview" (~20 questions) if you have no content. It outputs a full brand-voice profile ‚Üí paste into ChatGPT ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí **Personalization ‚Üí Custom Instructions**, so every future chat writes in your voice. (See [[Personal Branding#My AI Brand Voice ‚Äî Custom GPT Setup]].)

Gogo works AI by **voice, not typing**: *"I hit the microphone, I talk to it like it's my best friend."* Tell it the full story, then say "act like the best social media marketer on the face of the earth."

### Specificity ‚Äî Convince With Numbers
A closing post improves dramatically once you add real numbers (multiple offers, cash buyer, next-door lot, rental strategy). Investors want the bottom line, not fluff. *"This is how I convince someone working with me ‚Äî with numbers, not with words."*

### AI-vs-Human Writing Tells (strip these out)
- **Em dashes** (the "AI signature")
- **Buzzwords**: leverage, optimize, "unlock your potential," "boost revenue"
- Missing space before the first word
- Uniform paragraph lengths
- "In today's fast-paced world," and "in conclusion"

**Humans** mix sentence lengths, use contractions, add opinions/stories/local references. Read it aloud and break where you run out of air. **Maintain your GPT over time** ‚Äî revisit custom instructions every 1‚Äì2 years (Gogo's still listed old kid ages and a stale bio); correct it live ("no, I'd never say that") so it keeps learning.

**The 3-step AI workflow:** (1) give context, audience, tone, goal ‚Üí (2) generate a rough draft ‚Üí (3) human-edit for readability, voice, specificity, and CTA.
