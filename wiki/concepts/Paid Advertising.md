# Paid Advertising

Google Ads strategy for realtors, as taught by [[Ashley Brock]] inside [[Gogo Bethke]]'s community. The core insight: Google is intent-based advertising — you're not interrupting people, you're meeting them mid-search.

## Intent: Why Google Is Different

On social media, users are browsing passively — they don't know what they want yet. On Google, someone has already decided they want something and is actively searching for it. They just need to find you.

> "On Google, someone is searching for what you do and who you are. They just simply need to find you."

This makes Google leads higher intent than most social media leads, especially for high-consideration purchases like real estate.

## Two Types of Google Inventory

### 1. Local Service Ads (LSA)
- **You pay per lead** (not per click)
- Requires uploading your license, insurance, and sometimes a background check
- Ranked primarily by review volume and star rating — agents with more 5-star reviews appear higher
- Can dispute low-quality leads and get the cost refunded
- Takes longer to gain momentum (more competition, verification process) but produces high-quality leads
- Tip: automate Google review collection — volume compounds over time

### 2. Google Search Ads
- **You pay per click** — $3–$30/click for realtors depending on market competitiveness
- Nearly immediate — campaign can start spending the day it's set up
- Triggered by keywords you choose; text-based ads (no video required)
- More control over targeting and messaging

## Budget

Any budget can work, but the algorithm needs data to optimize. More spend = faster learning. Ashley recommends starting at $10–$15/day and spending at your "discomfort level."

> "The most successful entrepreneurs have the highest tolerance for pain. Whatever makes you most uncomfortable — that's where I want you to start."

## Campaign Structure

```
Campaign
├── Budget, goal, and location targeting
└── Ad Group 1 (keyword theme: "near me" searches)
    └── Ad (text copy)
└── Ad Group 2 (keyword theme: brand/name searches)
    └── Ad
└── Ad Group 3 (keyword theme: competitor searches)
    └── Ad
```

**Three keyword groups for realtors:**
1. **Brand** — people searching your name ("Ashley Brock realtor")
2. **Category** — people searching what you do ("realtor near me," "best real estate agent Dallas")
3. **Competitor** — people searching for a specific competitor or brokerage ("best Keller Williams agent Dallas")

Location targeting is set at the campaign level. You can run one campaign targeting a 25-mile local radius (for "near me" searches) and a separate campaign targeting the whole US (for people searching for agents in your specific town from out of state).

## Landing Page Strategy

Do not send Google ad traffic to a property search page. Send them to a simple **you page** — a 3-page site (home, about, contact) that answers: who are you, why should they work with you, and how do they reach you.

**The conversion flow:**
1. User clicks ad → lands on your page
2. User fills out contact form
3. User lands on a unique thank-you page URL (e.g., `/thank-you`)
4. Google reads the thank-you page visit as a conversion signal
5. Algorithm learns to find more people like this user

After form submission: respond immediately via email or text, then nurture with 2–3 emails on why working with you is the best strategy.

## Quality Score: The Cost Multiplier

Quality Score (1–10) determines how much you pay per click. **Higher score = lower cost per click.**

| Factor | What it measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| Landing page experience | How relevant is your page to the keyword they searched? |
| Ad relevance | Does your ad copy match the search term? |
| Expected CTR | How likely are people to click your ad based on its assets and format? |

**Why it matters:**
- $50/day budget at $10 CPC = 5 clicks/day
- $50/day budget at $5 CPC = 10 clicks/day
- Same spend, double the traffic — just by improving quality score

Add assets to your ad (phone number, images, descriptions) and make sure your landing page keywords match the search terms you're targeting.

## Google Business Profile (GBP)

Non-negotiable for any realtor running Google ads. Reviews pull directly into your LSA listing and search ads. Set up at business.google.com.

Automate review collection — agents with 1,000+ reviews rank significantly higher in LSA results and are perceived as more trustworthy by searchers.

## The Find + Remind Strategy

Getting someone to your website once is not enough. **80% of buyers need 5+ touchpoints before they commit.**

- **Find:** Google ads get people to your site
- **Remind:** Facebook and Instagram retargeting ads bring them back

Install both the Google pixel and Facebook pixel on your site. This captures every visitor's data — even those who don't fill out the form — so you can retarget them across platforms.

Think of it like dating: a Google ad is the first date. You don't propose on the first date. You keep showing up.
