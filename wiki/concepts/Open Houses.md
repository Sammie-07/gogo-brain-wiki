# Open Houses

[[Gogo Bethke]]'s complete open house system — the bridge between social media's broad reach and face-to-face conversion. Her personal highest-leverage lead source during her active selling years (40–50 transactions/year at peak).

> "If I could just get in front of them — when I am face to face with someone in person, I win them over pretty much every time."

## The Core Logic

[[Social Media and Email Marketing]] casts a wide net: you reach everyone who follows you. The open house narrows that to people actively on the market right now and puts them in front of you in person. The two work together — social drives traffic to the open house; the open house converts strangers into clients.

## Phase 1: Pre-Event Marketing

**Success is determined by what you do before the doors open.**

### MLS and Portals
- List the open house in MLS **no later than Thursday**
- Zillow, Realtor.com, Trulia take 2–3 days to sync — missing Thursday means missing the weekend entirely
- If holding someone else's listing, coordinate with the listing agent to get it updated

### Social Media Blast
- Post on every platform: LinkedIn, Facebook (feed + stories), Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
- Email your full buyer database: "I'm holding an open house at [address] this weekend — I'd love to see you"
- The best marketer wins. Cast the net as wide as possible on every platform.

### Door Knocking (20–50 Homes)
- Go to 20–50 homes around the property — all directions
- Bring a lender (can pre-approve on the spot) and/or another agent (share leads + safety)
- **Script:** "Your neighbor is selling. Do you have a best friend, a family member, someone you'd want living next door? This is your chance to control who moves in. Don't let me bring a random — tell your best friend."
- Invite nosy neighbors: they *will* show up to see how it's decorated and what it sold for

### Signs
- **40 directional signs** placed every 10 feet leading into and through the neighborhood
- **Helium balloons** tied to signs — visible and moving in the wind, impossible to miss
- **Two 7-foot standing banner signs** with your full photo, name, phone number, and brokerage logo at the driveway — evergreen, reusable across all open houses

### Printed Materials
- Color flyers
- MLS listing ticket (your marketing sheet with all details)
- Seller disclosures + lead-based paint disclosures

### Study the Listing
Know everything: roof age, HVAC, septic vs. city, well vs. city water, water softener/reverse osmosis, what conveys (appliances, fixtures, pool table). If a buyer asks and you don't know, you've lost expert status.

> "Know that listing inside out, upside down, in three other languages, swimming backwards."

If you don't know something, call the listing agent or the seller before the open house.

### Seller Prep
Tell sellers in advance:
- Home must be **clean and pristine** — no unmade beds, dishes, kids' shoes, dog home
- **Hide all valuables:** iPads, medications, collectibles, anything pocketable — not your responsibility if stolen, but prep them ahead of time so it's their responsibility if they don't comply
- **Remove personal photos** from walls — especially critical if a single woman lives alone (prevents targeting and safety risk from strangers learning occupant details)
- Consider adding men's clothing to the master closet if only women's clothing is present (prevents lowball signals and protects seller privacy)

### Interior Ambiance
- Arrive well before doors open
- Turn on **all lights** and **heat** — a cold, dim house does not feel like a home
- **Bake cookies** (pick up dough from grocery store, ask to use the oven) — or have sellers cook bacon before they leave. Smell is powerful.

## Phase 2: At the Door — Welcome Protocol

**Stand at the front door.** Never sit on the couch looking at your phone. You are the host.

- Greet every visitor personally — big smile, introduce yourself, welcome them to the property
- Address children immediately ("How adorable! There are cookies in the kitchen!")
- Target service level: **Louis Vuitton** — every visitor feels attended to

### Sign-In System
- Use an iPad with **KVCore** or **Brivity** open house app — auto-populates your CRM, no manual data entry
- Entry script: *"Please sign in for seller security."* Frame it as seller-required, not agent-required
- If they refuse to sign in → they do not tour. "You're welcome to come back with your realtor for a private showing."
- Know who is in the home at all times

### The Business Card Trick
Never hand out business cards at open houses.

> "I lie to your face and I say, 'Oh I'm sorry, I just ran out. Let me text you super quick so you have my information.'"

By "running out," you get their number — you control the follow-up. Giving a card means waiting and hoping they call. Getting their number means you act.

## Phase 3: Touring and Engagement

- **Don't hover** — give visitors freedom to explore
- As you hand them the flyer, give one teaser: "Wait until you see the kitchen" or "The backyard is going to surprise you" — gives them something to look forward to
- If guiding them: call out key features as you walk ("Paint is all new, roof is brand new")
- When they return to the main living area: **"How does this compare to other homes you've seen today? Are you looking for yourself or scouting for a friend?"**
- Treat every visitor like a new best friend — ask about their kids, their accent, their style. People buy from people they like.

## Phase 4: Post-Event Follow-Up

### Security Sweep (Before Leaving)
- Check and **physically lock every window on the main floor** — burglars will unlock windows during the open house to return later
- Lock all exterior doors (confirm locked, not just closed)
- Turn off all lights and gas (if oven was used for cookies)
- Call the seller: confirm home is secure, give a traffic update ("19 couples came through, two very interested, I'm following up now")

### Immediate Follow-Up (Before Leaving the Driveway)
Send a **selfie-style video text** to every attendee, using their first name from the sign-in sheet:

*"Hey Jessica — thank you so much for coming through today. I just wanted to let you know we had 19 couples walk through the property. Two of them seem very, very interested and I think they're going to make an offer by end of day. If you want this home, tonight is your chance — you need to make an offer. If you have a realtor, reach out to them now. If you don't have one, I'd love to represent you. Just respond to this message."*

Key elements:
- Use their **first name**
- Share **traffic numbers** (social proof + urgency)
- Give a **clear call to action** (offer tonight)
- Offer to **represent them** if unrepresented

> Follow up with *everyone* — not just unrepresented buyers. Represented buyers may fire their agent later.

### Follow-Up Week
- All leads into CRM; set up automated email follow-up sequence
- Follow up **multiple times** in the week after
- Update them on property status: went under contract → "I have a similar listing coming up..."
- Goal: be the agent they call when they're ready, whether that's now or in 6 months

## The Philosophy

> "You have to outwork everyone. You have to outperform everyone. You have to out-expert everyone. You have to out-market everyone. You have to out-follow-up everyone."

> "If you want what the top 1% has, you have to do what 99% is not willing to do."

## The Two-Buyer Open-House System (Circle coaching)

*Source: [[The Circle Coaching — Listings-First, the Open-House Two-Buyer System and the Value Ladder (de-identified)]]. Private [[The Circle|Circle]] coaching, de-identified — Gogo's generalizable teachings only.*

The open house is the engine that turns one listing into **two buyers and three commissions** (seller side + the buyer of that home + a buyer for a comparable nearby). It only works if the opens are guaranteed and the follow-up is relentless.

**Write weekend opens into the listing agreement.** Held **every Saturday and Sunday until sold** — not optional, not weather-dependent. Baking it into the agreement is what makes the two-buyer math dependable.

**Personalized ~30-second driveway video to every attendee, by name.** Recorded in the driveway before you leave — one individual video per attendee/couple, **never a blast.** Same rhythm every time:
1. **Greet by name** (pulled from the sign-in sheet)
2. **Thank them** for coming
3. **Give the update** — traffic count, level of interest
4. "If you want to offer, **tell your realtor**"
5. "If you don't have one, **I'd love to represent you**"
6. "If this isn't the home, **I'd love to show you others** in the same area and price range"

That last line is what harvests the *second* buyer — the person already shopping that area and price who won't buy this house but will buy a comparable one.

**Monday 9:00 a.m. review.** A standing team meeting to review the weekend: which homes were held, traffic counts, and whether **every** attendee was followed up. "Buyers are liars" — follow up with all of them regardless of what they claimed at the door.

**Delegate and keep the split.** Hand open houses to a **licensed team member** and still earn the split (~50%) without giving up your weekends and family time — "50–60% of *something* beats burnout."
