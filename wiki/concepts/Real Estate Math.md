# Real Estate Math

[[Gogo Bethke]]'s framework for making data-driven decisions in real estate — from choosing your brokerage to reverse-engineering your income goal into a monthly transaction target.

## PPP: Productivity Per Person

**Formula:** Total brokerage transactions ÷ total agent headcount = PPP

Why it matters: you are the average of the people you surround yourself with. A brokerage's PPP is the most honest measure of the production environment you're entering.

| Benchmark | PPP |
|-----------|-----|
| National average (many brokerages) | ~2 transactions/agent/year |
| [[#teamgogo]] (as of early 2026) | 6.15 transactions/agent/year |

> "Team Go agents outproduce the other agents in this nation by about 300%."

NAR stat (2025): **71.1% of licensed US realtors sold zero homes in 2025.** This drags down brokerage averages and is why PPP varies so wildly.

## The "Average of 5 Friends" Exercise

Add up the incomes of the five people you spend the most time with. Divide by five. Gogo's claim: your income is close to that number.

> "I hate to say I guarantee it, but I guarantee it."

Implication: choosing your brokerage, team, and peer group is one of the highest-leverage decisions you can make as an agent.

## Commission Reverse-Engineering (eXp Realty Example)

Gogo walks through this live with agents to show that income goals are achievable with far fewer transactions than people assume.

**Given:** $120,000 income goal, $430k average sale price, 3% commission, at [[eXp Realty]]

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Average gross commission | $430,000 × 3% | $12,900 |
| eXp split (pre-cap) | $12,900 × 20% | $2,580 to company |
| Agent keeps (pre-cap) | $12,900 − $2,580 | $10,320 |
| Transactions to cap | $16,000 cap ÷ $2,580 | ~6 transactions |
| Pre-cap earnings | $10,320 × 6 | $61,920 |
| Post-cap commission (after $250 fee) | $12,900 − $250 | $12,650 |
| Remaining income needed | $120,000 − $61,920 | $58,080 |
| Post-cap transactions needed | $58,080 ÷ $12,650 | ~4.6 → round up to 5 |
| **Total transactions to hit $120k** | 6 + 5 | **~11 transactions** |

**Conclusion:** One transaction per month. Take December off and celebrate.

## The Open House Math

For an agent whose strength is being in front of people (Gogo's own lane), open houses are the math-optimized lead source:
- 4 weekends per month = 8 open house opportunities (Sat + Sun)
- Conversion is a function of traffic × closing rate
- Even without your own listings, call producing listing agents and ask to hold theirs open

## Why Brokerage Choice Is a Production Decision

> "That's why it matters where you hang your license. That's why it matters where you learn. That's why it matters where you get referrals from. That's why it matters where you do business."

The math argument: being surrounded by agents with a PPP of 6+ vs. 2 is a direct input to your own production, referral flow, and learning environment.

## Assumable Mortgage Math (AssumeList)

*Source: [[Assumable Mortgages and AssumeList (Nora Simpson and Mike Lorino)]]*

The numbers behind assuming a seller's low-rate loan — the affordability lever [[AssumeList]] surfaces for [[#teamgogo]] in a high-rate market.

**Which loans qualify.** Only **FHA, VA, and USDA** loans are assumable — never conventional. USDA adds an income test: the assuming buyer's income must be **≤115% of local median household income**.

**The equity-gap formula.** The buyer assumes only the *remaining balance*, not the sale price — so they must cover the gap created by appreciation and paydown. In practice that's **~10% down**, with the balance above that financed by a **second mortgage**:

```
Purchase price − remaining assumable balance = equity gap
Equity gap  ≈  ~10% cash down  +  second mortgage (the rest)
```

**Monthly savings.** Assuming a sub-3% loan instead of originating at ~6.25% saves roughly **$500–$1,000/month today** (down from $2,000–$3,000 a few years ago, when the rate spread was wider). Some buyers pursue an assumption to save as little as ~$100/month.

**The display threshold.** AssumeList only surfaces homes whose assumable loan sits **below 5%** — the line where the assumption is worth the added second-mortgage complexity versus a fresh loan. See [[Assumable Mortgages]] for the full mechanics and fee structure.
