# Goal Setting

[[Gogo Bethke]]'s system for setting and achieving goals — combining brutal self-honesty with a spiritual belief in the law of attraction, practical reverse engineering, and behavioral accountability through the CPR framework.

## Core Principle: Big, Obnoxious, Scary Goals

> "If your goals are not scaring you shitless, you didn't set big obnoxious goals. You settled."

Most people set lower goals to protect themselves from disappointment. Gogo rejects this entirely. If you don't feel a shiver when you think about your goal, you haven't set it high enough.

## Step 1: Own Where You Are (Current GPS Location)

Before setting a destination, you must be honest about where you are starting. Gogo calls this your "current GPS location." Most people avoid this because it hurts to admit.

> "If you don't like your results, own up to it and let's change it."

Write it down. Later, go to the bathroom, look yourself in the eye, and read it out loud.

## Step 2: Set the Destination — No Plan B

Your internal GPS cannot take you to two places at once. The moment you create a fallback, you confuse the system.

> "When you create yourself a plan B, when you allow yourself to accept something less, you confuse your GPS and you might get nothing."

Rule: **No plan B.** One destination. One commitment. No half-destination like "I want $250k, but I'll be happy with $100k." That's like telling GPS "take me to San Francisco, but Utah is fine."

> "What I don't change, I accept."

## Step 3: Define the Goal in Detail

- Specific income / career level / lifestyle target
- Don't settle for "close enough" — close enough *is* settling
- Set a specific deadline (December 31st of the year)

> "If you say 'I want to make more money' — a dollar is money, and you'll make it when you're 93."

The universe needs to know *what* and *by when*. Without a deadline, the universe has the rest of your life to deliver it.

## Step 4: Visualize Until You Believe It

> "If I cannot imagine it, I cannot have it."

If you feel a twitch of "there's no way," you haven't done the mental work yet. Gogo's practice: sit quietly (meditate), close your eyes, and visualize the specific goal — the stage, the car, the cash in the bank — until you can see it clearly without the "no way" feeling. Do not get up until you can see it.

The belief is foundational: the universe takes your thoughts as frequencies. If you believe it's possible, it has to happen.

## Step 5: Know Your Strengths and Stay in Your Lane

Goals must be set within your actual skills. No matter how hard you train, you cannot be Michael Jordan if God didn't give you those gifts.

> "It doesn't matter how fast you run towards east — you will never see a sunset."

Exercise: Text 5–10 people you trust, without warning, and ask: *"What am I good at? What do I suck at? Give me five for each."* This reveals your God-given lane because natural talents often go unnoticed by their owners.

## The CPR Framework

A commitment accountability structure. Used any time Gogo sets a goal for herself or coaches others.

| Letter | Stands For | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **C** | Commitment | The specific goal, with a deadline (e.g., "40 transactions by Dec 31, 2026") |
| **P** | Penalty | Something genuinely painful if you fail — must hurt enough that failure is unacceptable |
| **R** | Reward | Something you've wanted but couldn't normally afford — booked/purchased when goal is hit |

Rules:
- Penalty and reward must be set *high* — a trivial penalty ("no coffee for a day") will not motivate
- Replace "if" with "when" — "when I hit 40 deals" not "if I hit 40 deals"
- Say it out loud and write it down; telling others creates social accountability
- Stick it somewhere visible

Example (live audience): Commitment = 40 transactions. Penalty = no physical affection with husband for all of January. Reward = trip to Japan, booked by end of January after closing deal 40.

## Step 6: Reverse Engineer the Goal

Break the annual number down to smaller, non-overwhelming pieces:
- Annual → Quarterly → Monthly → Weekly → Daily → Hourly

The point: at the hourly level, the task should feel almost trivially small. 1% change per day = 365% change by year end.

> "Most people overestimate what they can do in one year and underestimate what they can do in ten."

Use ChatGPT prompts (Gogo sells a prompt package) to automate the reverse engineering, create a marketing plan, identify your ideal client, and map daily tasks.

## Step 7: Track, Adjust, and Celebrate

- Track actions and results daily, weekly, monthly
- Adjust strategy when something isn't working — don't abandon the goal, change the route
- Celebrate wins with dangling carrots (Gucci belt, spa day, hotel) — the reward psychology keeps you working
- Be a person of your word — how you do anything is how you do everything

> "Goals are only as good as your follow through."
