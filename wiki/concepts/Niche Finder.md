# Niche Finder

Gogo's AI tool that runs a short questionnaire through her "second brain" to output a complete, personalized niche + 90-day marketing plan.

## Core Idea

"When you're selling to everyone, you're selling to no one." The Niche Finder helps an agent name and own a specific niche, then hands them an execution plan. It is powered by [[Gogo Bethke]]'s **AI "second brain"** — ~15 years of her trainings, podcasts, and content embedded and linked to Claude — so the output reflects her actual marketing philosophy. (Originated in the GoGet'Em 2026 social media challenge.)

## How It Works

- A **10-question** questionnaire, run through Gogo's brain in her cloud. **$24 per report** (a free generic prompt is also given for agents to run in their own Claude/ChatGPT, but it won't draw on Gogo's data).
- Run it **separately per avatar** — buyer-side ideal client, listing-side ideal client, and ideal agent recruit/downline — then blend (e.g., Mon/Wed sellers, Tue/Thu buyers, Fri agents).

## What Each Report Delivers

1. Three **Instagram bio** options (<150 chars; bio must name the niche in the first 5 words — "if it does not, you are invisible").
2. **Top 8 marketing ideas** filtered to the agent's life/niche (use only 2–3 at a time to avoid burnout/analysis paralysis).
3. **SEO strategy** — primary keyword + 5 long-tail keywords + title tag + meta description.
4. A **12-week blog content calendar** (articles 800–1500 words) + internal linking plan.
5. A day-by-day **90-day marketing plan** (month 1 foundation, month 2 momentum, month 3 scale).

## The "Obvious Choice" Training Behind the Report

The live niche-training *How to Become the Obvious Choice in Your Market* is the coaching that sits behind the $24.99 report — the first-ever public run of Gogo's "second brain," built over months from every YouTube video, presentation, and post she has ever made ("15 years of brain dump").

- **Be the only, not the best.** "Stop trying to be the best realtor for everyone. Be the only realtor for first responders buying new build in Martin County." Narrowing makes you the obvious choice for a specific person.
- **Your niche comes from your real life.** The application asks about kids, marriage, pets, languages, past jobs, hobbies, and personal story ("have diarrhea of the personality mouth"). Gogo works with foreigners (she is one) and type-1-diabetic families (her son has it) — authentic, lived-truth niches convert.
- **Niches evolve as you evolve — pivot, don't restart.** The *shoes-at-5-vs-55* metaphor: "Imagine being 5 years old putting on your shoes to go for a run, and being 55 trying to put on the same shoes. It would hurt... same DNA, same fingerprint, same social security number — it is not the same person." Gogo's niche 15 years ago was young stay-at-home moms wanting a 3/2 with a yard; today it would be empty-nesters downsizing to the lake. Feeling "burnt out" usually means you've outgrown a phase — rerun the report.

### Report structure (full)
1. **Instagram bio** — name the niche in the first ~5 words, under 150 chars, reads smoothly aloud, no em-dashes/bullets; drop brokerage name and "years of experience" (clients don't care which brand you're under). Pick ONE of three options and own it.
2. **Top 8 marketing ideas** — filtered from the GoGet'Em marketing framework and tied to the agent's actual life/niche. Don't start all eight — pick 2–3 that excite you, run the "how to start" first step this week, add more once they're on autopilot.
3. **SEO = exact keywords in exact order.** Google "spiders" crawl every site; if the page doesn't literally say "best realtor in Martin County for new construction," you won't rank. Put the primary keyword + longtails naturally on every page ("massage" it in, don't stuff). **Build your OWN website in Claude** (own yourname.com, one page per niche), not a BoldTrail/[[Lofty]]/KVCore template.
4. **12-week blog calendar** — one 800–1,500-word niche-keyworded article per week; repurpose each into an Instagram carousel, a reel, and a LinkedIn post via Claude. A separate **color-coded 12-week social calendar** (meeting = blue, stories = yellow, feed = purple, reel = green, giveaway = pink).
5. **90-day marketing plan in three phases** — **Month 1 Foundation** (wk 1–4: "plant your flag," get on camera weekly, establish brand signature); **Month 2 Momentum** (wk 5–8: launch lead magnet, run paid ads, 5+ partner coffees/week); **Month 3 Scale** (wk 9–12: repurpose top reels into YouTube, make lead magnet permanent, build monthly rituals). Then rinse and repeat.

### Execution tactics
- **$5/day paid-ad test** to a lead-magnet landing page (e.g. relocation PDF targeting feeder-market zips), run 30 days before reviewing cost-per-click and scaling.
- **DM-keyword automation is one step, never two** — the bio's "DM me [keyword]" delivers the lead magnet AND captures the email in the same message, then feeds the CRM ([[Lead Generation]]).
- **Builder / partner pipeline**: coffee with one builder rep per week, put their brand on the closing gift, interview them on your podcast (one agent reported 3–4 closings/year from this). Veterans: VFW/American Legion posts, JAG attorneys, and military-base transition offices as pre-earned-trust referral partners.
- **Two ways to run it**: free — copy Gogo's exact prompt into your own Claude (lacks her 15 years, pulls generic internet info); paid **$24.99** — runs on her "second brain" (costs data out of her Claude, hence the charge).

## Notes & Caveats

- **AI search now reads social posts** (Instagram, TikTok), not just websites — so niche-keyword discipline on socials drives AI recommendations of you.
- The generated report has recommended third-party schedulers like **Buffer and Hootsuite**; on the June 24 call Gogo flagged that she personally does not use or know those tools. (Reports surface tools present in the underlying knowledge; treat tool name-drops as suggestions, not Gogo's personal endorsement.)
- Niches change as you change — **pivot, don't restart** your social account; your followers move with you.
- **Run it twice — once for production, once for agent attraction.** Answering the questionnaire from the agent-attraction angle (who's your *ideal agent* to partner with, their pain/"pebble in the shoe," the language they speak) makes the report output an attract-*agents* plan: recruit-focused IG bio, blog posts, SEO keywords, and a 3-month day-by-day plan. ## Circle Coaching — One Niche at a Time

*Sources: [[The Circle Coaching — Listings-First, the Open-House Two-Buyer System and the Value Ladder (de-identified)]], [[The Circle Coaching — Niche Ownership, AI Execution Layer and Full-Service Delegation (de-identified)]]. Private [[The Circle|Circle]] coaching, de-identified — Gogo's generalizable teachings only.*

**Run the Niche Finder for ONE segment at a time — never blend.** Don't merge listings vs. buyers vs. upsizers into a single report. Answer the 10 questions for one segment, generate, then **re-run** for the next. Blended inputs produce a muddy niche and generic ideas.

**Specificity is what makes the output usable.** Specific answers — price point, seller age band, motivation — produce a sharper niche plus the **8 marketing ideas** and the **90-day posting schedule** a VA can load straight into a scheduler. Vague inputs can't be delegated.

**Team leaders still need a defined listing-side niche.** Even if buyer agents catch "all buyers," the leader's defined listing niche feeds the whole team — it's the engine, not an optional refinement.

**Solve the avatar's objection *inside* the offer.** If your ideal client is "too busy to learn," build the offer around exactly that (async/recorded, late-night live, "watch anytime") and **say so directly**. The niche isn't just who they are — it's answering their specific objection in how the offer is structured.

*(Companion principle — [[Personal Branding#Circle Coaching — Niche Ownership & Verified Delegation|own the niche you're already living]] rather than inventing a new one.)*
