## The Fiat Currency Thesis

There are 180 fiat currencies recognized in the world. The US dollar has lost 99.8% of its purchasing power since it was created. A house bought for $20,000 is now worth $2 million -- but that is not because the house improved. The house is 50+ years old and needs renovation. The price went up because the dollar went down. Real estate appreciation is dollar depreciation in disguise.

When confidence in fiat collapses (Zimbabwe, Venezuela, Egypt, Turkey), citizens wake up to 30%+ overnight purchasing power losses. Zimbabwe printed trillion-dollar bills. In Bali, something that cost $16 million in local currency cost $75 USD.

The US dollar is the world reserve currency and the most stable fiat -- but it still loses purchasing power every year through money printing. The government will always print money. It cannot stop. Therefore the dollar will always go down. Therefore Bitcoin will always go up in dollar terms.

## What Bitcoin Is

Bitcoin is simultaneously an asset, a network, a protocol, and an ideology.

**The apex commodity:** Bitcoin is the only finite commodity in existence. Total supply: 21 million coins. That number will never change. No government, company, or person can ever print more.

Gold is Bitcoin's closest competitor, but Bitcoin is superior:
- Gold cannot be digitally audited (you can't verify a gold coin is real by looking at it)
- Gold cannot be digitally transferred (try paying rent with a gold coin)
- Gold requires expensive physical custody (Fort Knox costs a fortune to guard)
- Gold is difficult to fractionalize for everyday commerce

Bitcoin solves all four: auditable on the blockchain in seconds, transferable anywhere in minutes, self-custodied with complete security, fractionalized to $0.001 increments.

## FASB Accounting Rule Change (2025)

FASB changed accounting rules in December 2024, effective for 2025: publicly traded companies can now use fair value accounting for Bitcoin on their balance sheets.

Previously, companies could only report Bitcoin going down (impairment losses). They could not show Bitcoin going up. This created zero incentive to hold Bitcoin on a corporate balance sheet.

Starting 2025: if Bitcoin goes up, companies can show it going up in their quarterly reports. Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, Tesla, and others will start putting Bitcoin on their balance sheets. This creates enormous new demand.

## Why Bitcoin Will Keep Going Up

**Mechanism 1 -- money printing is infinite:** Every government will always print money. More dollars chasing the same 21 million coins means the dollar price goes up forever.

**Mechanism 2 -- capital reallocation:** Approximately $330 trillion in global wealth across bonds, stocks, cash, real estate, gold, and other assets. Mark's calculation: if just 1% of bonds (roughly $300+ trillion in bonds) flows into Bitcoin, that alone takes Bitcoin from $58,000 to $1.2 million. At 2%: $2.4 million. At 5%: $7 million.

**Mechanism 3 -- rich people never sell scarce assets:** BlackRock does not sell their real estate. They hold, borrow against it, buy more. Rich people do the same with Bitcoin. The supply available on the market shrinks as large holders accumulate.

## The Halving Cycle

Every 4 years, the new supply of Bitcoin created per block is cut in half. Less new supply plus growing demand historically produces massive price increases 12-18 months after each halving.

**Critical pattern:** All of Bitcoin's gains in any 4-year cycle happen in approximately 10 days. If you sell before those 10 days, you miss the entire rally. The pattern: Bitcoin goes up, you get scared it will pull back, you sell, it immediately continues higher and you are locked out.

The only rational strategy: hold long-term, never sell.

## Why the Blockchain Cannot Be Hacked

The Bitcoin blockchain has never been hacked in 15+ years. China tried to ban it and failed. Russia cannot hack it. The US government cannot hack it.

Bitcoin now requires more computing energy to attack than the total computing power of every government, company, and intelligence agency combined.

The blockchain is the record. Your device (Coinbase, hardware wallet) only holds the keys to access what is on the blockchain. Common security failure: credentials stored as a screenshot in phone notes. A hacker who gets into the phone does not hack Coinbase -- they just find the credentials and log in. This is user error, not a Bitcoin failure.

## How to Buy Bitcoin

**Entry level -- Bitcoin ETF:**
Buy IBIT (BlackRock's Bitcoin ETF) through any brokerage (Schwab, E*Trade, Fidelity). Good for retirement accounts. No wallet management required. Mark would tell his grandmother to do this.

**Intermediate -- Coinbase:**
1. Download the Coinbase app
2. Complete KYC (upload driver's license)
3. Link bank account or Apple Pay
4. Buy any dollar amount
5. Set up recurring purchase (daily, weekly, or monthly)
6. Enable two-factor authentication (text message code required before any transfer)
7. Never store username/password in a phone screenshot

**Advanced -- self-custody:**
Move Bitcoin off Coinbase onto a hardware wallet (Trezor, ColdCard, or Ledger). You hold the private keys, not Coinbase. For large holdings: multisig setup requires multiple key holders simultaneously. Even at gunpoint, a single key holder cannot access the funds without the other key holders present and willing on camera.

The hardware wallet does not hold Bitcoin -- Bitcoin is always on the blockchain. The wallet holds the keys to access your Bitcoin. If you smash your wallet, buy a new one, enter your seed phrase, and your Bitcoin is instantly accessible again.

## Bitcoin vs. Real Estate (For Non-Wealthy Investors)

Real estate problems for non-wealthy investors:
- Cannot diversify (one property in one market = concentrated risk)
- Not competing on a level field with experienced investors who have off-market deals, pocket listings, and bank relationships
- Today's interest rates make most real estate deals unprofitable for small investors
- Buying one duplex and hoping it works out is essentially gambling with concentrated risk

Bitcoin advantages for everyone:
- Invest from $1 to $1 billion with zero barriers
- No landlord duties, no maintenance, no vacancies
- 24/7 liquidity -- sell any time, any day, any amount
- Perfectly fractionalized -- own exactly what you can afford
- Decentralized -- no single government can confiscate it (unlike gold, which governments have confiscated historically)
- Mathematical scarcity enforced by code, not by trust

## Bitcoin vs. Other Cryptocurrencies

Bitcoin is a store of value -- like digital real estate. You do not sell your Apple stock to buy coffee. You do not sell your real estate to pay rent.

Other cryptocurrencies (Ethereum, Solana, etc.) are venture capital bets. Some will succeed but most will go to zero. Mark does not recommend them for the average investor.

Bitcoin is unique: it is the only one with true fixed supply, no issuer, complete decentralization, and 15+ years of proven network security. No other cryptocurrency has all four.
